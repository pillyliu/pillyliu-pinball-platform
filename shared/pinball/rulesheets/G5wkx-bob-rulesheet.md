<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Big Flipper EM - Chicago Coin | 1970</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/2773b68c-4ad9-4d2a-be4a-ca83f6fd50b1-medium.jpg" alt="Big Flipper backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 2</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">UTAD.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Bumper exits can go down the middle or hit off the top outer corners of the return lanes and into the outlanes. Rebounds from the inevitable missed shots with the unwieldy oversized flippers can side drain or center drain.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Bigger is not always better. In this case, bigger is about as bad as it gets. The oversized flippers make shooting far less accurate and narrow in range than normal.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Score-wise, it’s all about building up the bonus score with the A-B-C top lanes and the two side stand-up targets, and also getting the ball to drop in the center saucer to collect it. Bonus value collected at the saucer stays at the current value. When the bonus is at 500 and you get another bonus advance, you score 100 for the lane or target plus 1000 points for rolling over the bonus value, which then resets to zero. Bonus saucer value is preserved from ball to ball AND from game to game, making it a bad tournament choice - - if you start a game with the 500 lit, your first top lane advance scores 1100 total, while if you start with it at zero, you just score 100.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Drain down either side to collect the current bonus score; center drains score nothing.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Clearly marked rollover buttons on the lower playfield control the ball saver post between the flippers. Use the center post when it’s up. Most shots on the flipper are fairly late, i.e. close to the tip. Probably the lamest game I’ve ever played.</p></div></section></div>
