<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">King Kool EM - Gottlieb | 1972</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/db2e9ab2-c919-485e-8cfb-b0f84171af59-medium.jpg" alt="King Kool backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 2</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">get the K-I-N-G letters to increase your bonus multiplier one step, then UTAD through the spinners.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">When the center bumper hits the ball through a spinner towards an outlane, when a side bumper hits a ball through a spinner towards a center drain, and balls falling from outside the spinners towards the center drain. Missed shots are common with these flippers.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This is the 2-player version of King Rock.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This is a tricky game for ball control, due to the double in-line flippers. Only a handful of machines ever used this flipper design.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Skill Shot is one of the four KING letters. King letters all score 1000 and add 1000 to bonus.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The spinners advance bonus, but only slowly [once per ten spins].</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Try to complete the KING letters; doing so raises the bonus multiplier to 2X on balls 1-4 and from the automatic 2X on ball 5 to 3X.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">You can’t cradle the ball on the lower of either flipper pair, and there’s a high risk of scissoring [losing the ball between the flippers due to improper flip timing]\! Upper flipper cradling does work but be careful when attempting to get the ball to settle there: you risk draining up and over the post between the upper flippers and the outlanes\!</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Shoot the center bullseye stand-up target ONLY when it’s lit to “open gate.”</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Most of the time, it’s shoot the lower King letters or UTAD. UTAD is not as productive as on some other games since the spinners only build bonus slowly and shots through them often don’t end up netting any King letters before they come back down. The angle of the flippers and spinners is such that cross-side spinner shots with the lower flippers often go spinner-10 bumper- back down right away; some will rattle around, but not a high percentage. If forced to use a lower flipper, try to go between the opposite side spinner and the opposite side lane, not through either spinner.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Your best bet is to shoot through the same-side spinner, but you can only do that from the upper flippers unless you make a backhand shot on the fly. The angle from the upper flippers through the same-side spinners is pretty good for getting some action up top, since it tends to go between the bumpers. You might even go up the I or N lanes.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Any time you can nudge a ball into either side 1000-point lane, do so. Don’t try shooting these from below, though.</p></div></section></div>
