<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Big Ben EM - Williams | 1975</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/057e2e71-4fe1-4422-90b9-c92d5eb14f87-medium.jpg" alt="Big Ben backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 1</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Up top until your base bonus is at maximum, then shoot the B-E-N oversized drop targets to double it. At that point, resume UTAD from the right flipper and choose to either shoot the right hand saucer for 5,000 from the left flipper or go UTAD with it as well.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Mild bias Right</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Up top is safe, at least until the ball falls into bumper randomland. Drop target rebounds can drain. The kickout from a successful side saucer shot is safe, but misses can be deadly. Balls coming down from the top near the right bumper seem to drain more than ones on the left side because the post above the lower left “B” target partially blocks the left outlane from that angle.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This game was used for qualifying at the New York State Pinball Championship in 1975, where first prize was a brand new Bally Wizard. Big Ben doesn’t have much to offer in terms of strategy, but I include it for historical purposes.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The only decent points on the game are your bonus, the right saucer when lit and the three 1000-point rollovers on the left. Going up top right away with both flippers will let you advance your bonus; each B-I-G lane gives one advance. Completing the three of them lights the saucer on the right for 5,000. Your first order of business is to just keep pounding the top shot until both of these are accomplished.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">At that point, you can double your bonus by completing the three B-E-N drop targets. These involve more risk of draining on the rebound, though, so if you notice them doing so, ignore them and continue UTAD.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Once the right hand saucer is lit for 5k, that’s your highest-value shot, so take it when you can if you’re accurate enough and the kickout from the saucer is safe. Watch out if the special is turned on but does not award points: completing B-I-G a second time changes the saucer value to special, making it totally worthless in that case.</p></div></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Skill Shot:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">get a letter in B-I-G at the top that you lack. Note that there’s an open gap left of the top lanes, so a too-casual plunge won’t get any letter.</p></section></div>
