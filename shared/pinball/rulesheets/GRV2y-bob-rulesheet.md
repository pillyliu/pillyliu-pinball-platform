<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Big Star EM - Williams | 1972</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/4c045a48-0e83-4ec6-a9fc-538cdff8a580-medium.jpg" alt="Big Star backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 1</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">shoot the red rollover buttons to advance the value of the center saucer, hit the drop target in front of it to expose it, shoot the saucer.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Very High</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This is the add-a-ball version of Super Star, with different point values.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Not a game I&#x27;m thrilled about.  It&#x27;s got multiple ball protect features that are moderately useful, but not much to shoot at.  Let&#x27;s start with the protectors:  the upper left yellow standup raises the rubber post at the left outlane, the upper right target does it at the right outlane.  Both posts can have the ball creep past them, but they will save at least half the balls heading their way.  The posts are lowered if you hit the middle standup target on the same side of the machine, or if you make the center saucer shot.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The two lower outside slingshots raise the center post, but the button right above that post lowers it, so again it&#x27;s only partial protection - - a ball heading SDTM can go over that button, lower the post, and drain.  On the other hand, some balls heading towards a center drain will deflect off the edge of the down post button enough for you to save them with a flipper.  You can have some long game times if you keep the three posts up and the tilt is liberal.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">As for points, see the quickie version:  shoot the red rollovers to raise the saucer value, hit the drop target in front of it, shoot the saucer.  A well-aimed shot will score all five rollovers on a side in sequence; each advance through number five moves the value of the center saucer up by 10000 points.  Bumpers, standup targets and slingshots, all are worth diddly squat [10 or 100] compared to the 10000 to 40000 for the center saucer.  The &quot;advance&quot; standup targets to the sides of the center saucer each advance you a couple of 1-2-3-4-5 numbers towards raising the saucer value (how many is setting-adjustable).</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Extra balls and specials may be set to award 100,000 points instead.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The top three SU-P-ER lane at least give you 5000 when you get all three of them.  Yawn.</p></div></section></div>
