<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Card Trix EM - Gottlieb | 1970</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/05c6c326-8ce1-4ca7-a33c-cf2b20cf2c0e-medium.jpg" alt="Card Trix backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 1</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Ace all day from the left flipper, UTAD from the right.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Left</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Very High; get the ball into upper portion of playfield if you can&#x27;t get the Ace</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This is the add-a-ball version of Flip-A-Card.  The playfield and strategy are the same, but the values are all 10 times higher than on Flip-A-Card.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Full version:  Ace all day.  Really.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The Skill Shot is to get one of the two Kings for 3000.  Best choice is to soft plunge for the king on the far right, which may also feed the Ace below it.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">After that, it&#x27;s all about the Ace, worth 5000 points, more than anything else in the game.  Completing the cards only gets you a worthless special.  Since you&#x27;re after points, ignore which cards you have.  UTAD from the right flipper to try to get the J or Q for 1000 or, ideally, a King for 3000.  Shoot the Ace from below with the left flipper.  And if the right flipper is strong enough, when cradled, try to flip the ball into the right side of the 1000 bumper to bank it into the Ace lane from above.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Key feed:  the exit from the bottom of the Ace lane.  If it drains down the middle too often, switch your strategy to UTAD with both flippers.</p></div></section></div>
