<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Panthera SS - Gottlieb | 1980</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/8221c759-6fb2-46eb-a06e-e31bb6afec83-medium.jpg" alt="Panthera backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Up top until you have all four colors lit, then drop targets all day. Once the saucer on the left is lit to collect bonus, do so with a multiplier of 3X or more.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Most drains are multi-step with rebounds off the drop targets or balls coming down from the top going into extensive slingshot action. Either bumper can smack the ball down the middle. Balls exiting the top right through the 1/2/3 lanes or the longer lane at the far right can hit the rubber between the star rollover and the extra ball stand-up target and drain down the center. Balls kicked out of the saucer below the far left lane will be hurled at the right slingshot out of control.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Panthera is a bit better score-balanced than some of its peers. It’s also got a dozen drop targets for fans of these which are worth enough to play for.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Bonus is advanced by lit drop targets, the top right star rollovers, the right side bullseye target and lit 1-2-3 lanes. Base bonus goes up to 29K. Advancing your bonus multiplier is a two-step process. First, you need to score top lanes to light the drop targets of matching colors – white, green, yellow and blue. You then need to knock down all three lit targets of the same color to advance you one multiplier, up to 5X. At the end of a ball, any drop targets you knocked down that were lit stay down, ones you hit that were unlit pop back up. Base bonus resets but colored lanes and your multiplier are held.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">You want to hit drop targets when lit not just to work on your bonus X but because they’re worth 5K lit, just 500 unlit.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The top lanes also light the corresponding color return lanes and outlanes for 5K rather than 500. The top lanes are only worth 5K the first time you score them; likewise, the 1-2-3 lanes are worth 10K only the first time.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The saucer below the left \#2 lane scores 10K and 3 advances, but the ball doesn’t always settle into it when exiting that \#2 lane. Once you have at least 20K in base bonus, this saucer lights to collect bonus. When collected, base bonus resets but multiplier is preserved.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Basic strategy is to go up top via the left spinner and right rollover star lane to get all the colors lit. Once you’ve done that to make your targets worth 5K and set up getting your multipliers, shoot the drop targets. Focus on completing a color set at a time rather than one or two of each. Since your bonus multiplier is held, it’s important to get it up as high as possible on ball one.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">As soon as you have your bonus at 20K with a multiplier of 3X or better, try to get the ball in the left collect bonus saucer. There are at least three ways to do this: shoot at it directly, which may risk a left side drain; nudge the ball into the top of the left \#2 chute, although the ball will sometimes skip through; and have the right slingshot kick the ball into it, perhaps with a nudge assist on your part.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Yes, that sound you recognize is the same as on Counterforce - - thrum, thrum, thrum, thrum, and the wiggly-screech at the end.</p></div></section></div>
