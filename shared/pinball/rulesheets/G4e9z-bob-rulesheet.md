<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Flipper Fair EM - Gottlieb | 1961</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/32ff7c7e-e3bf-425d-b3d4-b0a7a6b9a382-medium.jpg" alt="Flipper Fair backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 1</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">When the center stand-up target has matching green and yellow lights, shoot it, otherwise UTAD.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Mostly bad feeds out of the bumpers down the middle or into the slings. The side lanes can also put the ball into the slings leading to a side drain. Do your best to avoid hitting a bumper or the bottom of the lane dividers next to them face-on.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Pretty basic. Color-and-number coded top lanes light the corresponding colored bumpers and upper side lanes beneath when made. The more lit, the better, but you can only get one on the plunge.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The primary point-maker here, other than just bumper-love and getting lucky with an up-one-top-lane-down-another bounce off the bumpers, is the center target. The center target is worth 100 points, except when the green and yellow lights below it are on the same row; it then awards an add-a-ball. Each green or yellow bumper you hit raises the light below the center target for that color one notch. Getting them to match and then hitting the target is the only partly skill-based play on the game. If you’re looking for a “coin-flip” game against a superior opponent, this is a good one to pick.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The other way to add a ball is by completing the 1-2-3-4 lanes.</p></div></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Skill Shot:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">get any top lane you don’t already have.</p></section></div>
