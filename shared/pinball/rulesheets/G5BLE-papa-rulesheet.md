<span class="bodyTitle"> Playfield Description </span><BR>
[left to right]
<p>
Where possible, the game's description of the shot is used.  If you have
played Theatre of Magic, you can safely skip this section, and not miss much.
<p>
<dl>
<dt>Left Inlane/Outlane <dd> standard;
Sometimes a magnet above the inlane automatically can keep
the ball out of the outlane. The magnet works by a proximity sensor
which senses magnetical objects.
<p>
<dt>Hocus Pocus Targets <dd> Two standups a short distance above the left inlane.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Left Lane <dd> Roughly in the same position as World Cup's Travel Lane.  Normally,
balls through the lane pass around the back, past the top rollovers, and
up a short ramp onto a habitrail back to the right flipper, so that the
shot can be repeated [the setup is similar to Getaway's lock ramp].
Weaker shots that don't make the ramp fall to the Bonus X Rollovers.
Also, a post diverter [like Tommy] can stop the ball on its way to the
bumpers, pushing it into the Vanish Lock.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Vanish Lock <dd> About 2/3 of the way around the Left Lane, the Vanish Lock can
hold one ball.  The ball locks on a shot through the Left Lane, and the
kickout pushes the ball out the Left Lane entrance.  The lock releases at
the start of any multiball, or at the end of the game.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Tiger Saw Captive Ball <dd> Just to the right of the Left Lane, the Captive Ball
is actually two.  A first ball is stuck [nailed?] to the playfield, and
most of the ball can be hit [unlike traditional captive balls].  Hitting
the first ball propels a second, moving ball, and pushing that ball to the
top of a short lane scores the shot.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Left Inner Loop <dd> This short loop goes around the back of the Trunk, and out the
Right Inner Loop entrance [through the spinner].  A post diverter behind
the Trunk can push the ball into the Rear Trunk Entrance, or a weak shot
up the Left Loop may fall into it.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Magic Trunk <dd> The biggest "gimmick" on the game is left of center, about halfway
up the playfield.  It rotates to four faces, and has a Hall-effect sensor
in front [like Road Show's bulldozer] to judge hits.
<p>
<ul>
<li> Face 1: nothing special, just a trunk.
<li> Face 2: like Face 1, with a hole in the center large enough for a shot.
<li> Face 3: like Face 1, with a green light near the top.
<li> Face 4: A bulls-eye target, with a magnet in the center that takes the
ball off the playfield when activated.
</ul>
<p>
All four faces are used often; later rules describe when.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Rear Trunk Entrance <dd> behind the Trunk, this opening leads to the Trapdoor
Kickout.  The Rear Trunk Entrance also serves as the lock for Theatre
Multiball.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Center Staircase Ramp <dd> halfway up the playfield, this ramp feeds the Left
Inlane.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Trap Door Targets <dd> Two targets to the left and right of the Center Ramp.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Right Inner Loop <dd> This loop goes directly through the Spinner, past the Rear
Trunk Entrance, and out the Left Inner loop entrance.  Like the Left Inner
Loop, the ball can be diverted to the Rear Trunk Entrance, or may fall
into it.  Beware that a full Loop shot may send the ball straight down
the middle, and plan accordingly.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Spinner <dd> within the Right Inner Loop.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Right Staircase Ramp <dd> This longer ramp travels around the back of the machine,
all the way to the Right Inlane.  A shot that doesn't make it up the ramp
falls behind the Spinner and comes out the Right Inner Loop entrance.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Spirit Ring <dd> associated with the Right Ramp, the Spirit Ring pulls the ball off
the Right Ramp habitrail, holds it briefly, then drops it on the Center
Ramp habitrail [to the Left Inlane].  The Ring itself is on the left side,
above the Tiger Saw Captive Ball.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Trap Door <dd> a Slot Machine-like hole comes out from under the playfield at
certain times, blocking the Right Ramp.  This also serves as the kickout
from the Magic Trunk.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Right Orbit <dd> Normally, this orbit travels all the way around the back of the
machine, coming out the Left Lane entrance.  A diverter can stop the ball
along the orbit, keeping it above the Bonus X Rollovers.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Poof Target <dd> Single standup above the Right Inlane.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Right Inlane/Outlane <dd> Identical to Left Inlane/Outlane [reversed, obviously].</dd>
<p>
<dt>Bonus X Rollovers <dd> Two rollovers at the top.  Lane-Change is active with either
flipper.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Mirror <dd> behind the Bonus X Rollovers, the Mirror allows you to see the rollover
area without craning your neck.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Pop Bumpers <dd> Three, in standard pattern, below Rollovers.  The only bumper exit
is out the entrance to the Right Orbit.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Flippers/Slingshots <dd> Standard two at the bottom.  No upper flippers or non-
standard kickers or slingshots.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Manual Plunger <dd> Woo hoo!  The plunger drops the ball on the diverter from the
Left Lane's ramp, leading to the Right Inlane.</dd>
</dl>
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle"> Basic Rules </span><BR>
[using names and descriptions above, again left to right]
<p>
<dl>
<dt>Left Inlane/Outlane <dd> The outlane lights for Special by random award or by
beginning Grand Finale.  Nothing special on the inlane.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Hocus Pocus Targets <dd> When either is hit, outlane magnets light for
15 seconds (default).  No effect during any multiball.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Left Lane <dd> Collects letter in THEATRE anytime in single-ball play.  Normally,
the diverters allow the ball onto the small ramp; if Vanish is lit, the
ball will divert to the Vanish Lock, and during Strait Jacket Illusion,
the ball will divert to the Bonus X Rollovers.  Also collects Extra Ball
when lit.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Vanish Lock <dd> The Lock releases at the start of any multiball.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Tiger Saw Captive Ball <dd> Advances count to light Vanish Lock [starts at 3, then
6, then 6 and so on]; if Vanish Lock or Tiger Saw Multiball is lit, does nothing.
When the third Vanish Lock is lit the Extra Ball will also be lit.
Also a Skill Shot [see
individual section], the scoring shot in Tiger Saw Illusion, and the
Jackpot in Tiger Saw Multiball.  Unlit ball hit scores 2M.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Left Inner Loop <dd> Lights for Theatre Multiball lock and Grand Finale.  Also one
of the scoring shots in Safe Escape Illusion, and usually a scoring shot
in Spirit Cards Illusion [if Lock or Grand Finale is not lit].</dd>
<p>
<dt>Magic Trunk <dd> Since there are so many rules involving the Trunk, they don't
really fit well in this section.  The few "basic" rules to give here are
the Trunk's activity when nothing major is going on: shoot the Trunk three
times to light an Illusion [the Trunk rotates], then shoot the Trunk's
hole to start the Illusion.  The Magic Trunk is a scoring shot in every
Illusion, but is the only scoring shot in Hat Magic and Trunk Escape
Illusions.  The Trunk is also a Skill Shot [see individual section].</dd>
<p>
<dt>Rear Trunk Entrance <dd> scores a Lock whenever hit in single-ball play,
regardless of whether the lock was lit or not.   [See Theatre Multiball
section.]  Lock scores 20 million.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Center Staircase Ramp <dd> Lights to spell MAGIC for the first two multiballs.
Also can be lit to Raise Trap Door, and is the scoring shot in Levitate
Woman Illusion.  Unlit ramp scores 500K.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Trap Door Targets <dd> Hit these targets four times to light Raise Trap Door at
the Center Ramp.  If the Trap Door shot is not collected, one or more
target hits may be necessary to light Raise Trap Door again.  The number
of target hits registered resets at the start of each ball.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Right Inner Loop <dd> Lights for Theatre Multiball lock.  Also one of the scoring
shots in Safe Escape Illusion, and a scoring shot in Spirit Cards
Illusion.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Spinner <dd> Scores 100K/spin.  Scoring shot in Spirit Cards Illusion; each spin is
then worth at least 500K.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Right Staircase Ramp <dd> Lights to spell MAGIC.  If Spirit Ring is not lit, the
Right Ramp lights it until collected.  Also the scoring shot in
Metamorphosis Illusion.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Spirit Ring <dd> If Spirit Ring is lit on the Right Ramp, the Ring activates,
pulling the ball off the Right Ramp habitrail and onto the Center Ramp
habitrail.  The first Spirit Ring shot scores 10M, then lights Spirit
Ring for 20M for a brief period.  If the 20M is collected, it is lit again
for 30M for a brief period.  If the 30M is collected, Spirit Ring is not
lit again.  The Spirit Ring will also activate [but not necessarily score]
during Metamorphosis Illusion.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Trap Door <dd> Shooting lit Raise Trap Door on the Center Ramp raises the Trap Door
for a very brief period.  Entering the Trap Door scores 15M and earns a
trip to the Basement [see individual section] for a random award.  The
Trap Door serves as kickout from the Trunk, and when all balls have been
locked; pay attention, as there is no ball saver.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Right Orbit <dd> advances Clock [see section] by one hour in single-ball play and during Tiger Saw Multiball.
Begins Tiger Saw Multiball when lit.  Normal diverter action sends the
ball around out the Left Lane entrance; in Strait Jacket Illusion, the
ball diverts to the Bonus X Rollovers.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Poof Target <dd>
Scores 100K, that's it. On prototype machines, hitting the Poof Target
would raise a drain shield between the flippers but this feature was
removed when the game went into production.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Right Inlane/Outlane <dd> Identical to Left Inlane/Outlane [reversed, obviously].</dd>
<p>
<dt>Bonus X Rollovers <dd> completing both rollovers advances Bonus X, to a maximum of 8x.
After that, completion will score 5M. The Bonus X value resets after each
ball.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Pop Bumpers <dd> add 101,320 to THEATRE value, and advance the lit Illusion.  Also
the scoring method in Strait Jacket Illusion.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Flippers/Slingshots <dd> Flippers are controlled by buttons at side of cabinet.
Slingshots act of their own accord, score some random number equal to
epsilon times the replay level, and advance the lit Illusion.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Manual Plunger <dd> plunge, then go for a Skill Shot.  Or not.  It's your life.
It's your choice.</dd>
</dl>
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle"> Skill Shot </span><P>
Whenever a ball is plunged, the player has an opportunity for a Skill
Shot.  If it is the first plunge of the ball, the Skill Shot will be located
at the Trunk, for one of three awards.  Plunge when the award you want is on
the dot display.  The three awards are selected among four possibilities:
<p>
<ul>
<li>  10 Million OR 20 Million
<li>  2x Bonus [Multiplier]
<li>  Advance Clock [2 hours]
<li>  Begin Illusion OR 2x Combo
</ul>
<p>
Begin Illusion is always a choice if Illusion is not already lit;
otherwise, 2x Combo is always a choice.  Both are never lit at the same time.
<p>
2x Combo doubles all Combo scores for the ball [see individual section].
<p>
If Illusion is lit, you must make the Trunk's hole to get credit for the
Skill Shot.
<p>
If it is not the first plunge of the ball, the Skill Shot will be located
at the Captive Ball, and is always Advance Clock [1 hour].
<p>
Skill Shots count toward End-of-Ball Bonus [see individual section].
"Amazing skill!"
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle"> Illusions </span><P>
There are eight illusions, listed in a book in front of the flippers.  The
lit Illusion is flashing; Illusions already complete are solidly lit.  The lit
Illusion is advanced by the slingshots and bumpers.
<p>
There are three ways to begin an Illusion: by Skill Shot, by Basement
award [see section], and by shooting the Trunk.  To begin the Illusion by
shooting the Trunk, you must first light the Illusion by shooting the Trunk
three times [lights in front of the trunk indicate the number of shots made].
The Trunk then rotates to reveal a hole, and shooting the hole begins the
Illusion.  If you manage to hit the Trunk a fourth time while it is still
rotating into position, the game will give you the benefit of the doubt and
start the Illusion immediately.
<p>
Generally during Illusions, you are encouraged to complete the task at
hand: "You must concentrate."  "You must hurry up!"
<p>
A list of the Illusions, and their scoring level:
<p>
<b> Illusion 1: Tiger Saw </b><br>
During this round, shoot the Captive Ball for 15 million, and animation of
a tiger... sawing.  The first hit shows the tiger sawing a woman in half,
and the second shows the tiger pulling a Fiorello LaGuardia, sawing a
pinball machine in half.  Presumably it is a Popeye.  The two animations
alternate for later hits.
<p>
<b> Illusion 2: Levitate Woman </b><br>
Shoot the Center Ramp for an increasing award: 11 million, 12 million, no
limit.  Each shot shows an animation of a levitating woman, who winks at
you, and the female voice says "Levitate!"
<p>
<b> Illusion 3: Trunk Escape </b><br>
The trunk has been thrown underwater!  As the pressure climbs, shoot the
standard side of the Trunk four times to break the chains.  Each shot is
17 million; completing the round gives a good animation.  "You must break
free!"  "The pressure is building!"
<p>
<b> Illusion 4: Spirit Cards </b><br>
Shoot the spinner to collect points, and watch the cards fly between
hands.  The first spinner shots gives 800K/spin, then 1.1M/spin, 1.4M/spin
and so on without limit.
If Lock or Grand Finale is lit on the Left
Inner Loop, you must shoot the Right Inner Loop to collect.
<p>
<b> Illusion 5: Safe Escape </b><br>
The assistant is locked in a safe, held by a rope.  Shoot either Inner
Loop to get part of the safe's combination [the combination is 11-04-69,
probably someone's birthday].  The first two shots score 20M
each and the final third scores 30M. When the round is completed the
display says "Total: 60M" for some strange reason. Again, a very
good-looking animation is yours for completing the round. "The rope is
breaking!" "She's in danger!" "You are amazing." [completion]
<p>
<b> Illusion 6: Metamorphosis </b><br>
The assistant is holding up a cape.  "I am only an illusion."  Shoot the
Right Ramp to score; the Spirit Ring will activate, giving you a chance to
loop the ramp.  The first shot is 12 million, then 16, then 20...  The
first shot shows the assistant transforming into a dove.  The second shows
her transform into a tiger.  The third... well...
<p>
<b> Illusion 7: Strait Jacket </b><br>
The assistant is in a strait jacket, and spikes are closing in.  Shooting
the bumpers pushes her hand closer to the freeing buckle.  Either the Left
Loop [if Vanish is not lit] or the Right Orbit will deposit the ball in
the bumpers.  6 million is scored for each bumper, 60 million [10 bumpers]
completes the round.  "I knew you could do it!"
<P>
This round is also useful if you want to build up your Bonus X.
<p>
<b> Illusion 8: Hat Magic </b><br>
The trunk remains on the side with the hole; shoot the hole for 15
million and some magic.  With the first shot, a rabbit comes out of the
hat; doves come out the second time; the third time, a telephone.  A
telephone?  [Try the Easter Egg section if you must...]  Later shots just
give the rabbit again.
<p>
Each Illusion has around a 20-second time limit.  Timers freeze while the
ball is in the pop bumpers or underneath the playfield.
<p>
In addition to the points particular to each Illusion, extra points are
awarded for Trunk hits during rounds which do not involve the Trunk [all but
Trunk Escape and Hat Magic].  The Trunk scores an increasing amount for each
hit: the first hit is 2 million, then 3 million, 4, etc.  The value resets each
ball, but carries between Illusions.  In low-scoring Illusions [such as Spirit
Cards] it may be more worthwhile to shoot for the Trunk.
<p>
Beginning all eight Illusions awards one-fourth of the items necessary to
light the Grand Finale [see individual section].  Note that this award is given
as soon as the eighth Illusion is begun, and not as soon as it ends.
<p>
Illusions count toward End-of-Ball Bonus [see individual section].
"The next performance is about to begin!"
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle"> Vanish / Tiger Saw Multiball </span><P>
Vanish lights at the Left Lane after a set number of hits on the Tiger Saw
Captive Ball [hits during any Illusion count as well].  The default is to start
at three Captive Ball hits to light Vanish.  "Make the ball vanish!"
<p>
Vanish scores 40 million, then qualifies the Right Orbit to start Tiger
Saw Multiball.  Upon making the Orbit, the second ball is kicked down the Left
Lane, and the Jackpot is the Tiger Saw Captive Ball.  The first shot to the
Captive Ball is 30 million, then 40 million, then 50 million up to a limit of 70M.  "Tiger Saw
Jackpot!"  You do not need to relight the Jackpot,
and there is no ball saver on this multiball.
<p>
Upon completion of Tiger Saw Multiball, the count of Captive Ball hits
resets to zero, and the number of hits needed to light Vanish will be six instead of three.
<P>
Lighting the third Vanish will also lite Extra Ball.
<p>
If Theatre Multiball is started after Vanish has been collected, the ball
will be kicked out of the Vanish Lock for a four-ball multiball.  Upon
completion of Theatre Multiball, the Left Lane will immediately be lit for
Vanish and another 40 million.
<p>
Tiger Saw Multiball does not count toward lighting Grand Finale.
<p>
During multiball, other game functions are interrupted; you cannot advance
THEATRE, you cannot advance to Vanish Lock, you cannot advance Trunk Hits and
you cannot advance toward a Trap Door Award.  You can however
advance the Clock and build up your Bonus X.
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle"> Theatre Multiball </span><P>
To start Theatre Multiball, you must first qualify two locks.  Lock is lit
by completing MAGIC on the ramps.  For the first two multiballs, either ramp
will spot a letter in MAGIC; for the third, only the Right Ramp will spot a
letter.  Progress toward MAGIC is displayed in lights in the center of the
playfield.
<p>
When MAGIC is complete, Lock becomes lit on either Inner Loop shot.  The
first Lock is 20 million, and a new ball is served to the plunger or from the
Trap Door Kickout [if three balls are already in the lock]; if the ball is
served to the plunger, an opportunity for a Skill Shot is given.  "The first
ball is locked!"  The second Lock is immediately lit at the Inner Loop.
<p>
There is another method for achieving the first Lock; shooting the Rear
Trunk Entrance at any time will award a Secret Ball Lock, a great animation,
a free completion of MAGIC, and the first Lock.  "Oh NOOoooo!!"  After a Secret
Ball Lock, the second Lock is immediately lit at the Inner Loop.
The Secret Ball Lock is only valid for the
first two multiballs. After that, a Rear Trunk Entrance will only
score 10M.
<p>
The second Lock is 20 million, and spins the Trunk to the side resembling
a bulls-eye target.  Hit the Trunk, and the ball is stuck to its side by a
magnet.  The trunk spins to drop the ball into the Rear Trunk Entrance, and
multiball begins.  Before the balls are kicked out, the Trunk spins to the
side with the hole.
<p>
As balls are kicked out, a timer counts down immediately: 5... 4...
"Three"... "Two"... "One".  Shooting the Trunk hole during the countdown scores
an immediate 100 million points.  "ONE hundred MILLION!"  This counts as a
Theatre Jackpot, and the trunk spins to the side; a green light flashes at the
top of the Trunk.
<p>
Note: the countdown is aborted if any other major shot is hit in the
meantime.
<p>
If the 100 million shot is unsuccessful, Jackpot is lit at the Trunk, base
value of 50 million [always].  Shoot the Trunk to collect the Theatre Jackpot.
"Theatre JACKPOT... jackpot... jackpot..."  A nice echo effect on the Jackpot.
<p>
Several shots will raise the Jackpot by 5 million.  They are: Left Lane,
Left Inner Loop, Right Inner Loop, Right Ramp, Right Orbit.  These
shots raise the Jackpot value, whether the Theatre Jackpot is lit or not.
There is no apparent maximum to the Jackpot value; if there is one, it is at
least 115 million.
<p>
After a Theatre Jackpot is collected, the Trunk spins to the side, and a
green light flashes on the Trunk.  You must shoot the Trunk a number of times
to relight the Theatre Jackpot; this number increases for each Jackpot you
score.  To relight the Jackpot the first time, only two Trunk hits are
required; the second time, three; then four; then five.  I believe five is the
maximum.  When the required hits are complete, the Trunk spins back to reveal
the hole.
<p>
A shot that falls into the Rear Trunk Entrance will also score the Jackpot
when lit.  Nothing happens if the Jackpot isn't lit, though this would be a
nice Easter Egg to include in future versions [if there are any].
<p>
Entering Theatre Multiball qualifies one of the four items necessary to
light the Grand Finale [see individual section], but if Theatre Multiball is
the fourth item [unlikely], Grand Finale will not light until multiball is
over.
<p>
During multiball, other game functions are interrupted; you cannot advance
THEATRE, you cannot advance the Clock, you cannot advance to Vanish Lock, and
you cannot advance toward a Trap Door Award.  However,
the right Bonus X diverter is closed during multiball so a shot to the
left loop will always end up at the Bonus X lanes, helping you to
advance your Bonus multiplier.
<p>
Collected letters in MAGIC count toward End-of-Ball Bonus [see individual
section]; this includes only letters collected from ramp shots, and not any
letters added by a Secret Ball Lock.
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle"> THEATRE Hurry-Up </span><P>
The base value for the THEATRE Hurry-Up is 50 million; the value is
advanced with every bumper hit by about 100 thousand per bumper.  Piddly
points, basically.
<p>
Pick up a letter in THEATRE by shooting the Left Lane in single-ball play.
The letters in THEATRE are spelled out on the dot matrix after each shot, and
are also in playfield lights in the front center.  When THEATRE is complete,
the THEATRE Hurry-Up is lit at the Trunk, which spins to the side with the
hole.  You must shoot the hole within 10 seconds to collect, after which time
the Trunk spins back to whatever side it was on.  To collect the Hurry-Up, you
must shoot the Trunk hole before it spins away.  The Hurry-Up timer pauses
while the ball is in the bumpers.
<p>
Whether the Hurry-Up is collected or not, THEATRE resets.  If you collect
the Hurry-Up, the value resets to 50 million; otherwise it keeps its current
value.
<p>
Your spelled THEATRE letters are carried over ball-to-ball until you
collect the Hurry-Up. After that they will be reset at the end of each ball.
<P>
Spelling THEATRE qualifies one of the four items necessary to light the
Grand Finale [see individual section].  You do not need to collect the Hurry-
Up.
<p>
Collected THEATRE letters count toward End-of-Ball Bonus [see individual
section].
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle"> Clock / Midnight Madness </span><P>
Advancing the Clock can be done in three ways: by Skill Shot [either at
the Trunk (2 hours) or the Captive Ball (1 hour)], by Basement award [see section], or by shooting
the Right Orbit during single-ball play or Tiger Saw Multiball.  Note that the basement can award
both "Adv. Clock" [1 hour] and "Midnight" [immediate Midnight Madness].
<p>
Advancing the Clock yields a quote about the time, more frantic as the
hours go by.  "It's two o'clock."  "ELEVEN O'CLOCK!"
<p>
Advancing to 6:00 lights Extra Ball on the Left Lane for the first time
through to Midnight; successive go-throughs score 40 million at 6:00.
<p>
Advancing to Midnight [12:00, of course] begins Midnight Madness
immediately.  All mattresses are 50% off... no, wait, that's not it.
<p>
The Trunk spins to reveal the bulls-eye target, and the Clock starts
rewinding itself from 12:00.  Shots to the Trunk catch on the magnet, and score
twice the current hour on the Clock, or 10 million, whichever is larger.  Every
5-7 seconds, a loud "bong" is heard, and the Clock goes back one hour.
<p>
An immediate shot to the Trunk after the ball drops from the magnet may
not catch on the Trunk's magnet, as it takes a few seconds to get ready for
another shot.  If this happens, there is no score compensation; you only get
credit for shots that activate the magnet.  Still, there is enough time for
two shots per "hour" of time in Midnight Madness.
<p>
Getting to Midnight qualifies one of the four items necessary to light the
Grand Finale [see individual section].  You do not need to finish Midnight
Madness; if this is the fourth item, Grand Finale will light immediately on the
Left Inner Loop.
<p>
The current Clock time counts toward End-of-Ball Bonus [see individual
section].  Unlike MAGIC and THEATRE letters, there is no carryover between
completions.
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle"> Trap Door / Basement Awards </span><P>
Getting a Basement award is done in three stages:
<p>
<ul>
<li> Hit the Trap Door Targets four times to light Raise Trap Door on the
Center Ramp.
<li> Shoot the Center Ramp for Raise Trap Door; the Trap Door will block the
Right Ramp entrance.
<li> Shoot the Trap Door for 15 million and a Basement Award.  The animation
of walking down to the basement is very nice.
</ul>
<p>
There are always 10 Basement Awards to choose from; the screen resembles
Creature's Snackbar Menu and Road Show's Bunker Menu.  The selection is
apparently random, but surely has a lot to do with the game in progress and so
on.  The game rarely gives the same Basement Award twice in one game.
<p>
The selections:
<p>

<tr><td> MIDNIGHT       </td><td>PINBALL</td></TR>
<tr><td> LITE EX. BALL </td><td>TRUNK MAGIC</td></TR>
<tr><td> ADV. CLOCK         </td><td>LITE SPECIAL</td></TR>
<tr><td> ILLUSION          </td><td>BONUS X</td></TR>

<p>
Notes on the selections...
<p>
<ul>
<li> Random millions can be anywhere from 15M to 40M.
<p>
<li> Midnight advances the Clock to Midnight immediately; if 6:00 is passed,
the appropriate award [Light EB or 40M] is given automatically.
<p>
<li> Bonus X only awards if 8x is not the current multiplier.
<p>
<li> "Pinball" = Video Mode.  Shoot all six targets in 20 seconds for
Extra Ball [usually] or 40 million.  [Also see Easter Eggs section.]
<p>
<li> Light Special at both Outlanes, lit continuously for the rest of the ball.
<p>
<li> Illusion starts the currently lit Illusion immediately; this -can- be
awarded while an Illusion is already in progress, I believe.
<p>
<li> Trunk Magic spins the Trunk to reveal the hole; shoot it within about 5
seconds for 40 million.
</ul>
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle"> Combo Shots </span><P>
A Combo is awarded for any two shots in a row, made from the following
list: Left Lane, Left Inner Loop, Center Ramp, Right Inner Loop, Right Ramp,
Right Orbit.
<p>
The first Combo Shot is 2 million; successive [immediate] Combo Shots
increase by another 2 million.  So a 3-Way Combo would be 4 million, 4-Way is
6 million, and so on.  There does not appear to be a limit to this behavior.
<p>
If you are lucky enough to make the "2x Combo" Skill Shot, it doubles
every Combo value for the ball.  2-Way Combos become 4M, then 8M, 12M, etc.
<p>
Some of the Combo Shots are easily repeatable; the Left Lane and Center
Ramp are most obvious.  A decent way to build points is to continually loop the
Left Lane.
<p>
3-Way and higher Combos also generate encouraging quotes from the game:
<p>
<ul>
<li> 3-Way [4M]: "Amazing!"
<li> 4-Way [6M]: "Marvelous!"
<li> 5-Way [8M]: "Magnificent!"
</ul>
<p>
There are definitely more.  Often, some other event [such as completing
MAGIC or THEATRE] gets in the way of the Combo displays and quotes.
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle"> The Grand Finale </span><P>
To light the Grand Finale, you must complete four tasks.  Two are simple,
one is hard, and one is extremely difficult.  What you must do:
<p>
<ul>
<li> "Theatre": Complete THEATRE [to light THEATRE Hurry-Up].  Collecting the
Hurry-Up is not necessary.
<li> "Multiball": Play Theatre Multiball.
<li> "Midnight": Get to Midnight on the Clock.
<li> "Illusions": Play all eight Illusions.
</ul>
<p>
Completing THEATRE is a piece of cake.  Getting to multiball is pretty
easy.  Getting to Midnight is difficult, but can be done when you put it at a
high priority.  But those Illusions are hard to come by, and eight... feh.
<p>
Anyway, completing all four tasks will light "Start Finale" at the Left
Inner Loop anytime during single-ball play.  Shoot
either left or
right inner orbit to start a single-ball round where every ramp and
loop are worth 50M and will give you a letter in "MAGIC THEATRE". You
have 60 seconds to complete these twelve shots for a rewarding 500M.
Shooting the left loop will divert the ball to the Bonus X lanes, a
good way to build your bonus and spell "MAGIC THEATRE" at the same time.
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle">Extra Balls</span><P>
Extra ball can be lit or collected doing the following:
<UL>
<LI> Getting the "LITE EX. BALL" Basement award when shooting into the
Trap Door.
<LI> Shoot down all six targets in Digital Pinball, another Basement
award, will give you an EB.
<LI> Advance The Clock to six o\xb4clock will lite EB at the left loop.
<LI> Lite the third Vanish lock (15 captive ball hits in total) will
lite EB.
</UL>
<P>
<span class="bodyTitle">Tournament Mode</span><P>
If the Tournament Mode Setting is set to "yes" most of the game play
will remain the same, the only thing that differ is that the random
Basement Awards are turned off. A shot into the Trap Door will only
award 15M.
<P>
Holding left or right flipper button when starting a game (see "Easter
Eggs" section) will have no effect in Tournament Mode.
<P>
<span class="bodyTitle"> End-of-Ball Bonus </span><P>
Like Flintstones, end-of-ball bonus is a combination of many awards.
Here is a listing of the awards [in order, I hope].
<p>
<ul>
<li> Magicianship: points based on the number of Grand Finale tasks complete.
<ul>
<li> none: "Novice", 2M.
<li> one: "Apprentice", 4M.
<li> two: "Journey Man", 6M.
<li> three: "Master", 8M
<li> four: "Master", 10M
</ul>
<p>
<li> MAGIC letters: 1 million x number of letters collected in the game.  This
counts only those letters collected in ramp shots, not "free letters" from
the start of the game or a Secret Ball Lock.
<p>
<li> THEATRE letters: 500 thousand x number of letters collected in the game.
<p>
<li> Clock Bonus: 1 million x current time on the clock.  12 noon = 0 points.
<p>
<li> Illusions: 4 million x number of Illusions played in the game.
<p>
<li> Skill Shots: 1 million x number of Skill Shots [Trunk or Captive Ball]
made in the game.
</ul>
<p>
ALL this is multiplied by the current Bonus Multiplier, for the total End-
of-Ball Bonus.  Bonus can sure be a lot; I've had it over 300 million.
<p>
If any of the six categories of Bonus are not applicable [no Illusions,
no Theatre letters, etc.], then that category will be skipped in the Bonus
count.  The only category always listed is Magicianship.
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle"> Miscellaneous </span><P>
<b> Ball Saver </b><br>
A short ball saver is active at the beginning of each ball.  "Stay right
where you are!"  There is no ball saver on any multiball, or any other game
situation.
The only exception is the operator adjustable
(on/off) Trunk Ball Save where every hit on the Trunk side during
single-ball play activates a short ball saver. The ball saver on a
buyin is much longer than the standard ball saver time.
<p>
<b> Extra Ball Buy-In </b><br>
Default buyin costs 1 credit, and nothing special happens on the bought
ball other than an extended ball saver.  Default maximum number of buyins is
three.
<p>
The buyin can also be set to 1/2 credit, and the number of buyins can be
set anywhere from 0 [off] to 4, or Unlimited.
<p>
A game with any buyin is disqualified for the "Master Magician" [Grand
Champion] score, as well as the first high score table.  A second table is
reserved for buyin scores only.
