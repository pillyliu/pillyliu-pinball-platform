<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Harlem Globetrotters On Tour SS - Bally | 1978</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/6d886496-f132-44c5-94d3-43b76b3a1d05-medium.jpg" alt="Harlem Globetrotters On Tour backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Shortname:</strong> HARLEM</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">right stand-up target to open the gate, then the four right inline drop targets to maximize your bonus multiplier and the spinner value, then spinner all day.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Left until spinner value maxed and gate open, then Right</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Feeds from the bumpers will get you most often, though unlucky rebounds off of the inline drop targets can, too. When balls are about to fall softly through either center spinner, try to prevent them from angling towards the center.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Harlem is one of the most popular classic games ever. It’s a spinner-and-bonus game, like many of that era. Simple but fun.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Once you get the ball, you want to shoot the inline drops on the right side. Besides being worth 5K each, these do three things: they increase your bonus multiplier to 2X-3X-5X; they increase your spinner value to 100-200-1000-2000; and they spot a letter in Globe. They are your top priority until finished the first time.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">You may also want to hit the right-side stand-up target to open the gate on the right to return a ball heading into the outlane; some people like to do this before shooting the inline drops, others prefer to do the drops first, and a few don’t shoot it at all. I like to get it early as a safety measure.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Once your bonus X and spinner are maxed out, it’s spinner all day. At 2K per spin, plus a likely letter in Globe each trip, you can get over half a million on a ball even before your maximum of 245K [5X49] in bonus.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The top saucer, inline drops and the two central spinners advance your bonus. Don’t bother shooting at the central spinners unless you’re just below a Bonus Hold threshold; even then, most players wouldn’t.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Important setting \#1: whether or not the Bonus Hold feature is on. If it is, when you get to 20K in base bonus, the 20K is held for future balls. Same thing for 30K and 40K.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Important setting \#2: how G-L-O-B-E letters are scored. May be sequential, in which case 5 shots to the saucer scores Globe, or may be random, in which case it will take longer to finish it. If set to sequential (“easy”), consider sending all balls from the right flipper up top through the spinner regardless of its value rather than trying to tap pass or otherwise transfer to the left flipper for shots at the inline targets.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Important setting \#3: whether or not Extra Balls are on. If they are, you get one by completing the stand-up targets on the left, then shooting that recessed gate target on the right.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Important setting \#4: whether the in-line drop targets come back up once completed. The saucer behind them is worth 25k base, 50k when lit for special, so if the drops stay down, that’s a valuable and easy shot.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Key feeds: the rebounds off of each of the four inline drops [some can go SDTM]</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Trivia \#1: The name Harlem comes from the Dutch city of Haarlem. When the Europeans came to America, the Dutch founded the city of New Amsterdam [named after guess where] at the mouth of the Hudson River, and the town of Haarlem, named for a town just west of Amsterdam, where that part of Manhattan now is. Years later, the English Navy forcibly took over the city and renamed the whole place after the Duke of York [who later became King James II]. The Harlem neighborhood kept its name, but the double-“a” was Anglicized down to a single “a”. My original IFPA photo was me holding my Dutch Pinball Masters Classics Champion trophy in front of a windmill in Haarlem a few days after that event. And yes, Harlem Globetrotters was one of the games they used.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Trivia \#2: Yes, the team has played in the Netherlands, including Amsterdam, though I was unable to find any definitive record of them playing an exhibition game in Haarlem (there is a basketball team in Haarlem). They’ve played in 124 countries, so they’ve really earned the name Globetrotters. I’ve seen them on TV but never in person.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Trivia \#3: Hall-of-Famer Wilt Chamberlain played for the Globetrotters before joining the NBA.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Trivia \#4: The bald basketball player was nicknamed “Curly.”</p></div></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Skill Shot:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">make the top saucer, to collect a letter in G-L-O-B-E. Finishing Globe scores 50K each time, might as well make progress towards it. Globe also lights the left outlane for 25k.</p></section></div>
