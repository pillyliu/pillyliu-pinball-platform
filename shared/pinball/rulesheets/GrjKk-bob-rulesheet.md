<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Jive Time EM - Williams | 1970</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/0d1a47b5-7e6d-4bff-9b7a-77ac170d67d6-medium.jpg" alt="Jive Time backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 1</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">raise the two posts, then UTAD with option for center saucer.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">As typical for games of this vintage, mostly bumper action and rebounds from the targets you need to shoot anyway.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Williams liked this 5-bumper arrangement back in the early 70’s, using it several times. Four of the bumpers can be lit by hitting the two stand-up targets just above them on each side of the top saucer - - left for green, right for yellow. The outer two stand-up targets near the top advance bonus, as do the stand-up targets on either side of the center saucer. Base bonus goes up to 10,000 and can be doubled by the spin disk.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">There are two saucers, one up top and one in the center, which award you a random spin value; the spinner is on the backglass, rather than under the playfield. It spins first; you don’t get the currently-pointed-at award. There are six possible awards: 2 each of 1,000 points, 3000 points, collect bonus and “Open West Gate”, plus one each of 10,000 and Double Bonus. The West Gate in essence is “light extra ball” since that’s what a ball through it scores.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">There’s a good chance a ball shot at the center saucer will sail through, but that’s not all bad either since it gets the ball into the bumpers. As long as you’re accurate enough to go in or through rather than hit one of the posts to the side of the saucers, it’s your best controlled shot. The other way to score safely is to shoot the ball up the sides of the game, trying to get the ball to rattle around in the bumpers as long as possible. This sometimes produces an “around the world” shot which may hit more than one of the light bumpers and advance bonus stand-up targets.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">There are two ball saver posts on the game, a light-up center one and a smaller one in the right outlane. The center post is raised by hitting the stand-up target at the far left of the center saucer; the right outlane post is raised by a similar stand-up target on the right. Both posts are lowered when you hit either the down posts stand-up on the right or the down posts rollover button near the westgate on the left. When the center post is up, you can let the ball settle between it and either flipper to set up shots. The right outlane post, though, is not foolproof - - the ball can sneak around it and still drain.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Assuming the bumpers are reasonably lively, I don’t bother shooting for the center spin saucer, I just go up the sides of the game and try to get the ball to bounce off of the bumpers into the top saucer or roll down into the center saucer.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Try to hit the left target to raise the center post a.s.a.p. That will make subsequent shots safer.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Don’t ever shoot at the stand-up targets on either side of the center saucer; you’ll advance the bonus more safely with the upper targets.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Bonus is not awarded at the end of a ball, only by collecting it via a spin. One downside to this game is that the spin values are random, so luck can play a part in the game outcome, e.g. if you get 1,000 and your opponent gets double bonus for 20,000.</p></div></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Skill Shot:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">drop the ball into the top saucer for a Spin.</p></section></div>
