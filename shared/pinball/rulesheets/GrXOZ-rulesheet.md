<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Andromeda SS - Game Plan | 1985</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/f465bda3-8833-4b25-8f2f-3852ab270cbb-medium.jpg" alt="Andromeda backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Shoot the black drop target on the left side, then shoot the orbit behind it to lock a ball. Shoot the stand-up “launch ball” target on the left to start multiball. Shoot the spinner during multiball.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Right</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">balls exiting the top to the right of, left of or between the drop target 3-banks can go down the middle, depending on speed and angle. The right outlane is usually only entered via action from the lower left bumper or right flipper shot rebounds off the lock-guard target, ball release target or misses shots. Rebounds off the drop target banks can drain or go out of control and are best avoided except during multiball.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Starting multiball has three steps: shoot the black drop target on the left; shoot the ball into the scoop above and behind it to lock the ball; shoot the launch ball stand-up target on the left below there to start multiball. The launch ball target is deceptively hard to hit, being somewhat guarded by the corner above the lower bumper. Two catches of note: first, unlike most machines, balls can go through the lock slot to the top without triggering the lock; second, there’s lock stealing here, the machine doesn’t care who locked the ball.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">There’s a playfield multiplier of 2X during multiball. The best strategy is to shoot the balls to the top through the spinner. Completing the six red drop targets scores 50K and advances the spinner value from 1K to 3K. You’ll inevitably get some drop targets without trying to, so just let them happen and only shoot at them if you’re down to just one or two left. Focus on the spinner. Completing the drops a second time is another 50K and raises the spinner value to 4K. Repeatedly playing multiball is the strategy, though if you’re playing last and need a moderate amount of quick points, a high-value spinner shot or two is good.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Bonus goes up to 80K, multiplier up to 10X. Completing the top 3 lanes advances the bonus X; it goes 2X, 3X, 4X, 5X, then suddenly to 10X, so that last set is very valuable. Drop targets and lanes all advance bonus, so don’t actively shoot to increase it, just let it happen when you can’t make other shots.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Lanes on the right side are okay value, 20K and 3 bonus advances each.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">You may want to hold up the left flipper while the ball is rattling around the left bumper to guard against center drains.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">There’s a hidden divider under the plastic behind the drop targets. The ball can come out the center of the drop bank or go to the right lane; the right lane is more common.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Key feed: right lane, does it come to a flipper (usually the right one) or drain down the center?</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Art note: 4 eyes, 4 arms, but only 2 legs. Must have strange shoulder joints.</p></div></section></div>
