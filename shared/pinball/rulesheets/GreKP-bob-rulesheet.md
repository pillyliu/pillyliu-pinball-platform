<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Aquarius EM - Gottlieb | 1970</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/1cfc591f-4ea2-4e0b-8927-2826e7ee759d-medium.jpg" alt="Aquarius backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 1</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">UTAD. Avoid the bottom entirely.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Everything here is a drain risk; expect short game times.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The single most important thing about this game is to note where the saucer kickouts go. Keeping the ball up top is paramount, since the decent points are saucers and lit center bumper hits. You want as many of these as possible. Your best strategy, so far as you can have one on this game, is to find a saucer whose kickout bounces the ball back close to a saucer so that you can get two saucers, perhaps plus a bumper hit, for the price of one. Find any saucer(s) whose kickouts come close to doing this and then look to nudge the bumper rebounds into that saucer.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This means that a 300 saucer whose kickout sends the ball below is less desirable than even a lesser-valued saucer where the kickout stays up top. The center saucer [left-most of the three 300 ones] usually throws the ball down past the bumpers, so avoid it if this is the case. The 200 saucer will throw the ball into the center bumper, which then kicks the ball back up, possibly into a saucer. If that bumper is lit for 100, so much the better. The Skill Shot is usually one of the two right-most 300-point saucers, which typically throw the ball into the right bumper.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">When the ball is on a flipper, shoot it up towards the outside of the right and left bumpers. The flippers are set so low on this game that they probably won’t be able to shoot much more centrally anyway, so this is your best chance to get the ball up top. Avoid the bottom stand-ups whenever possible.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This saucer-bumper-nudge-saucer strategy is the same one as on “2001”.</p></div></section></div>
