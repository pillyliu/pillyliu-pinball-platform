## Quickie Version

Start by touching all four chapter features: **Library**, **Practice**, **League**, and **Game Room**. Each one teaches a different rhythm. Finish all four to light **Final Exam** at the saucer. Final Exam is a short timed mode where completed features convert into a letter grade and a chunky bonus. Win it, cash out, and start the ladder over at a higher value.

<a id="heading--chapter-features"></a>
## Chapter Features

**Library** is the left-loop lesson. A clean orbit spots a reference card and builds **Study Bonus**. Repeating it raises value, but the real purpose is control: the table wants you to settle in before you chase anything ambitious.

**Practice** lives in the center. Hit the standups or finish the drop bank to log reps and raise the **Confidence Meter**. Practice is steady money, especially when a multiplier is lit, and it is the safest way to turn a messy ball into progress.

**League** is the right-ramp chapter. Shoot it when lit to import a match and raise the **Pressure Meter**. League pays best later in the ball, once the calmer chapters have already built you a base.

**Game Room** is the captive-ball chapter. Repeated hits reorganize inserts and improve later cash-outs. It is the joke feature and the important one: if your room is chaotic, every other feature feels worse.

<a id="heading--final-exam"></a>
## Final Exam

Complete all four chapters once to light **Final Exam**. Shoot the saucer to start a short timer. During the mode, any lit chapter shot counts as a correct answer. Alternating chapters scores better than repeating one shot, so the mode rewards range instead of panic. Reach the target before time expires and you earn a letter grade plus a big bonus. A perfect run relights Final Exam at a higher value.

<a id="heading--scoring-and-strategy"></a>
## Scoring and Strategy

The strongest path is not to spam one shot. Open with Library for control, move into Practice for safe points, cash League once the ball is stable, and use Game Room whenever the layout starts feeling crowded. In other words: learn first, train second, compete third, organize constantly. That is the whole PinProf joke turned into a ruleset.

The fail case is also the lesson. If you rush straight into League or force Final Exam too early, the ball gets wild and your scoring falls apart. Play like you are preparing for something important, not trying to prove you were ready from ball one.
