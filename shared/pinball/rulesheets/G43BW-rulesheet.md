<h3><u>Playfield Layout:</u></h3>
<P>
As always, starting at the bottom and working clockwise.
</P>
<P>
<DL>
<DT>Laser Canon<DD>Along the top, left edge of the score card apron is a moving
ball launcher. Balls are fed in from the left ramp. When enabled, the canon
swings out to aim up the playfield. There is a &quot;Fire&quot; button on the lock
down bar to fire the canon at the designated shot.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Flippers<DD>Two standard flippers, right about where you'd expect to find
them.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Left Inlane<DD>A normal inlane. The left ramp dumps the ball here if it is
not fed to the Laser Canon. Has the &quot;H&quot; of &quot;S-H-A-G&quot;.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Left Outlane<DD>A normal outlane. Has the &quot;S&quot; of &quot;S-H-A-G&quot;.
</DD></DT>

<DT>MOJO Standups<DD>Two standup targets.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Mini Me<DD>A spinner with a plastic &quot;Mini Me&quot; figurine on it in place of the
usual paddle. It is counterweighted and spins well when hit. This is at the
entrance to the Left Loop, which leads all the way around to the Right
Loop.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Fat Bastard Ramp<DD>A short ramp leading to a toilet seat with Fat Bastard.
The ball exits the toilet to the &quot;Mini Me&quot; lane to its left.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Big Frickin' Laser<DD>The left ramp. Loops around behind Fat Bastard, down
past Mini Me, and exits to the Laser Canon or the Left Inlane.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Left Standup<DD>A small standup target at the left side of the entrance to
the Time Machine ramp. Goes &quot;beep&quot; when you hit it.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Time Machine<DD>Right in the centre of the playfield, there is a short ramp
that leads up to a spinning disk with a pinball sized hole in the middle.
Shots to this toy are stopped, then released out the back in to the Loop
and returns via the Mini Me spinner.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Right Standup<DD>A small standup target at the right side of the entrance to
the Time Machine ramp. Goes &quot;beep&quot; when you hit it.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Dr. Evil<DD>A motor driven (up/down) target shaped like the good doctor. When
up, hit him with the ball to score ???. Comes up briefly at the beginning
of ball, to show you that he's there.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Rollover Lanes / Skillshot<DD>Two rollover lanes. At the beginning of a ball,
one will be lit to collect the skillshot (Bonux 6X).
</DD></DT>

<DT>Pop Bumpers<DD>Three.
What's to say? They're pop bumpers.</DD></DT>

<DT>Austin Powers Hole<DD>A scoop beneath a large Austin figure. Drop the
ball in the scoop and watch Austin spin. Has playfield lights leading up to
it for ???.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Right Ramp<DD>??? Loops around the back, then down the right side and exits
to the Right Inlane.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Right Loop<DD>???
</DD></DT>

<DT>Right Outlane<DD>A normal outlane. Has the &quot;G&quot; of &quot;S-H-A-G&quot;.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Right Inlane<DD>A normal inlane. The Right Ramp dumps the ball here. Has the
&quot;A&quot; of &quot;S-H-A-G&quot;.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Ball Plunger<DD>A combo-plunger. The player uses the manual plunger. The game
can auto-launch balls to start multiball, ball savers, etc.
</DD></DT>
</DL>
</P>

<h3><u>Rules:</u></h3>

<p>As with many recent games, Austin Powers is a mode based game. Complete the
major shots a number of times to start a mode related to that shot.
</p>

<P>One of the most interesting features of the Austin Powers pinball machine is
that each of the six main modes has a definite successful completion.  During
normal play, you may look at the lights for a mode to identify one of three
states:</P>

<h4>-1-</h4>
   <P> Mode has never been started.  The red light for the mode will be dark
    while the lights below will indicate one of the following patterns [bottom
    to top]</P>

<table border="1" CLASS="body">
<tr><td>    Flash</td><td> Off </td><td> Off </td><td> Off  </td><td> 4 hits will start mode   </td></TR>
<tr><td>     ON  </td><td>Flash</td><td> Off </td><td> Off  </td><td> 3 hits will start mode   </td></TR>
<tr><td>     ON  </td><td> ON  </td><td>Flash</td><td> Off  </td><td> 2 hits will start mode   </td></TR>
<tr><td>     ON  </td><td> ON  </td><td>  ON </td><td>Flash </td><td> next hit will start mode </td></TR>
</table>

<h4>-2-</h4>
   <P> Mode has been started but not completed (i.e. 'failed').  The red light
    at the top will be lit, but the lights below will be as per
    -1-.</P>

<h4>-3-</4>
    <P> Mode has been completed.  All five lights are lit solidly.  Making the
    shot will award a "GROOVY" sound and graphic and award 1M, but have no
    other useful effect.</P>

<P>Completion criteria for each mode:</P>

<DL>
<DT>Mini Me<DD>Complete the Mini Me spinner four times to start Mini Me Hurryup.
Hit the Mini Me spinner again to lock in the value, then more times to
collect that score. During this mode, the number of flashing lights below 
the "Mini-Me" lane will show the number of shots remaining.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Fat Bastard<DD>Complete the Fat Bastard ramp shot four times to start Fat
Bastard Multiball. This is a four-ball multiball with jackpots.
    Hit all six shots four times each during multi-ball.  The number of
    flashing lights below each target shows the number of shots remaining.
    The fourth hit on any shot is worth Super Jackpot, but additional hits
    (until all six have been hit four times each) score nothing.
Lots and lots of farting, too. There is a generous ball saver on the
multiball start.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Frickin' Laser<DD>Complete the Laser ramp shot four times to enable the Big
Frickin' Laser. Up the ramp again to load the ball launcher. It swings out
to aim at the playfield, and you hit the &quot;Fire&quot; button to shoot the
laser 
    into the Austin Powers hole.  If you miss, you may try
    again immediately (without shooting Laser ramp four more times) by shooting
    the ramp within a short time limit.  If you miss a second time, you can
    get one more chance by shooting the ramp quickly.  A third miss ends the
    mode immediately.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Time Machine<DD>A cool and simple ball catcher. Shoot the ramp up to the
hole in the spinning disk. Four times and you start Time Machine multiball.
This is a two-ball multiball with jackpot shots.
    Hit all six shots once during multi-ball.  If the lights below a shot
    are lit, it will award jackpot.  Otherwise the shot will be worthless.
    Making all six shots will relight all six.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Sub-drill<DD>
    Shoot both ramps four times each within time limit.  The number of
    flashing lights below each ramp shows the number of shots remaining.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Henchmen<DD>
    Shoot all six shots once each during time limit.  The top red light near
    each shot will be lit until the shot is made.
</DD></DT>

<DT>S-H-A-G<DD>Spell &quot;S-H-A-G&quot; by collecting the letters from the in/outlanes. Starts
SHAG Hurryup.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Bonus Multiplier<DD>Collect the top two rollover lanes to advance the end of
ball bonus multiplier.
</DD></DT>

</DL>

<P>Except for the multi-ball modes (Fat Bastard and Time Machine), making all
the required shots will cause a mode to exit immediately.  In the multi-ball
modes, you may keep going after making all the shots as long as you keep
more than one ball in play.  If you've made all the shots, however, the mode
will become unavailable as soon as you lose multi-ball.  Note, btw, that if
Time Machine is no longer available, shots to the Time Machine become
dangerous since the ball-saver associated with them will no longer be in
effect.</P>

<P>Starting the fourth mode will light extra ball.  Starting the sixth mode
will enable Virtucon multi-ball.  I've not figured out the exact rules of
this mode, but it's a multi-ball mode with a very generous (over a minute?)
ball-save timer.  Each of six shots will score a 'jackpot' the first time
you hit it, and hitting all six will reset the targets so they may be shot
again.  As a big incentive for completing modes, however, any mode that
you've completed may be shot any number of times for a double jackpot.  So
if you've completed all six shots during the Time Machine multi-ball you can
simply start bashing away at the Time Machine during Virtucon multi-ball for
lots of points.</P>

<P>Each shot in Virtucon multiball will start with the red light lit; the shots
for completed modes will also have the remaining lights flashing.  Making a
shot will turn off the red light but if the other lights were flashing they
will remain so (you need to make all six shots to re-enable shots for non-
completed modes, so having the red light turn off lets you know you've made
the shot even though, as indicated by the other flashing lights, you may
still shoot it again for Super Jackpot.</P>

<P>Based on the backglass display, I believe that completing all six modes will
enable Moon-base multiball.  I have not managed to do that,
however.</P>

<P>The game features a not-terribly-useful "MOJO" multi-ball.  After spelling
MOJO in the left-side targets (or having the multi-ball enabled via Mystery),
the game starts a two-ball multi-ball in which the goal is to bash Dr. Evil
ten times.  Once this is done, Dr. Evil drops down and the six main shots
become available.  Hitting Time Machine awards Super Jackpot and caused Dr.
Evil to pop up until you hit him ten more times, etc.  Dr. Evil is worth
1M, then 1.1M, etc. so bashing him enough times can start to get a little bit
lucrative, though not a whole lot (30 bashes yield 45 million).</P>

<P>A few notes on scoring: watching the display won't tell you where many of the
points in the game come from.  The skill shot is worth 6M plus, and each extra
bonus X earned in the top lanes is 1M.  More significantly, starting
any mode for the first time yields about 10M, and completing any mode yields
about 15M.  A successful laser shot, for example, shows up on the backglass
as being worth 3M, but is in fact worth closer to 30M.</P>

<P>The machine does seem to have some annoying quirks sometimes.  It is very
odd in when it registers Mini-Me and Time-Machine (or, all too often, doesn't).
The left ramp sometimes fails to award an &quot;H&quot; letter since the
ball sometimes skips over the switch.  Hitting Time Machine during MOJO
multi-ball shortly after the machine registers loss of a ball will sometimes
award Super Jackpot and cause Dr. Evil to pop up again and stay up during
normal play (poses a bit of an obstacle).</P>

<P>Mode interactions are rather interesting.  During multi-ball, no other modes
are ever allowed, but otherwise many modes may overlap.</P>

<DL>
<DT>Mini-Me<DD>Does not prevent starting any other mode.  Runs as normal during
  most modes, but timer is suspended during multi-ball.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Fat-Bastard<DD>No other modes possible
</DD></DT>

<DT>Laser<DD>Other modes locked out during try-again timer.  This timer also locks
  out the SHAG lights.  You can still score letters, but you can't see the
  lights to know how to shift the lanes (BOO!).
</DD></DT>

<DT>Time Machine<DD>No other modes possible
</DD></DT>

<DT>Sub-Drill<DD>Locks out Laser, but other modes possible.  Runs as normal during
  most modes, but timer is suspended during multi-ball.
</DD></DT>

<DT>Henchmen<DD>Locks out other modes except perhaps MOJO multi-ball.
</DD></DT>
</DL>

<P>Starting Mini-Me or Sub-Drill just before Fat Bastard can be very lucrative,
since you have lots of time and four balls with which to repeatedly bash the
shots that need repeated hits.</P>

<h3><u>Ball Savers</u></h3>

<P>
The game includes two ball-savers in addition to the
very long ball-savers on multi-ball.  If the ball drains immediately after
SCORING the time-machine (it must be an eligible target) during single-ball
play, the machine will try once to relaunch it (if the relaunch attempt
fails, you lose).  Also, if you start the SHAG Hurryup with an outlane
during single-ball play you'll get the ball back.  Note that for that to
work the system must recognize that you are in single-ball play.  Being in
two-ball play and having both balls go down opposite outlanes simultaneously
starting the hurry-up does not qualify.</P>

<P>Since the &quot;Shoot again&quot; light stops blinking well before the end of the
multi-ball ball-saver, I have found the best way to judge the expiration of
the ball-save is to listen to the music.  The music has an ABAC pattern, and
the multi-save ends around the start of the C [for Time Machine, it's the
ominous slow notes; don't know how best to describe other modes].</P>

<P>As for the other two ball-savers, if the ball drains immediately
after SCORING the time-machine during single-ball play, the machine will try
once to relaunch it (if the relaunch attempt fails, you lose).  Also, if you
start the SHAG Hurryup with an outlane during single-ball play you'll get the
ball back.  Note that for that to work the system must recognize that you are
in single-ball play.  Being in two-ball play and having both balls go down
opposite outlanes simultaneously starting the hurry-up does not qualify.
</P>

<h3><u>Artwork:</u></h3>

<P>Very bright and colourful artwork. As expected, it's 1960s cartoony with
flowers, psychedelic swirls and Brittish flags. If you're in to the theme,
you'll probably find lots of cool things to look at.
</P>

<P>For some reason, there is a small plastic cat screwed to the inside of the
cabinet, near the upper right hand corner of the playfield. It's too small
to be easily seen by the player and serves no obvious purpose. The next
version of the hidden cow maybe?
</P>

<h3><u>Sounds:</u></h3>

<P>Lots of theme-specific comments, voice quotes, and such. A few random
&quot;beep&quot; noises for the standup targets by the entrance to the Time Machine
ramp. As with the artwork, if you are in to this theme, you'll find lots to
like here.
</P>


<h3><u>Comments:</u></h3>

<P>I give the Stern guys high marks for theme integration on this one. Despite
the fact that I don't care for the theme itself, it's well used and the
various voice quotes and sounds fit well in to the game. The toys are
appropriate and the Time Machine ball catcher thing is both simple and
cool. The artwork is loud, but appropriate and (IMHO) attractive.
</P>

<P>This game might be set up a tad too easy, as I only paid for my first
credit, played three games, and had to leave with a credit still on it.
</P>

<P>A few people have commented on the playfield layout so far. Maybe it's me,
but it seems a lot like AFM or MM to me. The shots are pretty much the
same, and in the same order, as AFM. This isn't a bad thing, as AFM was a
popular game, but there's no new ground being broken in playfield design.
</P>
