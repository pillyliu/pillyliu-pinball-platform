<span class="bodyTitle">Summary</span><P>
Twister is based on the blockbuster movie of the same name.  The
pinball machine is very much a typical Sega Pinball with that
Sega "feel".  The rules differ in that it doesn't have a progressive
mode based rule set but instead has different modes that can be
started independently at different times.  These modes are well
balanced scoring-wise so that the strategy can change at different
times in the game
<p>
There are many similarities with the older Williams game, Whirlwind
with the spinning disk on the playfield and fan on top of the
backbox.  Overall, in my opinion I give the machine a B+.
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle">Background</span><P>
Rent the movie...
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle">Playfield</span><P>
Here's the various playfield parts, starting from the plunger lane
and working clockwise.
<p>
<dl>
<dt> Plunger<dd>
Dual plunger, automatic and manual.  Note as with all Sega
games, flipping both flippers will release the ball automatically.</dd>
<dt> Right Outlane<dd>
Can be lit for Blown Away.  Simply some extra points to make
you feel better about losing your ball.  Slings alternate lit
outlane.</dd>
<dt> Right Inlane<dd>
Scores South compass direction when not lit.  Flippers alternate
lit inlane with the other (except in Weathervane mode).  Also
lights left orbit for Wind.</dd>
<dt> Flippers<dd>
The usual two, in the usual places. Center post too.
Typical Sega type flippers.</dd>
<dt> Slingshots<dd>
Standard, just like the flippers.  Seem to be stronger than
usual Sega games.  This can be bad in that it often launches
the ball off the playfield (and sometimes to the outlane!).</dd>
<dt> Left Inlane<dd>
Scores West(?) compass direction when not lit.  Flippers alternate
lit inlane with the other (except in Weathervane mode).  Also
lights right orbit for Wind.</dd>
<dt> Left Outlane<dd>
Can be lit for Blown Away (see right outlane).</dd>
<dt> Southwest and Northwest Targets<dd>
Scores respective compass directions when not lit.</dd>
<dt> Left Orbit<dd>
Can be lit for Wind (See Cow Multibull below).  Can also be lit for
Debris (See Twister Targets below).  Lights itself for Wind if not
lit.</dd>
<dt> Twister Targets<dd>
Completing Twister targets (that spell Twister) IN ORDER awards
next Twister mode (see below).</dd>
<dt> Cannister Target/Hole<dd>
Drop target hides a hole (see Cannister Multiball below).</dd>
<dt> Rollovers<dd>
Three rollovers.  Complete rollovers to advance Bonus X.  Bonus X
maxes out at 8x?  Completing after that scores Extra Ball Lit once
per game.  After that, 20M?</dd>
<dt> Pop Bumpers<dd>
Score 250K a hit except when lit for Super Pops, score 1M a pop.
Completing a certain number scores a certain value.  This may
progress somehow.   It may also light Super Pops.</dd>
<dt> Orbit Magnet<dd>
A magnet at the top of the orbit grabs balls when an orbit shot
at a lit Wind occurs.  This captures the ball and scores the
Wind value.  Also active for all balls shot here when Super
Pops are active.</dd>
<dt> Ramp<dd>
Only one ramp, advances towards Chase Multiball (see below),
scores Extra Ball, used during Chase Hurry-Up, and Twister
Multiball for jackpots.  Ball is fed to playfield just above
left flipper.  Also lights Wind on right orbit.</dd>
<dt> Ramp Targets<dd>
Targets on each side of ramp score North and Northeast
compass directions respectively when not lit.</dd>
<dt> Right Orbit<dd>
Can be lit for Wind (See Cow Multibull below).  Can also be lit for
Debris (See Twister Targets below).  Lights itself for Wind if not
lit.</dd>
<dt> East and Southeast Targets<dd>
Scores respective compass directions when not lit.</dd>
<dt> Spinning Disk<dd>
The main toy.  A spinning disk with a magnet on it.  The magnet
is active during skill shot and at start of Cannister Multiball.
Also on during Cannister Multiball where it captures a ball and
lets it go.  The disk spins most of the time throughout the
game, diverting your ball slightly.</dd>
</dl>
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle">General Rules</span><P>
Holding right flipper and pressing start puts the game in
Wizard mode.  We have no idea how the rules differ here yet.
Holding left flipper and pressing start, starts "League Play".
See below for differences in rules.
<p>
Buy In is NOT enabled in default settings.  No Buy-In high score list.
<p>
Software Changes: None known at this time...
<p>
In general, there are many things to do in this game.  See the different
modes below and try them out!  There doesn't appear to be any long
term goal besides scoring points and not draining.
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle">Skill Shot</span><P>
Shoot ball with plunger towards magnet on spinning disk.  If ball is
captured before a switch is hit, score skill shot.
Skill shot awards are generally scored in the following order:
<p>
<ul>
<li> 30M
<li> Free Game or Extra Ball Lit or Weathervane
<li> Super Pops
<li> Super Lock
<li> Max Bonus Multiplier?
<li> Hold Bonus?
</ul>
<p>
The free game and extra ball lit vary based on current score, machine
settings, and past game award history.  This order can vary from player
to player unless the game is set to League play in which case each player
will receive the same skill shot awards in order.
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle">Awarded Modes</span><P>
There are a set of modes which can be "awarded" to a player.  This can
happen via completing the compass targets or through the skill shot.
Modes are not repeated until they are all completed.
<p>
<b>   Weathervane</b><br>
All other game activity stops for this mode EXCEPT scoring locks for
Cannister Multiball and starting and playing said mode.  After Multiball
ends, Weathervane resumes.
<p>
All compass targets are flashing.  You get 10M for starting and 5M+10M for
each target scored.  Once all targets are complete or 30?
seconds pass, mode ends.  No extra award for completing targets.
<p>
Note inlane compass directions do NOT rotate with flippers!  Get that
Huxley shot down!  You actually need that here!
<p>
<b>   Super Pops</b><br>
Pops score 1M a hit for rest of ball (or after X hits?).  Stacking
Super Pops awards does nothing.  When Super Pops is lit, upper orbit
magnet always grabs the ball.
<p>
<b>   Extra Ball Lit</b><br>
Shoot ramp for extra ball.  These DO stack.
<p>
<b>   Super Lock</b><br>
A three ball multiball.  Hurry-up value starts at 42M and counts
down to 10M.  Shoot left orbit to lock value.  Shoot lock hole to award
value until back down to one ball.
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle">Twister Targets</span><P>
Complete Twister targets IN ORDER to score following background modes
in order.  These modes are not active during a multiball mode or
Weathervane.
<p>
<b>   Debris</b><br>
Shoot left orbit to score 10M, 20M, 30M, 40M, 50M, and 60M respectively at
which point mode ends.
<p>
<b>   Chase Hurry Up</b><br>
10M for starting plus a count up from 20M to 50M.  Each shot to ramp
scores value.  Ramp and pops temporarily stop count up.
<p>
<b>   Damage</b><br>
Identical to Debris except at right orbit.
<p>
<b>   Double Scoring</b><br>
Everything scores double for rest of ball!
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle">Cannister Multiball</span><P>
[Factoid: This was originally called Dorothy II multiball in the
prototypes yet was renamed due to potential licensing risks with
MGM's Wizard of Oz.]
<p>
Shoot lock drop target to reveal hole.  Shoot hole to lock ball.
First multiball requires 3 locks (5 on extra hard settings), next 4,
and subsequent multiballs require 5 balls.  When drop target drops for
the start multiball shot, it only remains down for a certain period of
time.  The time is shorter for subsequent multiballs.
<p>
The number of balls is generally the number of locks EXCEPT when
playing multi-player in which case you get a ball for each lock
at minimum PLUS any previous players balls that have been locked in
the Cannister.
<p>
The balls are ejected from the cannister or auto plunged to make up
any balls short in the cannister.  They are deposited onto the spinning
disk and magnet.  Once they are collected, the magnet releases and the
balls go flying!  You get 25M for starting.
<p>
Shoot the ramp for jackpot which starts at 10M * balls in play and builds
by 5M per cannister lock shot, and 202,220 per switch.  Once it is scored,
you have 8-16 seconds to shoot ramp again (depending on difficulty
settings) to score double jackpot, then again for triple jackpot.
After any three jackpots are scored then you must shoot the lock for
super jackpot for the sum of the three jackpots you just scored.  Repeat
until only one ball remains.  Super Jackpot timer is 7, 9, 11, 13, or 15
seconds depending on difficulty settings.
<p>
Note: If ball is going to be auto plunged, manually plunge ball hard
so it doesn't collect on magnet and gives you time to try and shoot
it up the ramp while the other balls are kept out of your way.
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle">Chase Multiball</span><P>
Shoot ramp 5 times (plus number of previous Chase Multiballs played)
to start Chase Multiball.  Shooting ramp twice in succession scores
an extra ramp, thrice in succession, two extra, etc.
<p>
Chase multiball is a 5 ball multiball.  Your objective is to advance
through the different tornado levels F1 through F5.  You must score (40
+ F Level scored * 20) switches to light ramp for jackpot and add-a-ball.
jackpot scores 1M * switches scored to light it.  Each switch scores 1M
while jackpot is lit.  Once F5 is scored, mode restarts at beginning but
with 140 switches to light jackpot.
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle">Cow Multibull ("We've got Cows!!")</span><P>
Shoot orbits when lit for Wind to advance towards Cow Multibull.  Each
Wind shot scores ?.  Initially 9 shots are required to start Cow Multibull.
Subsequent starts each require 1? more?   If the lit orbit is scored twice
in succession, an extra advance is scored.  This only happens if the orbit
magnet misses (or is broken), or the ball rolls up and down the lane
without getting captured.
<p>
Cow Multibull is a 5 ball multibull.  Each orbit is lit for a 20M jackpot
which advances 250K for each switch.  It completes when one ball is left.
Next cow multibull jackpot value resets to 20M.
"Mooo!"
<span class="bodyTitle">Combos</span><P>
Successive ramps and orbits gain an extra ramp or orbit.
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle">Bonus</span><P>
1M/Ramp + 1M/Twister Target (lit or unlit) + 3M/Orbit + 3M/Lock.
Cap on each of 99M.  Only shots made that ball count.
<p>
This bonus is then multiplied by the current bonus multiplier
which is not held.  It can be up to 8X.  Once bonus multiplier
is maxed, next scored may light Extra Ball, otherwise 20M is awarded.
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle">Extra Balls</span><P>
There are many ways to collect extra balls in this game:
<ul>
<li> Possible from Skill Shot
<li> Possible from Completing Compass Targets
<li> Maxing Bonus X
</ul>
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle">League Play Mode</span><P>
Hold left flipper while pressing start button.  Only known difference
observed for sure is that skill shot and compass awards are consistent
from player to player.
<p>
<span class="bodyTitle">Strategy</span><P>
The big modes are Cow Multibull, Chase Multiball, and Cannister Multiball
generally in that order.  So get your orbit shots down!!  Shoot orbits
when lit.  Shoot ramp or lock hole when respective orbit is not lit.
Start those modes and score as much as you can!  That's about it.  Go
for the other modes when they present themselves.  AVOID Weathervane!
Since everything else stops, the risk of the mode is not worth it in
general.  If your skill shot is lit for Weathervane, intentionally miss
it.  If you get it by accident you might as well play it, it is points,
but it is more danger than it is worth, I feel.
<p>
A good score is over 1B.  A wizard score is 3B+...
