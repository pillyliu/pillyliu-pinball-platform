<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Bank-A-Ball EM - Gottlieb | 1965</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/1e42bf35-7f32-4d73-93b8-d32fc6fee0d3-medium.jpg" alt="Bank-A-Ball backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 1</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">UTAD.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Everything; even the \#6 and \#14 lane feeds aren’t always safe. Learn to anticipate how balls coming down from the top bounce or roll depending on their speed and approach angle; you might at least occasionally prevent some drains. Keep balls away from the tops of the slingshot mounts, especially the outer edges.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Bank-A-Ball is not designed for tournament play, instead being geared specifically towards winning free games. I include it here for reference only.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Collect pool balls 1-7 or 9-15 to light the center stand-up target for the 8-ball and a special. Once you have 1-8, the right side lane labeled “Low” lights for a special; if you have 8-15, the left side “High” lane lights for a special.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The bumpers are lit by either side lane at the top and by either return lane to the flippers. By the way, this was the first pinball machine to have the now-standard return lanes. The center stand-up 8-ball target turns the bumpers off.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">There’s little strategy to speak of, just flip the ball into the bumpers and nudge for the best. The only time to shoot at a stand-up target is if you need it for a replay.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Gottlieb’s marketing folks made a big deal when this machine came out about the “animated backglass” where the balls scored drop down in the glass box there.</p></div></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Skill Shot:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">make either “lights bumper” outer lane. If you’re close to getting a replay, go instead for any number you still need.</p></section></div>
