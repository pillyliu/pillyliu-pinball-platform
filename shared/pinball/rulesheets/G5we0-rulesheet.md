<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="K00l_abbreviations_&amp;_words"></a>1.  K00l abbreviations &amp; words</span><P>
<ul>
<li>    Bug - the word used for a hostile alien species in the Starship
Troopers universe
<li>    K - 1,000 points
<li>    M - A million points
<li>    SDTM - Straight Down The Middle
<li>    MB - Multiball
<li>    DMD - Dot Matrix Display
<li>    EB - Extra Ball
<li>    ST - Starship Troopers
</ul>
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Playfield_Description"></a>2.  Playfield Description</span><P>
In the most clockwise order I could come up with:
<p>
<dl>
<dt>Left flipper<dd>Standard flipper here.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Left inlane<dd>Lights the right loop for an Orbit Multiball
letter.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Arm Nukes hole<dd>A small hole just above the left inlane.  Can be lit for EB
and Planet Multiball.  The ball is kicked out from the hole to
the right flipper.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Bug standups<dd>Above the Arm Nukes hole, are four standups, standing side to side.
Two yellow targets, one red and one green.  The colors correspond
to a particular bug species (see 4. Main Objective).  Hit a flashing
standup and kill a bug - simple as that.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Left loop<dd>A loop, going all around the back of the playfield, ending up at the
entrance to the Right loop.  The loop can be either unlit, in which
case the ball will be sent to bonus X rollovers, or lit for an Orbit
Multiball letter.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Left ramp, "STARSHIP"<dd>Shooting the ramp lights STARSHIP (see 8. Recon Awards).  Lit for
jackpots during regular Multiball.  A yellow Bug standup is to the
left of the ramp entrance.  Slow shots drop into the Orbit Multiball
lock (see below), really slow shots have a nasty tendency of going
SDTM.</dd>
<p>
<dt>The Warrior<dd>This is sort of a mobile standup target.  It can move back and forth
(slowly).  Shooting it a number of times (see 6. Regular Multiball)
will light Multiball.  As you shoot it, it moves backwards, finally
revealing a sinkhole - this is where Multiball is started and
Super Jackpots collected.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Orbit Multiball lock<dd>Behind the Warrior is a small sinkhole.  A magnet can stop balls
coming from the left or right loop and drop the ball into the
sinkhole for an Orbit Multiball lock.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Bug standups II<dd>More standups;  Two green, two blue.  Some of them can be hit by
random bumper action.  The standups flank the exit from the bumper
area.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Bumpers<dd>Three bumpers, arranged the usual way.  Balls can exit through
a passage to the right of the left ramp.</dd>
<p>
<dt>B-U-G rollovers<dd>Located just above the bumpers.  Spell B-U-G to advance bonus X by
one.  Just above the 'U' is another magnet that can stop a ball
coming from either loop and drop it to the rollovers.  This is
what happens when you shoot an unlit loop.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Recon Award scoop<dd>A small scoop, just below the bumper area.  See 8. Recon Awards for
the details.  Balls coming from the Orbit MB lock and the Warrior
sinkhole are kicked out here as well.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Right ramp, "TROOPERS"<dd>Shooting the ramp lights TROOPERS (see 8. Recon Awards).  Lit for
jackpots during regular Multiball.  A yellow Bug standup is to the
right of the ramp entrance.  Slow shots drop into the bumper area.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Brain bug<dd>A target hidden underneath the playfield, which can pop up for you
to shoot at.  And it's a huge target!  Located infront of the
Right ramp/Recon scoop.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Right loop<dd>Same as the left loop.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Bug standups III<dd>Even more standups;  two yellow, one green and one red.  These
are just above the right slingshot.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Right inlane<dd>Lights the left loop for an Orbit Multiball letter.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Right outlane<dd>This one is a bit different;  it's actually a combined
outlane / shooter lane.  A one-way gate above the combo-plunger
sees to that drained balls leave the playfield peacefully.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Right mini-flipper<dd>Mounted to the right of, and slightly above, the standard-sized
right flipper is a small Thing-sized flipper.  The flipper is
controlled with a separate button mounted below the normal
flipper button.  Trust me, this is confusing at first!</dd>
<p>
<dt>Right flipper<dd>Standard flipper.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Status displays<dd>Just above the flippers are four single-digit displays.  These tell
you how many bugs are left on the planet currently under attack.
The displays are:  Warriors (yellow), Plasma Bugs (blue),
Hoppers (green), Tankers (red).</dd>
<p>
<dt>Planet status<dd>Above the Status displays are the six planets o' bugs portrayed;
Antilles, Zegema Beach, Dantana, Tango Urilla, Planet P, and finally
the ultimate bug planet from hell, Klendathu.  These can be
unlit (not attacked), flashing (have been attacked) or solid
(have been nuked as well).</dd>
</dl>
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Skill_shots"></a>3.  Skill shots</span><P>
There are three possible skill shots:
<p>
<ol>
<li> Plunge the ball;  a magnet will catch the ball above the B-U-G
rollovers.  One of the rollover lights will be flashing;  move it
with the flipper buttons.  If you do it right, you're awarded
+1 Bonus X.  (This is very similar to the skillshot in Medieval
Madness)
<p>
<li> Hold the left flipper button as you plunge the ball;  hitting
the right ramp will award a 5M Ramp Skillshot.  Holding the
left button will disable the magnet that otherwise would've
captured the ball.
<p>
<li> Hold the left flipper button as you plunge the ball;  plunge just
hard enough to get the ball into the Orbit Multiball sinkhole.
You will be awarded an Orbit MB letter.  This probably isn't an
"official" skillshot though.
</ol>
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Main_Objective"></a>4.  Main Objective</span><P>
Six planets full of bugs, and you must kill 'em all.  You attack the
planets one at a time in a fixed order.  There are four potential
species to be annihilated;  Warriors, Plasma Bugs, Hoppers and
Tankers.  The type of bugs and their numbers differ from planet to
planet.  To kill a bug, you simply shoot any flashing Bug standup.
Each bug is associated with a color:
<p>
<table BORDER=1 CLASS="body">
<tr><td> Warrior    </td><td>Yellow </td></TR>
<tr><td> Hopper     </td><td>Green  </td></TR>
<tr><td> Plasma Bug </td><td>Blue   </td></TR>
<tr><td> Tanker     </td><td>Red    </td></TR>
</TABLE>
<p>
So, to kill a Hopper, you shoot for the flashing GREEN standup of your
choice.  Note that bugs can be killed only during regular play!
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="The_Planets"></a>4.1  The Planets</span><P>
When the game starts, Antilles is under attack.  To get to the next planet,
you must first eliminate all bugs.  At first, plenty of Bug standups will
be lit.  However, as you kill more and more bugs the number of lit
standups will decrease.
<p>
The planets and their inhabitants, in order of appearance:

The point values for the different bugs increase as you move on to new

Warrior on Zegema Beach is worth 150K.  Point values for Bugs on
each planet:

<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Mini-flipper_scoring"></a>4.3  Mini-flipper scoring</span><P>
If you shoot a Bug standup with the mini-flipper, that hit will count
as two hits.  Get good at shooting bugs with the mini-flipper;  you
will advance through planets much quicker if you do!  It is possible
to cheat the machine by flipping the mini-flipper after you've made
a shot with one of the two regular flippers.
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="The_Brain_bug"></a>4.4  The Brain bug</span><P>
Once all bugs on a planet have been whacked, you may try to capture
the Brain bug.  The Brain bug is represented by a huge standup target
that pops up infront of the Recon Award scoop.  To capture it, you must
hit it a number of times (it's hard to miss).  The first time you need
one hit, the second time two hits, etc.  Each hit is worth 1M.  This is
a timed mode (though I can't remember a timer being displayed);  if you
run out of time, the Brain bug escapes.  I usually trap the ball and
wait for the Brain bug to disappear;  the risk you take shooting at it
isn't worth a couple of millions.
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Advance_Planet_vs._Planet_Multiball"></a>4.5  Advance Planet vs. Planet Multiball</span><P>
Once the Brain bug is gone (doesn't matter if you captured it or let
it escape), the Arm Nukes hole will be lit for Planet Multiball, and
the Recon scoop will be lit for Advance Planet.  They will remain
lit until either shot has been made.  A hurry-up, starting at 5M and
counting down to 1M, is started at the same time;  shoot the Recon
scoop or the Arm Nukes hole to collect.
<p>
You should try to advance to the next planet, unless you're already
on the fifth planet (Planet P), in which case that option is
unavailable.  If you collect Advance Planet, you will return to
regular play with a whole bunch of new Bugs to whack.
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Planet_Multiball"></a>5.  Planet Multiball</span><P>
Planet Multiball is an N-ball Multiball, where N is determined by the
number of planets being nuked.  All flashing planets fall victim of
the nuke attack.  The more planets you nuke simultaneously, the higher
the point values during Planet Multiball, and the more balls you get.
The idea is to advance to as many planets as possible before starting
Planet MB.
<p>
The following shots will be lit at the start of Planet Multiball:
<p>
Left loop, Left ramp, Recon scoop, Right ramp, Right loop,
Arm Nukes hole.
<p>
You must make the first five Nuke shots to light Super Nuke at the Warrior.
The Recon scoop awards a Double Nuke.  The Arm Nukes hole will spot a
Nuke for you and award the associated jackpot.  Each shot has a
flashing arrow or lamp labeled "Nuke" that will turn solid once hit.
<p>
The point values depending on the number of planets being nuked:
<p>
<table BORDER=1 CLASS="body">
<tr><td><b># planets</b></td><td><b>Nuke Jackpot</b></td><td><b>Super Nuke</b></td><td><b>Number of balls</b></td></TR>
<tr><td> 1    </td><td>1M       </td><td>10M      </td><td>2  </td></TR>
<tr><td> 2    </td><td>2.5M     </td><td>25M      </td><td>2  </td></TR>
<tr><td> 3    </td><td>4M       </td><td>40M      </td><td>3  </td></TR>
<tr><td> 4    </td><td>7M       </td><td>70M      </td><td>4  </td></TR>
<tr><td> 5    </td><td>12M      </td><td>120M     </td><td>4  </td></TR>
</TABLE>
<p>
Once the Super Nuke has been collected, all Nuke shots are relit for
another go.  This multiball can be worth a lot!  After Planet MB is
over, all nuked planets will be lit solid.
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Regular_Multiball"></a>6.  Regular Multiball</span><P>
(I believe it's actually called Live Fire Assault Range MB.)
<p>
Shooting the Warrior a number of times will start Multiball.  The first
Multiball it takes 4 hits, second MB 6 hits, etc.  As you shoot the
Warrior target, it will move backwards.  When only one shot is left
to light MB, the Warrior will move back and forth to indicate this.
<p>
The Multiball itself is a 4-ball MB.  Both ramps are lit for jackpots;
the jackpot value starts at 3M and can be increased to a maximum of 4M.
You increase the jackpot value by hitting the Bug standups.  All
standups will be unlit at the start of MB;  if you hit a standup, it
will light up, and will not add points to the jackpot value anymore.
Lighting all standups awards an Extra Ball (which I haven't been able
to confirm, since one of the standups is broken on the machine I'm
playing).
<p>
You need five jackpots to light the Super Jackpot, which is worth
a whopping 15M.  The Warrior will at all times spot a jackpot for you,
but will not award the associated point value.  The Super Jackpot
remains lit for 16 seconds.  Once the Super Jackpot has been collected,
or the timer has reached zero, the cycle repeats.
<p>
If you fail to shoot any jackpots at all, a Multiball restart sequence
will commence.  You have some 15-20 seconds to get the ball into the
Warrior sinkhole.  You only get two balls in the restarted MB.
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Orbit_Multiball"></a>7.  Orbit Multiball</span><P>
To start Orbit Multiball, you must make two Orbit locks.  To lock
the first ball, you need to shoot three lit loops (the DMD will
display the word ONE, with the collected letter flashing).  The inlane
switches light the opposite loop, for a period of around 5 seconds.
The third loop shot will be caught by a magnet, and dropped into the
Orbit MB sinkhole.  The same applies for lock number two, with the only
difference the word to spell is TWO.  So, all in all, it takes six loop
shots to start Orbit MB.  This number does not increase.  Note that
making a lit loop shot does not unlight that loop, so it's fully
possible to make 3 loops shots in a row for an Orbit MB lock!
<p>
Orbit Multiball starts out as a two-ball Multiball.  Both loops are lit
for a 5M jackpot, and remain lit throughout Orbit MB.  A successful loop
shot will be caught by the magnet, dropped into the sinkhole and then
kicked out from the Recon scoop.  The first two loop shots will add a
ball as well, even if the shot is made after the ballsaver has
expired.  Thus, a maximum of four balls can be in play.
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Recon_Awards"></a>8.  Recon Awards</span><P>
A Recon Award is a pseudo-random award collected in the Recon scoop.
By shooting the left (STARSHIP) and right (TROOPERS) ramp you go to
the next Recon Level.  There are six Recon Levels (1-6).  The higher
you go before collecting at the scoop, the better the awards.  The
DMD will display four choices, and one of them will "randomly" be
picked for you.  The scoop is lit for a Recon Level 1 award at the
start of each ball.
<p>
The following awards are those I know of:
<p>
<table border="1" CLASS="body">
<tr><td><b>Level</b></td><td><b>Awards</b></td></TR>
<tr><td> 1 </td><td>Ground Attack, Psychic Test, Spot Species, Bonus Hold, Recon Bounty 1M </td></TR>
<tr><td> 2 </td><td>Ground Attack, Orbits lit, Super Pops, Recon Bounty 2M </td></TR>
<tr><td> 3 </td><td>Double Scoring, Spot Planet, Multiball Ready, Recon Bounty 5M </td></TR>
<tr><td> 4 </td><td>Ramp Hurry-up, Air Attack, Recon Bounty 8M, Nuke Chase </td></TR>
<tr><td> 5 </td><td>Multiball, Multiball Ready, Orbit Multiball, Extra Ball </td></TR>
<tr><td> 6 </td><td>Extra Ball, Special, 25M points </td></TR>
</TABLE>
<p>
The awards have been known to move up or down a level.  The major awards
(such as Orbit MB, Double Scoring, Ramp Hurry-up) seem to not move, though.
<p>
It is my experience that level 5 will always award Regular MB if you
haven't had one already, otherwise Orbit MB.  Level 6 will award 25M.
Note that Regular MB and Orbit MB usually are worth much more than 25M.
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Recon_Boost_&amp;_Combos"></a>8.1  Recon Boost &amp; Combos</span><P>
You'll sometimes be awarded a Recon Boost when you shoot the Recon scoop
(only when it's lit for a Recon Award).  This seems to be random, and can
be very annoying if you're fishing after that lucrative Orbit MB and get
25M instead.  You can be boosted any number of levels, I think.
<p>
A more controlled way of boosting the Recon Level is to do a Recon
Combo.  I'm not 100% sure how this works just yet;  if you shoot the
Right ramp, Left ramp, Recon scoop you boost three additional levels.
Or so I like to think, anyway.        <i>Could use some help here</i>
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="List_o'_Recon_awards"></a>8.2  List o' Recon awards</span><P>
The awards described briefly:
<p>
<dl>
<dt>Ground Attack
<dd>    A two-ball multiball.  The DMD will cycle through 100K, 200K,
300K, 400K *rapidly*.  Hitting any Bug standup target will award
the displayed value.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Psychic Test
<dd>    The video mode.  You get to cycle through a deck of cards with the
left flipper button.  Select with the right button.  If your card
matches the one picked by the game you're awarded a Special.  There
are also point awards for matching color, number, etc.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Spot Species
<dd>    Kills all bugs of the species in majority.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Bonus Hold
<dd>    Holds the bonus multiplier, I would assume.  I've never gotten it.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Recon Bounty
<dd>    Point award.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Orbits Lit
<dd>    Both loops will be lit for an Orbit MB letter, for the remainder of
the ball in play.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Super Pops
<dd>    Bumpers worth 50K / hit.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Double Scoring
<dd>    All points values are doubled.  Lasts 20 seconds.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Spot Planet
<dd>    Kills all bugs on the planet currently under attack.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Multiball Ready
<dd>    Lights Regular Multiball at the Warrior.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Ramp Hurry-up
<dd>    A hurry-up starting at 3M, ticking down to 1M.  Shoot either ramp
to lock the value.  For 20 seconds, both ramps will be worth that
same value.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Air Attack
<dd>    Same as Ground Attack, only with a 100k-900k scoring range.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Nuke Chase
<dd>    Shoot the lit Nuke for 3M+250K.  The Nuke will move even if you
don't make the shot.  Lasts 25 seconds.</dd>
</dl>
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Extra_Balls"></a>9.  Extra Balls</span><P>
There are at least five ways of collecting an Extra Ball.  Of course,
I haven't been able to collect all of them due to some weird Lyle
settings.  No Extra Balls for me.  Anyway, here's a list of the EB's
I know of:
<p>
<ul>
<li> Play poorly.  A freebie EB will be lit on ball 3 if you have a score
lower than 20-30M.
<p>
<li> 15 shots to an unlit Arm Nukes hole will award an Extra Ball.
<p>
<li> 50 ramp shots will light EB.  After that it's 100 ramps.  After that,
I'm not sure.
<p>
<li> During Regular MB, light all Bug standup targets.
<p>
<li> Have some luck with the recon Level 5 or 6 award.  I have yet to see
it actually award an EB though.
</ul>
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Miscellany"></a>10.  Miscellany</span><P>
<dl>
<dt>Raise in rank
<dd> By shooting the Arm Nukes hole a number of times, you can raise in
in rank.  Four shots are needed to become Squad Leader (worth 5M),
after that it's N shots for Corporal (worth 10M).  I'm not sure
what comes after that.</dd>
<p>
<dt>End of Ball Bonus
<dd> Your bonus is determined by the number of bugs killed during that
ball.  Hoppers and Warriors are worth 100K a piece.  I believe the
same applies to Plasma bugs and Tankers.  Brain bugs are worth
300K.  The entire bonus is multiplied with the Bonus X value.
You increase bonus X by completing the B-U-G rollovers.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Shooting the Warrior
<dd> The Warrior moves pretty slowly;  sometimes this means it hides
the sinkhole behind it.  When a critical shot like the Super
Jackpot is lit, it is good to know that, at all times, the Warrior
standup counts as the sinkhole.</dd>
<p>
<dt>High-score table

<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Spoilers"></a>11.  Spoilers</span><P>
Spoilers ahead!  Close your eyes and think happy thoughts in case you
want to find out just what happens at planet Klendathu for yourself.
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Klendathu"></a>11.1  Klendathu</span><P>
Once all five planets have been nuked and cleared from Bugs, planet
Klendathu will be flashing.  Shoot the Recon scoop to start the attack
on Klendathu.  You get unlimited balls for 30 seconds.  To finish the
mode, you must kill 9 Bugs of each species (all Bug standups are lit)
and make all five Nuke shots.  The Nuke shots are worth 5M a piece,
the Double Nuke (collected at the Recon scoop) is worth 10M.  I assume
the Bugs are worth something as well, but the DMD doesn't display any
information on that.  If you manage to kill all the Bugs and make all
the shots, you're awarded a 50M completion bonus.  Note that once the
timer has expired, the mode will continue with the balls still in
play.  I'm assuming the release software has induced a few changes
to this mode;  with beta software, the mode continues until *all*
balls have drained.
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Easter_eggs"></a>11.2  Easter eggs</span><P>
When the military dude is briefing you ("This planet has 8 Hoppers..."),
press both flipper buttons.  He will die a most gruesome death.
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Known_software_glitches"></a>12.  Known software glitches</span><P>
(note how I cleverly avoided the word "bug")
<p>
OK, so I've been playing a machine with beta software.  I hope this
has been fixed in the release software:
<p>
It is possible to have Regular MB running with only one ball in play.
All other shots you'd expect to be active during regular play are there
as well.  You can shoot jackpots and Super Jackpots forever and ever.
How and when this happens is unknown to me.
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="The_mini-flipper"></a>13.  The mini-flipper</span><P>
The first few times I played ST I quite simply ignored that extra flipper.
Big mistake.  In order to control the ball in ST, you'll need to use it.
For instance, it's possible to drop-catch balls coming from the Recon scoop.
It's also good for slowing down balls coming from the right inlane, and
then catching it with the regular flipper.
<p>
Shoot Bugs with the mini-flipper, or at least try to make the machine
think you did!  It makes advancing through planets so much easier!
<p>
You may notice it's extremely hard (the word "impossible" comes to mind)
to shoot the Arm Nukes hole with the regular right flipper.  While it's
possible to do a bank-shot from the left flipper, try this instead:
Catch the ball on the regular right flipper.  Holding this flipper up,
flip the mini-flipper.  If you're lucky (1 time out of 3 perhaps), the
ball will land in the Arm Nukes hole.  I assume the odds for success
vary from machine to machine.
<p><span class="bodyTitle"><a name="Strategy"></a>14.  Strategy</span><P>
Some thoughts:
<p>
Go for Regular MB and Orbit MB - these can be worth huge amount of
points.
<p>
Wait with the Planet MB;  try to nuke more than two planets at the
same time.  This implies that you at some time shoot for bugs,
which can be a hazardous venture.  Use the mini-flipper!
<p>
Shoot the left and right ramp for Recon Levels.  At level 5, you'll
more often than not be awarded Orbit MB.  Avoid level 6 - Orbit MB
is worth more than 25M!
<p>
My best so far is 656M, best Regular MB is ~150M, best Planet MB ~270M,
best Orbit MB 95M.
