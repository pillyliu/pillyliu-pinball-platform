<h1 id="heading--overview">Gameplay Overview:</h1>
<p>Immerse yourself in the world of The Princess Bride as you join your favorite characters in reliving each iconic scene as Modes and Multiballs, played out before your eyes. Shoot the three shots marked as Castle, Florin, and Guilder to choose different Story Modes.  Collect 6-fingered glove shots to play <a href="#heading--ptdmb"><em><strong>Prepare to Die Multiball</strong></em></a> and spell TRUE LOVE at the side targets to play <a href="#heading--truelove"><em><strong>True Love Multiball</strong></em></a>.</p>
<p>After you master the basics, enjoy exploring the myriad ways to enhance your ability to further progress through the story and increase your scoring via both traditional stacking and thematically interwoven boosts between the Shot Features, Story Modes, Multiballs, and Skill Shots.</p>
<h1 id="heading--playfield">Playfield Walkthrough:</h1>
<p>From left to right:</p>
<ul>
<li>The left side targets (below the upper left flipper) are used to spell out TRUE to advance <a href="#heading--truelove"><em><strong>True Love Multiball</strong></em></a>.</li>
<li>The left ramp (aka CASTLE) is on the extreme left of the playfield and supports launching up through the same ball path through the castle. The ramp returns to the left flipper</li>
<li>The left orbit (aka FLORIN) can capture a ball or pass through and exit to the right orbit. It can serve as a ball launch.</li>
<li>A standup target is in between the orbit and Max’s hut.</li>
<li>Max’s hut shot has a drop target in front of it and captures the ball behind it. It can be a ball launch.</li>
<li>A captive ball is to the right of the Max’s hut.</li>
<li>The center ramp loops behind the Cliffs and returns to the right flipper.</li>
<li>A standup target between the center ramp and the cliffs.</li>
<li>The Cliffs (aka GUILDER) shot is protected by a drop target and has the cliff moving magnet mech behind it. The cliff can serve as a ball capture to the trough, or carry the ball to the top of the cliff based on context.</li>
<li>The side loop (aka INCONCEIVABLE) shot is accessible from the upper left flipper and loops out of the left orbit entrance. It is <em>conceivably</em> possible to repeat this shot from the upper left flipper.</li>
<li>There are two standup targets that cover the entrance to the upper loop shot from the lower flippers.</li>
<li>The right orbit boasts a spinner emblazoned with the Dread Pirate Roberts. It exits via the left orbit entrance.</li>
<li>The right side targets (below the upper right flipper) are used to spell out LOVE to advance <a href="#heading--truelove"><em><strong>True Love Multiball</strong></em></a>.</li>
<li>The inlane/outlanes spell KISS and are rotated by using the upper flipper buttons (by default – can be configured to use the lower flipper buttons or to rotate when the spinner spins)</li>
<li>The Book:  immediately above the flippers on the playfield screen, shows detailed instructions on how to play each active aspect of the game (Modes, Multiballs, and Features) both during the intro video and while playing it, as well as the general status of Character and Mode progress, boosts, and any active <a href="#heading--narrative"><em><strong>Narrative Streak</strong></em></a>.</li>
<li>Tilt Warnings Remaining indicator: shown near the apron in the left outlane.</li>
</ul>

<h1 id="heading--skillshots">Skill Shots (Farm Boy)</h1>
<p>The game has 3 levels of Farm Boy skill shot combos available. Completing each combo sequence awards one use of <a href="#heading--asyouwish"><em><strong>As You Wish</strong></em></a>, which allows for the GUILDER modes to be played as a two-ball multiball. The skillshots must be achieved in order. Once a skill shot sequence is collected, subsequent balls will restart on the last uncollected sequence, wrapping around back to the first skill shot combo after completing the third combo. It is possible to complete and collect multiple skill shot combos in a single ball, with each consecutive skill shot becoming more lucrative.</p>
<p>The skillshots do not immediately turn off if some non-combo minor switches are hit, and it is possible to recover and complete the sequence. Follow the Westley icons at the top of the playfield screen which light the shots, and will turn off when Farm Boy is no longer available.</p>
<ul>
<li>Center Ramp → Center Ramp (Sequence 1)</li>
<li>Center Ramp → Left Orbit → Left Ramp (Sequence 2)</li>
<li>Center Ramp → Left Ramp → Right Orbit → Upper Loop (Sequence 3)</li>
</ul>
<h1 id="heading--characters">Character Progress</h1>
<p>Completion of Modes and collecting Multiball super jackpots awards Character progress for the Westley, Buttercup, Inigo, and Fezzik in the given scene. In order to unlock the final wizard mode, Four White Horses, you must collect 4 segments (adjustable) of each Character’s progress as indicated on the scoreboard displays. The notes below will outline what Character(s) will be earned by completing each mode and multiball.</p>

</div><h1 id="heading--modes">Story Modes</h1>
<p>Modes in The Princess Bride are tied to the three regional shots (CASTLE, FLORIN, GUILDER) and are qualified by hitting the shots a number of times (default settings: 2 for the first mode, 3 for the second+ mode in each region). The final shot will prompt you to pick a mode from that region’s selections.</p>
<p>Each region has 3 modes to select from initially, plus a 4th mini wizard mode to select after the respective three regional modes have been played once. These modes do not need to be completed to unlock the 4th mode.</p>
<ul>
<li>Modes that are not completed can be attempted again, and progress for most modes seem to carry over where you left off.</li>
<li>You may also choose to skip a mode selection if you don’t want to start a Mode from that region at the moment.</li>
<li>Modes may be completed <em>in order</em> of the story to contribute to a <a href="#heading--narrative"><em><strong>Narrative Streak</strong></em></a> bonus scoring (see below).</li>
</ul>

</div><h2 id="heading--castle">Castle Modes</h2>
<p>Castle modes are qualified and started at the left ramp.</p>
<h3 id="heading--nightmare">Buttercup’s Nightmare</h3>
<ul>
<li>Narrative Order: 7 (<a href="#heading--swamp"><em><strong>Fire Swamp</strong></em></a> before, <a href="#heading--blood"><em><strong>There Will Be Blood Tonight</strong></em></a> after)</li>
<li>Character Progress: Buttercup</li>
<li>Boosted by: <a href="#heading--promise"><em><strong>The Promise of a Woman</strong></em></a> feature</li>
<li>Completion Award: <a href="#heading--pit"><em><strong>Pit of Despair</strong></em></a> (or <a href="#heading--pills"><em><strong>Miracle Pill</strong></em></a>)</li>
</ul>
<p>Buttercup’s Nightmare is played as a two-ball multiball. To complete the mode, you must <em>survive</em> the entire time of the mode (~40 seconds) without losing either of the balls. Buttercup’s Nightmare starts with a significant ball saver that covers about the first half of the mode. To make matters more difficult, you are unable to hold any flipper up! Pressing a flipper button will quickly activate then deactivate the flipper.</p>
<p>Points are awarded at lit shots and targets. The upper flippers are active during this mode, so it might be possible to use those to your advantage! It’s also a good strategy to start this mode when <a href="#heading--truelove"><em><strong>True Love Multiball</strong></em></a> is already lit at the Max’s Hut (note: you can’t make <em>progress</em> toward TLMB during this mode), and if you can stack on the additional multiball about halfway through the mode, you should have enough ball save time to return any balls and successfully complete the mode.</p>
<p>Completion of this mode currently also awards <a href="#heading--pit"><em><strong>Pit of Despair</strong></em></a>; or – if Pit of Despair is already qualified – a <a href="#heading--pills"><em><strong>Miracle Pill</strong></em></a>!</p>
<h3 id="heading--mawidge">Mawidge</h3>
<ul>
<li>Narrative Order: 11 (<a href="#heading--max"><em><strong>Miracle Max (Mini Wizard)</strong></em></a> before, <a href="#heading--storming"><em><strong>Storming the Castle</strong></em></a> after)</li>
<li>Character Progress: Buttercup</li>
<li>Boosted by: <a href="#heading--rugenramps"><em><strong>Rugen Ramps</strong></em></a></li>
<li>Compwetion Award: boosts <a href="#heading--truelove"><em><strong>True Love Multiball</strong></em></a></li>
</ul>
<p>This mode is all about whuv. Twoo Whuv. But you want to move Buttercup and Humperdinck away from each other, because he’s not Buttercup’s twoo whuv. Hit indicated shots for Humperdinck and Buttercup to collect a letter in MAWIDGE. After collecting shots, Humperdinck shots will move left and Buttercup shots will move right (excluding the side loop). Hitting indicated Shift Points shots will move points to that side’s next Buttercup or Humperdinck lit shot.</p>
<p>Once six shots are collected, a new short timer begins to complete the mode by hitting the castle shot and collect a hurry-up worth the sum of the mode shots.</p>
<h3 id="heading--storming">Storming the Castle</h3>
<ul>
<li>Narrative Order: 12 (<a href="#heading--mawidge"><em><strong>Mawidge</strong></em></a> before, <a href="#heading--pain"><em><strong>To The Pain (Mini Wizard)</strong></em></a> after)</li>
<li>Character Progress: Inigo, Westley, Fezzik</li>
<li>Boosted by: <a href="#heading--pirate"><em><strong>The Dread Pirate Roberts Spinner</strong></em></a> feature</li>
<li>Completion Award: N/A</li>
</ul>
<p>Prepare to storm the castle by hitting roving scoops. Scoops will pop up from left to right, wrapping back around to the left. Hitting two scoops will increase the speed of the scoop sequence. After 4 made scoops, hit the left ramp to complete the mode.</p>
<h3 id="heading--pain">To The Pain</h3>
<ul>
<li>Narrative Order: 13 (<a href="#heading--storming"><em><strong>Storming the Castle</strong></em></a> before, Four White Horses after)</li>
<li>Character Progress: N/A</li>
<li>Boosted by: N/A</li>
<li>Completion Award: required for Four White Horses</li>
</ul>
<p>Coming to the end of the story and Westley is still feeling the effects of being Mostly Dead, so this mode starts with a longer ball save, and long timer, and very weak flippers. Hitting indicated side target banks will slowly add power to the respective flippers, making it easier/possible to hit shots.</p>
<ul>
<li>Shoot each shot once to complete the mode.</li>
<li>For multiplied scoring, shoot the “X”-marked body part shots in the order that Westley describes them to Humperdinck in To The Pain.</li>
<li>Scoring is also based on number of completed Castle modes.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="heading--florin">Florin Modes</h2>
<p>Florin modes are qualified and started at the left orbit.</p>
<h3 id="heading--eels">Shrieking Eels</h3>
<ul>
<li>Narrative Order: 1 (No modes before, <a href="#heading--cliffs"><em><strong>Cliffs of Insanity</strong></em></a> after)</li>
<li>Character Progress: Buttercup, Fezzik</li>
<li>Boosted by: <a href="#heading--cliffs2x"><em><strong>Cliffs Double Scoring</strong></em></a> feature</li>
<li>Completion Award: immediate access to Cliffs of Insanity</li>
</ul>
<p>Hit shots indicated by the Buttercup icons that are scattered around the playfield to help Buttercup swim away from the eels and reach safety. While there is a shorter initial timer (15 seconds) to hit the shots, each shot adds time to the mode and lights different shots (it is not possible to repeat the same shot back-to-back – except for the side loop – and make progress). Hit five shots to light the escape shot at the Cliffs worth half the sum of your total Buttercup shot values.  You can choose to continue shooting more Buttercup shots to score more points, add more time, and increase your final escape shot value. Later shots add less time.</p>
<p>Scoring for this mode can be <em>doubled</em> by quickly hitting the green-lit side loop one time when the ball is first launched into play.</p>
<h3 id="heading--blood">There Will Be Blood Tonight</h3>
<ul>
<li>Narrative Order: 8 (<a href="#heading--nightmare"><em><strong>Buttercup’s Nightmare</strong></em></a> before, <a href="#heading--guide"><em><strong>Guide My Sword</strong></em></a> after)</li>
<li>Character Progress: Fezzik, Inigo</li>
<li>Boosted by: <a href="#heading--rhyme"><em><strong>Rhyme Time</strong></em></a></li>
<li>Completion Award: easier <a href="#heading--ptdmb"><em><strong>Prepare to Die Multiball</strong></em></a></li>
</ul>
<p>Time to sober up Inigo! TWBBT has two phases, each with distinct timers:</p>
<ul>
<li>Phase 1: Sober up Inigo by hitting alternating ramps. Hitting three ramps will start the next phase.</li>
<li>Phase 2: Shoot the spinner to get The Man in Black to help Inigo plan.  After hitting the spinner enough times (25 total spins), shoot the hut to complete the mode.  After the Hut is lit for the final shot, you may opt to continue shooting the spinner to increase the final shot value.</li>
</ul>
<p>Scoring for this mode can be <em>doubled</em> by quickly hitting the green-lit side loop one time when the ball is first launched into play.</p>
<h3 id="heading--guide">Guide My Sword</h3>
<ul>
<li>Narrative Order: 9 (<a href="#heading--blood"><em><strong>There Will Be Blood Tonight</strong></em></a> before, <a href="#heading--max"><em><strong>Miracle Max (Mini Wizard)</strong></em></a> after)</li>
<li>Character Progress: Fezzik, Inigo</li>
<li>Boosted by: <a href="#heading--swords"><em><strong>Crossing Swords</strong></em></a> feature</li>
<li>Completion Award: Points &amp; satisfaction</li>
<li>Stacking: cannot be stacked with any Multiball nor Feature, except for <a href="#heading--cliffs2x"><em><strong>Cliffs Double Scoring</strong></em></a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>A P3 classic! Time for the walls to come up and to hit the indicated scoop, with a twist! One scoop will be the location you need to shoot (indicated in yellow). <h2 id="heading--narrative">Narrative Streak Scoring</h2>
<p>Completing the Story Modes in the narrative order (as listed in the book on the playfield display or backbox display, or by holding the left yellow button), including wizard and mini-wizard modes, will provide additional points collected in End-of-Ball Bonus as a % of the points scored during the Modes in the Narrative Streak and increased by the streak’s length.</p>
<ul>
<li>It’s worth noting that the streak can start anywhere in the list and continue on from there — it does not need to start with Shrieking Eels, for example.</li>
<li>Additionally, if a mode is failed, the narrative streak can still continue by playing the mode again and completing it before starting any other mode.</li>
<li>Narrative Streak Scoring is multiplied by Bonus X, so playing the modes in order can rack up points quickly.</li>
<li>The second completed mode in a streak scores 25% of that mode’s points in <em>base</em> EOB Bonus. The third completed mode in a streak scores 50% of that mode. The fourth is worth 75%, and the scoring finally caps at 100% for the fifth+ mode.</li>
<li>Any completed portion of a Streak gets awarded during that ball’s EOB Bonus.  A Streak can continue across balls – with the continually incrementing % amount – but only the newly completed mode(s) will be counted in that new ball’s EOB Bonus. For example, if you began your Streak with Buttercup’s Nightmare, you can collect the Streak Bonus from your second mode in the Streak (There Will Be Blood Tonight) on ball 1, and from Guide My Sword and Miracle Max on ball 2.</li>
</ul>

It is possible to score a double super jackpot as well with at least 3 balls in play, by completing the “kiss” ricochet shot with 2 balls held on up-post position!</p>
<p>The number of shots required to get to the super Jackpot phase is controlled by difficulty and past progress. On default settings, the first two Super Jackpot phases begin after just 4 ramp jackpots, and subsequent SJP phases require both the 4 ramp jackpots and 4 target jackpots to respell TRUE LOVE.</p>

<h2 id="heading--ptdmb">Prepare to Die Multiball</h2>
<ul>
<li>Narrative Order: N/A</li>
<li>Character Progress: Inigo</li>
<li>Boosted by: <a href="#heading--inconceivable"><em><strong>Inconceivable Loops</strong></em></a> feature and <a href="#heading--blood"><em><strong>There Will Be Blood Tonight</strong></em></a></li>

Collect all 6 Glove shots and then start at the left ramp. During the multiball, alternate Glove shots and captive ball bashes for jackpots and add-a-balls. The 6th jackpot will begin the Six-Fingered Duel super jackpot phase.</p>
<p><strong>“Let me explain:”</strong><br>
Prepare to Die (henceforth PTDMB) is qualified by first hitting the captive ball during play. Doing so will light a six-fingered glove on playfield shots for collection. Multiple gloves can be spawned at once by hitting the captive ball. Current progress is shown on the banner on the backbox monitor as # shots collected / 6. Collect all 6 gloves and then hit the Castle ramp to start PTDMB.</p>
<p>In PTDMB, the first phase begins with six playfield shots lit with glove emblems and they serve dual purpose during this phase: they can both light a jackpot or build a lit jackpot.  Collect 6 captive ball jackpots to advance to the next phase of the duel. Your built jackpot value continues building across subsequent jackpots.</p>
<ul>
<li>The first Glove collected (“starter” shot) will light a Jackpot at the captive ball and unlight that Glove shot from any further use during the first phase – as a “starter” or as a “build” shot.</li>
<li>Each jackpot is worth the sum of the Glove shots collected for that jackpot, plus the prior jackpot collected.</li>
<li>Each Glove shot can only be used once as the “starter” shot that lights a jackpot*, and Glove shots that have NOT yet been used as a “starter” can be used as a “build” shot on multiple jackpots.
<ul>
<li>*Exception: if you use the Upper Loop as a “build” shot, it will <em>replace</em> the “starter” shot and become that jackpot’s “starter” shot.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Collecting more remaining Glove shots before hitting a lit jackpot at the captive ball increases the jackpot value and unlights those shots as “build” shots.</li>
<li>For the first five jackpots/fingers, if you collect ALL lit Glove shots before collecting the jackpot, your built jackpot will also be DOUBLED.</li>
<li>The 2nd and 4th collected jackpots also award an add-a-ball.</li>
</ul>
<p>Once you have collected 6 jackpots at the captive ball, regardless of value, the 6-ball MB second phase of PTDMB begins. The number of balls in play will be upped to 6 and all six shots will relight as jackpots to collect at 6x, with the X counting down by the number of balls remaining in play. Completing all six shots lights a Super Jackpot at the captive ball, worth the sum of the 2nd phase jackpots and multiplied by the number of balls still in play.  The value of the 6-ball MB phase jackpot is based on your final jackpot of the first phase.</p>
<p>In addition to normal stacking on an eligible already-running mode, PTDMB can be started as a stack simultaneously with a lit Castle mode, making it a good choice for completing the harder-to-complete modes (<a href="#heading--mawidge"><em><strong>Mawidge</strong></em></a>, <a href="#heading--nightmare"><em><strong>Buttercup’s Nightmare</strong></em></a>).  If you have simultaneously started PTDMB with a Castle mode, you will see the 6-fingered glove icon on the Castle mode selection screen.</p>

<h1 id="heading--features">Shot Features and Scoring Boosts</h1>
<p>Shot Features are short, (mostly) timed mini-modes that can run concurrently with most Modes and Multiballs. Timed Features are qualified by repeatedly shooting various shots and run for 30 seconds.</p>
<ul>
<li>The number of shots remaining to begin each Feature is listed next to the respective Feature icon on the scoreboard displays.</li>
<li>Successive Feature plays throughout the game require more shots to qualify.</li>
<li>Progress toward starting Features resets each ball.</li>
<li>Timed Features may not be stacked with each other except for Cliffs Double Scoring, and you cannot make progress toward another Timed Feature with one already running.</li>
<li>Untimed Features are available during other Timed Features.</li>
</ul>

<p>Boosts <a href="#heading--storming"><em><strong>Storming the Castle</strong></em></a></p>
<p>Qualify and start at the right orbit spinner. Different awards, including a scoring boost to Storming the Castle, an <a href="#heading--asyouwish"><em><strong>As You Wish</strong></em></a>, or points, can be awarded by the number of spins in a burst on the spinner. The playfield monitor will show the current number of spins indicated in the upper right, which will reset a second or so after the last spin is counted.</p>
<h3 id="heading--unique">Unique Combos (untimed)</h3>
<p>Boosts <a href="#heading--wits"><em><strong>Battle of Wits</strong></em></a></p>
<p>Hitting any playfield “flow” shot will start a brief combo opportunity comprised of different <em>unique</em> shots in the combo.  The shots that can continue the Unique Combo will be indicated by a red dot over a yellow diamond shot marker at the top of the playfield screen.</p>
<ul>
<li>Completing a 4-way Unique Combo will add +1x scoring boost to your next play of Battle of Wits.</li>
<li>Completing a 5-way Unique Combo – or completing a 4-way Unique Combo three different times – will award a <a href="#heading--pills"><em><strong>Miracle Pill</strong></em></a>.</li>
</ul>
<h1 id="heading--other">Other Gameplay Elements</h1>
<h2 id="heading--pit">Pit of Despair</h2>
<p>The Pit of Despair (PoD) is a way to resurrect your ball/turn (including — yes — tilting out!). You can qualify PoD by hitting the side loop, completing <a href="#heading--nightmare"><em><strong>Buttercup’s Nightmare</strong></em></a>, or via <a href="#heading--mystery"><em><strong>Mystery Award</strong></em></a>. Once qualified, PoD will start when you drain/tilt.</p>
<p>Pit of Despair is a survivor-style multiball and by default, starts as a single-ball mode, and 3 (or 5) shots must be made to save your ball. The first shot is timed, though subsequent shots are not. All successful shots add a ball with no ball-save. If you drain a single ball, you become All Dead, so careful ball management is key.</p>
<p>If you successfully complete the last shot of PoD without losing a ball, you’ll be returned to play as “Mostly Dead.” Be wary, you will lose all your tilt warnings from the ordeal!</p>
<p>If you activate PoD by tilting or if you qualify Pit of Despair a second time after previously completing PoD, Rugen increases your difficulty to Level 50 and it will take 5 shots to complete.</p>
<ul>
<li>First PoD: Center Ramp, Left Orbit, Left Ramp</li>
<li>Second+ PoD (or a PoD activated by tilting): Center Ramp, Left Orbit, Left Ramp, Right Standup Targets, Max’s Hut</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="heading--pills">Miracle Pills (Extra Balls)</h2>
<p>Miracle Pills acts as an “Extra Ball” in TPB. They are awarded by completing the <a href="#heading--max"><em><strong>Miracle Max (Mini Wizard)</strong></em></a> mode and via the other methods described below. A Miracle Pill is consumed on drain of the current ball and immediately returns the ball to play without tallying an End-of-Ball Bonus and without ending any Mode or Feature that is currently running (think of the spatula save in Weird Al and you’ll get the idea).  The resurrected ball will also enjoy a lengthy ball-save.</p>
<ul>
<li>Just like extra balls, Pills can be stacked.</li>
<li>Unlike <a href="#heading--pit"><em><strong>Pit of Despair</strong></em></a>, Pills will <em>NOT</em> activate if you tilt – so don’t tilt!</li>
<li>Miracle Pills available:
<ul>
<li>Complete Miracle Max mini-wizard mode</li>
<li>Complete one selectable Mode from each Region. (Cliffs of Insanity does not count)</li>
<li>Complete a 5-way <a href="#heading--unique"><em><strong>Unique Combo</strong></em></a>, or complete a 4-way Unique Combo three different times.</li>
<li><a href="#heading--mystery"><em><strong>Mystery Award</strong></em></a></li>
<li>Conditionally, by earning a 2nd Pit of Despair on the same ball with one PoD already qualified and not used yet.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Miracle Pill interaction with Pit of Despair:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>When earned, both will show as separate icons between your flippers.</li>
<li>A Pill takes precedence over PoD (you’ll consume the Pill first)</li>
<li>If you tilt with both a Pill and PoD qualified, your PoD will activate:
<ul>
<li>If you successfully survive PoD, you will still have your Pill.</li>
<li>If the Machine kills you in PoD, then you lose your Pill along with your ball, and along with your Bonus.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Miracle Pills in Competition Settings:</strong><br>
If Competition Settings are enabled, each Pill will instead bank a dynamic value of points at the time you earned the Pill, equal to your current score divided by the ball in play:</p>
<ul>
<li>If ball one: Your current score is banked.</li>
<li>If ball two: You bank half of your current score.</li>
<li>If ball three: You bank 1/3rd of your current score.</li>
</ul>
<p>Just like the Miracle Pill in Normal Settings, you won’t actually use your Pill (in this case, score your banked Miracle Pill Points) until you drain your ball – so don’t tilt!  And just like Default Settings, you can resurrect any Pill Points you tilt away <em>if you succeed</em> at a PoD you also have qualified when you tilt.</p>

<li>20-Second Ball Save</li>
<li>Add BonusX</li>
<li>MLT Sandwich (Big Points)</li>
<li><a href="#heading--pit"><em><strong>Pit of Despair</strong></em></a> qualified</li>

<li><a href="#heading--pills"><em><strong>Miracle Pill</strong></em></a></li>
<li>Open All Regions</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="heading--bonusx">Bonus Multiplier</h2>
<p>The bonus multiplier can be increased by completing KISS in the inlanes and outlanes.  Use the white auxiliary buttons to rotate the lit letters (configurable to red primary buttons in settings or to cycle with spinner rotations, if you wish). BonusX via KISS caps out at 5x and subsequent collections of KISS will award points instead of further BonusX. After achieving 3x, rolling through a lit KISS letter will unlight it, making further completions more difficult!</p>
<p>It is possible to achieve a 6x Bonus Multiplier, if Bonus Multiplier is advanced through a mystery award!</p>
<h2 id="heading--bonus">End of Ball Bonus</h2>
<p>Many components comprise the end of ball bonus. Bonus can be multiplied up to 5x by completing the KISS lanes.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="#heading--narrative"><em><strong>Narrative Streak</strong></em></a>
<ul>
<li>Narrative Streak can be lucrative, with mode performance and length of streak factoring in to how many points are awarded</li>
<li>Narrative Streak bonus awarded for any Modes completed during that ball which were part of a Streak.</li>
<li>See <a href="#heading--narrative"><em><strong>Narrative Streak</strong></em></a> section for details on how to achieve this</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Each earned but unused <a href="#heading--asyouwish"><em><strong>As You Wish</strong></em></a> awards 10k</li>
<li>Each character segment of progress earned scores 5k points</li>
<li>Longest Combo chain that ball awards 1k for each shot in the combo</li>
<li>Your longest unique combo that ball awards 10k for each shot in the combo</li>
<li>The number of modes played awards 3k each</li>
</ul>
