<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Disco EM - Stern Electronics | 1977</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/a51c656e-1baf-4860-8474-2c64b791828d-medium.jpg" alt="Disco backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 2</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Build up bonus with rollover discs and spinner; triple it with Disco letters.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Right</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Any stand-up target Disco letter shot has rebound risk. Balls falling randomly from the top can drain down the middle or hit a slingshot. Watch out in particular for balls that hit the rubbers either above the D or below the O in Disco that start going side to side. Spinner shots are safe unless they hit a bumper face-on when they continue, risking a bad kick-down. Shoot what you need but be constantly on guard.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Disco was one of Stern’s first games, before even “Pinball” and Stingray. End-of-Ball bonus is the most important feature, so we’ll cover that first.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Base bonus builds with that top button, the similar button in the center above the flippers, and for every 10 spins of the lit spinner. Which spinner is lit is changed by hitting that advance bonus button in the center. Base bonus goes up to 100,000. To get Double Bonus, hit the Star target between the two top lanes. To get Triple Bonus, get both double bonus and each of the D-I-S-C letters, then hit the Star target again.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The other good point opportunity besides bonus is the rollovers in the chute at the top left. They’re only worth 1000 each to start, but if you hit the three S-C-O stand-up targets on the right side, they increase to 10,000 each. At that point, each shot going through the chute will be worth 50,000. The left return lane will spot you the S target; the right lane will spot the C target. The O target can only be scored by hitting the stand-up. The return lanes also are worth 5,000 each; outlanes are worth 10,000.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Strategy is to get double Bonus a.s.a.p., then get your DISCO letters. Once you have the star lit for Triple bonus, hit it. Then it’s left chute all day.</p></div></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Skill Shot:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">plunge so the ball rolls over the advance bonus button, then drops into the lane for whichever letter you don’t have. If you have neither, go with D.</p></section></div>
