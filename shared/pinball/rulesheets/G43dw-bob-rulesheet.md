<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Scuba EM - Gottlieb | 1970</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/9669ad56-3abf-4a3d-ae82-c084e55d7ebc-medium.jpg" alt="Scuba backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 2</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">UTAD, but in this case, the goal is less to hit bumpers and lanes than to nudge the game so that the ball hits all five stand-up targets below the top lanes. That increases the value of all lanes.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Balls coming directly down from the top often go out of control while those coming down through the 3 middle side lanes are much safer. Avoid hitting the bumpers with anything more than a glancing blow on the edge.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Scuba is an interesting case, but many feel it’s more of a luck-box than most EMs. In this case, rather than collecting a set of lanes to make targets worth more, you’re doing the reverse: collecting sets of targets to make the lanes worth more. Here, the five stand-up targets below the top lanes and largely hidden from the flippers by the three bumpers are the key. Completing all five of them scores 500 points and increases the value of three lanes from 50 to 300. Values hold from ball to ball, so finishing them as early in the game as possible is the key to victory. Hitting them is another matter. Only two are directly shootable from the flippers; the center, right and left targets are behind bumpers. They’re so important, though, that you must forego all else and try. Your best bet is to try to hit the two open targets on their edges so that the ball bounces sideways; the ideal situation is to get the ball zigzagging at an angle between the three bumpers and the five targets to hit as many of them as possible. Just keep shooting the ball up between the bumpers.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Once you have several lanes lit … not much changes. While you can try to shoot the ball up the right- and left-side 50 / 300 lanes from the flippers, I’ve found that’s not easy to do with the corners there, rather than a curved lane bottom. UTAD seems to work well, trying to get all of the lanes lit, then maybe getting the ball to up-and-down some of the top lanes off of the bumpers.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">If you see the ball heading for the top of the left lane on the right side [the one that drops below towards the flippers, try to nudge the ball off the post over to the outer lane that feeds the plunger. That’ll get you lane points plus plunge points. Similarly, on the left side, try to nudge for the outer lane rather than the inner one; the feed from the outer lane is usually cleaner to the left flipper than from the inner lane.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Since the flippers on Scuba are set at such a low angle, you’ll find you have to play this as a flow game. You can almost never get the ball cradled, and if you do, it’s not at a good angle for shooting anyway. Use the Bob Flying Backhand a lot to get the ball up top more easily, i.e. flip to backhand the ball as it’s approaching either flipper from the opposite side of the machine.</p></div></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-orange mb-1">Style Alert:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line italic">Go with the Flow\!</p></section></div>
