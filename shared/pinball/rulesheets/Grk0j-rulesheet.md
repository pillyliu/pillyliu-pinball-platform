<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Sky Kings EM - Bally | 1974</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/a88675dc-1670-4210-9828-f528cacef8fe-medium.jpg" alt="Sky Kings backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 1</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Shoot center saucer or mushroom bumpers to light captive balls lanes, then captive balls all day. Option for UTAD if rebounds from captive balls tend to drain.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Balls falling straight down the middle through the spinner, including after going in the top saucer; balls going around the spinner that fall fairly straight, e.g. just slightly angled off the rubbers to the sides of the bumpers; rebounds from shots at the captive balls and “light lane” mushroom bumpers.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Sky Kings is a mostly-captive-ball game, but with an alternate strategy available.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">If you don’t make the saucer Skill Shot, either UTAD until you get both captive lanes lit or light the captive ball lanes individually by hitting the mushroom bumpers next to them.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Once you have both lanes lit, you have to choose between two primary alternatives: continue UTAD to score 3000 point saucers plus whatever random points you get while the ball is up there, or shoot the lit captive ball lanes to get 1000 to 4000 per shot, depending upon how far up the captive ball goes. There’s rebound drain risk from either strategy, plus brick drain risk, so you need to figure out a few things to help decide:</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">1. **When you hit each captive ball squarely, where does the rebound go?</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">2. **How many points does a square hit get you, i.e. does the captive ball regularly go all the way up and down for the maximum value of 4K?</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">3. **What happens if you miss or hit the captive ball a glancing blow?</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">4. **When the ball comes down from up top, how likely is it to drain vs. come to a flipper or through a return lane?</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">5. **How readily does the ball go in the saucer when put up top?</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">6. **If you miss the top shot, what happens?</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Essentially, you’re doing a risk-reward calculation on the two. If hits to the captive ball fall cleanly into the return lanes or come safely to a flipper, that makes these the best choice. If not, try UTAD through the spinner for the center saucer. On most examples of the game, the captive ball choice is the better of the two, so try it first. YMMV; choose wisely.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The Extra Ball lights come on once you max the bonus [15K]; only one side is lit at a time. Take it if they’re on and you can play them\! The game can be set to have them come on sooner; again, once lit, shoot them whenever possible.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Double bonus is activated on the last ball only.</p></div></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Skill Shot:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">top saucer, which lights both side captive ball lanes and bumpers. Either side is next best, lighting one color or the other. Dribbling through the center without landing in the saucer is bad news, you light nothing.</p></section></div>
