<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Super Orbit SS - Gottlieb | 1983</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/e6500d7f-adf3-4639-b357-cf7eb1855690-medium.jpg" alt="Super Orbit backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">shoot the spinner on the right and the vari-target on the left to increase bonus, then try to go through the top right gate to collect it; repeat.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Left.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Bumper action is the highest risk. Rebounds from the vari-target can center drain or on rare occasions hit the top of the left slingshot mount and bounce out the left outlane. Balls coming down either side lane or the spinner tend to be safe though not a sure thing.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Younger brother of 1971’s Orbit, Super Orbit has pretty much the same rules as the original, just one more scoring digit. He’s the scoop.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The Skill Shot is to plunge the advance bonus lane, the center of the three at the top left. Just watch out, on some games the ball may then go SDTM.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The strategy is to build bonus and collect it as many times as possible during the ball. One way to do this is hitting the vari-target, which advances bonus and scores points; the further back you push it, the better, up to 50,000 plus 5 advances. You also get double bonus if you’re able to get an all-the-way-back level 6 shot to the vari-target; that can be huge, so see if the flippers are strong enough to do it. See where the ball rebounds after you hit the vari-target; you may be able to get the ball back to a cradle on the left flipper after your shot.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The other bonus-building strategy is shooting the right side spinner, which is usually a safer shot than the vari-target when you miss. The ball will continue up into the bumpers, which also give good value of 10,000 when lit [1000 unlit]. Slingshot hits toggle the bumper lights, as well as the top lanes at times.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">To collect your bonus, nudge the ball towards the upper right always-open gate; going through it collects [and resets] bonus and gives you a free plunge.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">If the bumpers are lit, the spinner/bumper option is the better one. I also prefer the spinner when I’m nearing maximum bonus, since shots through it have a chance of bouncing through the collect-bonus gate.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">There’s also a lower gate, opened when the ball goes through the left return lane, so nudge the ball into that when you can. Shots through the upper gate will close the lower gate if it’s open.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Base bonus maxes out at 190,000 and is doubled on the last ball.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Ignore the 30,000-point stand-up target on the right.</p></div></section></div>
