<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Playball EM - Gottlieb | 1971</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/481702bd-8e51-43a9-ba91-906f1aa5875c-medium.jpg" alt="Playball backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 1</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">UTAD towards the two top corner Triples horseshoes.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Besides bumper slams down the middle or into the slingshots, watch out for balls exiting the side “double” lanes center draining, likewise balls rolling down the sides of the vari-target chutes. Rebounds off of the vari-targets are mildly risky, but missing a shot at one is very risky.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">While the game is oriented towards scoring runs more than points, I have seen it in competition, so here it is. If you want points, go up top for the 300-point triples. Balls falling through those should come down to the same (opposite side) flipper for another shot or better, a dead bounce to the other flipper for a controlled mirror shot.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Any time you can get hits on the center bumper when lit or deflect the ball into the side double chutes, do so. The bumpers stay lit once on and may even start the game lit.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Most players should probably ignore the vari-targets; they’re tighter shots than other vari-targets and a missed shot can easily drain. If you can safely forehand or backhand them and push the ball back far enough to score a 5000-point home run, give it a go.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Consistently draining out the sides for 500 (and a home run) can be big in a low-scoring match.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The game has an autoplunger between the flippers, so there’s no skill shot.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">If for any reason you’re playing for runs, each numbered stand-up target puts a runner on that base; these are only worth 10 points each, irrelevant in score play. The strategy is the same.</p></div></section></div>
