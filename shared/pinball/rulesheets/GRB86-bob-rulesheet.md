<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Star Light SS - Williams | 1984</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/d0e81004-d3d6-402e-a222-6ef5d1103b11-medium.jpg" alt="Star Light backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Hit the STAR targets to light the lock on the right, lock the ball, play multiball. During multiball, UTAD.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Left</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Mostly bumper action, though target rebounds have risk, too. Since you need the S-T-A-R target bank to light the lock for multiball, most of your drains will be when you hit a STAR target and the ball rebounds into the bumpers, which then propel it to an outlane, center drain or slingshot.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Star Light is relatively rare, but a lot of fun to play. Multiball is the winning strategy and is easy to start, but can be hard to maintain at times.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Star Light has manual lit-lane change, but uses an extra button above the right flipper for it. On other machines with lane change, one or both flipper buttons do this; here, it’s a separate button positioned where the button for an upper flipper would typically be.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The ten numbers for the top lanes correspond to the ten star rollovers in the center of the playfield, as on Laser Ball. Completing all ten, whether by top lane or rollover, lights a “constellation” for 10K in base bonus. Maximum base bonus is 69K (six constellations plus nine leftover stars).</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The S-T-A-R stand-up targets at the top right light the small lock saucer on the right. The targets are easily hit from either flipper, since cradled backhand shots go straight up at them. You also get 10K for each completed constellation when you finish STAR.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Completing the five LIGHT stand-up targets, two on the left, three in the center, lights your spinner for 1K per spin; a second LIGHT makes the spinner 2K. Each time you score Light, the value for completing it goes up: 5K, 10K, 30K, 50K, 100K. Getting both STAR and LIGHT holds your bonus. If you earn hold bonus on your last ball, rather than get one additional count of your last-ball bonus, you get a timed extra ball of 10 seconds per constellation and 1 second per star.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Bonus multiplier is advanced by hitting whichever of the upper left stand-up target, lower right stand-up target and right orbit is lit. Multiplier goes up to 5X for a total bonus potential of 345K.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Multiball is the strategy here. Once you lock a ball, you plunge the other ball and any switch hit starts multiball. The eject hole itself scores 3K per lit star. During multiball, scores are doubled; go UTAD to get the spinner and more lanes; you’ll get plenty of Light letters and star rollovers by accident. If you have the spinner at 2K from getting Light twice, it’s now 4K per spin in multiball; you can get up to 100K per shot through the spinner then.</p></div></section></div>
