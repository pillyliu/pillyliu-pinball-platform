<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Quicksilver SS - Stern Electronics | 1980</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/b1051e47-38ea-4e21-a7d7-a54879a2ac53-medium.jpg" alt="Quicksilver backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Shortname:</strong> QS</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">UTAD via the right side drop target 3-bank from the left flipper and the spinner from the right flipper.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Top cause is bumper action; watch how balls come down through the right spinner - - fast ones may center drain. Rebounds off the center drop targets are risky. Balls going only part way up the left spinner and then falling back can be dangerous. The right spinner and right drop targets are safe.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Quicksilver tends to be a more “chop wood” game than others; your score correlates with ball time more so than on many other machines of this period.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The top lanes and lettered stand up targets all advance your base bonus up to 75K. Completing the right side drop target 3-bank scores 25K and advances your bonus multiplier towards 5X for a total max of 375K. You can often get all three targets in one shot when the ball sweeps across the faces of them, plus scoring the spinner as the ball continues onward. Shooting that 3-bank to get to 5X is a top priority, and great value if the ball also goes through the spinner.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The center target 4-bank raises the value of the saucer left of it and spots you a letter. Like the right side target bank, you can often get multiple targets down with one sweeping shot, especially from the right flipper.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Each return lane lights the opposite side spinner, but just for a few seconds\! Unlike most spinners, these are set to time out shortly after you light them, rather than staying on. A flow-style player one-time flip of a ball rolling down a return lane through either spinner produces good points. Do so if you’re accurate enough rather than trying to cradle up, since the spinner light may go off before you can make a cradled shot.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Ball control: you may be able to shatz the ball up the opposite return lane, lighting the spinner for a high value shot when the ball rolls back down. This is also useful for switching the ball from the right flipper to the left to shoot at the bonus X 3-bank targets.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Key feeds: the kickout from the saucer - - can you either hold up the left flipper or use a dead bounce to gain control of it?</p></div></section></div>
