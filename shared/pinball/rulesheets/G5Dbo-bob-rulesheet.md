<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Future Spa SS - Bally | 1979</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/ec9f5104-4520-42a9-a9a1-29b417936a55-medium.jpg" alt="Future Spa backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Shortname:</strong> FS</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">shoot the mid-left inline drop targets in the channel to get your bonus to 6X, plus EB if it’s either on or set to award points. Then UTAD.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Mild bias right until you have 6X bonus</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Upper bumper exits to the left are notorious for both left outlane and center drains. Balls that get in between the two lower bumpers easily drain out the gap under them. The inline drop targets have some center drain rebound risk, but less than you might expect. Any missed shot to the right risks going into that deadly bumper-outlane zone. Misses to the left risk an outlane; few missed shots center drain.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">One of the easier games to max out your multiplier once you have the ball cradled. That’s the key, cradling.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Base bonus goes up 2K for each letter you’ve collected in Future Spa. The Future letters are collected through repeated top shots with necessary nudging. The Spa letters are made by hitting the center stand-up target. But don’t shoot at it, since it’s a drain risk; you also get letters from the inlines and the kickback, so just stick with the drops and UTAD and let the spa letters come from random hits and kickbacks. The only time I’d shoot the spa target is if I needed just one more spa letter to complete the 9-letter set.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">When you complete all 9 letters, you light the 18K super bonus, which carries over to later balls. A second set moves you up to 36K, and you can also get 8 more letters for another 16K, for a max base of 52K and max total bonus of 312K. If you finish the letters a third time, you’re awarded a special and the letters reset. If specials are set to score points, it’s worth finishing; if not, stop one shy of your third set.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">You’ll find that you lose most of your balls down the left side on this game. There are four lanes on the left, with the far left one a permanent kickback award [3K, goes up 2K each time collected to a max of 9K], then an outlane, then a may-or-may-not return lane, then a return lane. The either-or lane is managed by a gate, which begins each ball angled to the right, making it an outlane. Skill shooting or otherwise getting the ball through a lit top lane [one of the two center ones] flips the gate straight up to make it a return lane.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The right side top saucer and horseshoe both rise in value each time you make them, the saucer from 3K to 9K in 3K increments, the horseshoe from 2K to 10K in increments of 2K. Both of these values reset each ball. Most of the time, though, you’re better off shooting the spinner to go up top for more letters in Future. Only if you have Future finished would I consider shooting the horseshoe instead. Horseshoe shots exit to the top right bumper, which thumps them somewhat towards that saucer, so you can sometimes get three scores for one shot – the horseshoe rollover plus the bumper value and a saucer.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Nudging advice:</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">When the ball approaches the bottom right bumper pair, try to nudge so that it bounces off a bumper and away towards the center of the machine.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">If the ball settles into the gap under the lower bumpers, try to get it to hop across the gap; even balls partway into the gap can sometimes be side-nudged to hop out of it.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">When the ball approaches the lane dividers on the left, nudge to “walk” the ball across the divider tops either all the way right to the return lane or all the way left to the kicker.</p></div></section></div>
