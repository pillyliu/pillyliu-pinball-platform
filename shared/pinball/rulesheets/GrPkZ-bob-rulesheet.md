<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Sing Along EM - Gottlieb | 1967</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/b0b17400-af2d-4a92-ace6-fcdf7ddfcda0-medium.jpg" alt="Sing Along backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 1</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">hit the center target to light the bumper, then either UTAD or, if able, left saucer all day.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Highest risk is balls exiting the bumper area. Balls falling through the side lanes are risky if their speed doesn’t take them to a flipper, so watch how they roll off or bounce off the rubber at the bottom of the lane. Anything you shoot other than a saucer has rebound risk. Unlike the similarly-four-saucered Kings and Queens, Sing Along has a larger center drain area with two gaps instead of one.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This is the replay version of Melody.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Completing numbers 1-2-3-4 is good [1’s, 2’s and 3’s score 50, the 4’s score 100]; getting lots of saucer collects is better. The saucers kick out from one to another left to right until the right one kicks the ball towards the right flipper. If you can, try to flip from the kickout into the left saucer to repeat the process; if not, UTAD. Ignore the bottom “3” targets. Hit the center 100-point stand-up target exactly once, to light the bumpers; ignore it thereafter unless the ball goes through the 50-point “bumpers off” gate between the upper bumpers. If that happens, go ahead and relight the bumpers with the center target.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">There’s a center post between the two central outlanes; use it and try not to flip then - - if you do and you mis-time your flip, you’ll drain either down the center or under the flipper. If you do get the ball stuck under a flipper [between the tip of the flipper and the larger posts below the tips], try to do a rapid multiple flip to get the ball to bounce off of the large post and back into play.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Saucers score 10 \+ 10 per lit number of that color. If you have all four numbers of a color lit, the hole awards an extra ball, if enabled. For instance, the top left “1” lane, far left central “2” lane, left side “3” stand-up and left “4” outlane, once collected, lights the left hole/saucer.</p></div></section></div>
