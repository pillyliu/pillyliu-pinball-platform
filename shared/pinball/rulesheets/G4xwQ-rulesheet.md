<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Alien Star SS - Gottlieb | 1984</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/01b593ae-65e0-4d1b-b995-f6b287aa56b6-medium.jpg" alt="Alien Star backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Shortname:</strong> AS</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">complete the right side A-L-I-E-N stand-up targets to light the left side lock. Shoot the lock, preferably when lit for 5X [shooting the spinner changes the lock saucer playfield multiplier]. During multiball, shoot the spinner immediately after a ball triggers the right return lane switch.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Left until lock lit, then Right</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Rebounds off the stand-up targets and feeds out of the bumpers after up top shots. Gotta shoot both, though.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Alien Star has a fairly simple rule set, yet it’s fun and challenging to rack up a big score on it. Your first priority is to finish the A-L-I-E-N targets on the right side. These do two things: they activate the ball lock on the left, and they add an award lamp for the top lanes, which rotates between the four. Hitting a specific target that you need is harder than it looks, judging by what I’ve seen in tournament play. Dead bouncing the ball off of the right flipper to get it to the left flipper for a target shot is a very good idea.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Once you’ve got your lock lit, you want to lock the ball, but not necessarily immediately\! Multiball scoring applies a playfield multiplier, but that can be 2X, 3X or 5X. Which multiplier you get cycles with spinner flips on the left. If it’s already on 5X when you activate the lock, lock the ball right away. If it’s on 2X or 3X, shoot the ball up top through the left spinner until it stops with 5X lit, and lock the ball at that point.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Once in multiball, the huge points come from nailing a spinner shot immediately after a ball triggers the right return lane switch. The spinner is lit for 10X value for a few seconds after that switch is hit; getting the ball through the lane and then directly through the spinner can be worth up to 50X base value per spin, i.e. 50,000 points instead of 1000. One precise maximal spinner shot can give you over a million points\!</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Alien letter progress towards lighting the lock, or having the lock lit, carries over from ball to ball.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The right return lane always lights the 10X spinner value timer, so whenever the ball goes through there, you want to shoot the spinner unless you’re about to lock the ball. Shatzing from the left flipper to the right flipper can set you up for a spinner rip, too, if the shatz goes all the way up to trigger the lane switch but not so far that you get an inlane-outlane drain.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The top lanes, once your rotating light is lit, give four awards, none of great value unless extra balls are on: hold bonus, collect bonus, advance bonus X and extra ball. Each completion of Alien adds another award to the rotation. Shots through the right spinner above the Alien targets also advance your bonus X. But since your maximum bonus is 5X and 29K, both the right spinner and those top lane awards pale in comparison to even a good single-ball lit spinner shot.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">If you have the ball cradled on the right flipper without the lock lit, use a post pass or alley pass to transfer to the left, then shoot an Alien letter.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">If you’re playing last and just need a few hundred thousand to win, get the ball to the left flipper, shatz it up the right return lane, then shoot the spinner.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The “N” is the hardest letter to shoot since it’s partially obstructed by the top of the right slingshot.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Players often get overeager once they start multiball and miss the spinner shot to the right by flipping early. Stay calm.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Key Feed: how the ball comes out of the lock saucer; you may need to nudge the machine to avoid having it clip the top of the left slingshot and rattle into the left outlane. This can be the most important aspect of the game - - getting control of the ball ejected from the saucer when starting multiball. Do so and you have a good chance to set up and collect a 20K, 30K or 50K per-spin shot; fail to do so and you might miss out on a chance to win $15,000. (See Indisc 2023 High Stakes final game on twitch/iepinball.)</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Tournament Format Note: in match play or head to head, consider just shatzing the right return lane to light the spinner, then shoot the spinner; don’t worry about multiball (but take it if you light it regardless of which multiplier is lit). A few good lit spinner shots will be enough to win many games. In best-game play, always go for 5X multiball, you’ll need it to have any chance at a top-10 score.</p></div></section></div>
