<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Sorcerer SS - Williams | 1985</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/8016de3e-c263-48d5-b77b-826a9748738e-medium.jpg" alt="Sorcerer backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Multiball lock shot until you start multiball and have your playfield multiplier at 5X. After that, UTAD except when spinners are lit, then shoot the spinners.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Right</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">The majority of your out-of-control balls result from balls grazing the outer side of the ramp, the outer right side of the left spinner or falling just right of the upper flipper. Any failed ramp shots, missed spinner shots or rebounds off the center stand-ups produce risk, of course.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">I used to play this at Station Break in Penn Station when passing through New York en route to other cities on the east coast. They never had one at the arcades near me, so I made the most of it; one quarter usually lasted until my train connection was due.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Sorcerer is seriously all about multiball. It just takes two left ramp shots to start it, in theory. In practice, some Sorcerer lock saucers are flaky and the ball can skip through the lock and go out the gate below it. Be prepared if you’re playing one of those.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">During multiball, playfield scoring is doubled; shoot the lock ramp again to increase your playfield multiplier from 2X to 3X, again for 5X. Once your multiplier is maxed, UTAD. If at some point you complete the letters in S-O-R-C-E-R-O-R, which lights the spinners for 2500 per spin, rip them when in multiball\! At 5X, that’s 12.5K per spin.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Locks can be stolen, and often are; keep that in mind.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Bonus goes up to 50K, earned by lanes and targets. Bonus multiplier starts at 1X and goes to 2X, 4X, 6X, 8X as you finish sets of top A-B-C-D lanes. Maximum bonus is 400K.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Completing the upper right 3-bank lights a return lane for three bonus advances, scores the lit point value, and awards hold bonus if lit; the targets are on a timer and will reset if not completed before it runs out.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Don’t bother shooting at the Sorcerer letters in single ball play; they’re drain risks. If you need any to light your spinners, wait until you’re in multiball to deal with it. Even then, I wouldn’t bother unless you just need one or two.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">A center post is standard but is usually removed or de-rubbered for competition.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">A recent modification to multiball has become common in tournament play. After your first multiball, you must complete the three drop targets above the upper flipper before the timer on them runs out to re-enable a lock.</p></div></section></div>
