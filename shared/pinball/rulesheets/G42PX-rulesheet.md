<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Space Invaders SS - Bally | 1980</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/56787dce-cc1f-42ab-8900-900ccf5871e5-medium.jpg" alt="Space Invaders backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">center horseshoe all day, if you can hit it; UTAD if you can’t. Once your bonus is high, shoot the right side drop target, then shoot through the gap where it was.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Mild bias Left</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Lots of balls drain when leaving the bumper area three easy ways: direct center drains; falling onto the top of a slingshot mount or inlane divider next to it and rebounding into the outlane; and falling onto a slingshot, leading to a drain. Missed shots lead to more drains than usual, too for multiple reasons: shots with the upper mini-flippers tend to be less accurate than normal; shots with the main flipper are mostly very far away and easy to not hit perfectly; and shots at the horseshoe that miss by even a small amount will hit the posts guarding it. The lower flippers are a bit less crisp-feeling to begin with, which doesn’t make it any easier.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Yes, a pinball machine based on a video game. It was 1980, video games and Space Invaders in particular were the Big New Thing, so it was inevitable. While the machine does have the “bump-bump-bump” sound effects of the video game programmed in, the backglass art owes more to the movie Alien than to the video game.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This game is mostly about bonus, although a long enough ball will net you tons of points with spinner shots and various targets around the game.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Space Invaders has two separate bonuses to build up. The normal bonus is advanced by lanes, targets and the horseshoe shot, and goes up to 39K. If you get it to 20K or more, the 20K portion is held to future balls, if any. The horseshoe shot is used to advance the bonus multiplier, up to 5X. Total normal maximum bonus is therefore 195K. The upper miniflippers have a better angle on the horseshoe shot, but they’re a bit harder to be precise with. If you can hit the shot with them, fine, if not, use them for ball control, i.e. to deflect, roll or transfer the ball safely to a lower flipper and shoot with that. Once your bonus is at 5X, the horseshoe shot is still worth making since it’s then worth 25K.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Bonus two is built up by completing the five red invaders stand-up targets, four below the top lanes and one at the game center. Each set earns 20K up to 60K max and is held for future balls. The multiplier applies to this, too, for another potential 300K in bonus.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The captive ball scores 10K for the first hit, then goes up 10K per hit, topping out at 50K. Decent points, but a risky shot. Getting all three top lane blue invaders lights the top center lane to score the “Clone Chamber Value,” i.e. the current captive ball award.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Getting all five blue invaders [three top lanes plus two upper flipper return lanes] lights EBs in the main flipper return lanes and lights the center stand-up for 5K and 3 bonus advances.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The drop target with a pass-through behind it on the right side halfway up the game is important. When it’s blinking and beeping, it’s worth 50K bonus or for the targets, as indicated. Shots through the gap behind it will roll over a collect bonus switch and then return to the plunger. Your collected bonus is not reset, so this can be very valuable; once your bonus multiplier is maxed, shooting the drop target and then the gap is the highest-scoring strategy.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Trivia note: this is the game used in the Captain Marvel movie, including the fight scene when one of the “baddies” picks it up and attempts to smash her with it.</p></div></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Skill Shot:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">make the center lane at the top; this lights the spinner on the right and scores 10K for the captive ball chamber.</p></section></div>
