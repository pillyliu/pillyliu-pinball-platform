<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Rawhide EM - Chicago Coin | 1977</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/26e86264-0d76-4c7c-ba15-7ab6fc737960-medium.jpg" alt="Rawhide backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">UTAD via the spinner.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Left</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Occasional direct drains from the upper playfield area, but usually drains come from slingshot action; balls falling from above go into the slingshots often. The two stand-up targets are high-risk, make or miss, with frequent side drains or going into slingshot action.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Rawhide came out just as Chicago Coin was being sold to Stern; you might see it under either company’s name.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Bonus is built by the return lanes, outlanes and the three small rollover buttons, the one at the very top and two others a few inches above the flippers. Base bonus goes up to 19K. Getting double bonus involves a bit of luck since you need to have “lites double bonus” on when you make either saucer shot. The catch is, any shot to change the saucer value may rebound and change it again before you get the ball back on a flipper.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The two lower standup targets are worth 500 unlit, 5000 lit. Exactly one is lit at all times; which one cycles on switch hits.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The strategy here is up top via the spinner until you collect each of the different saucer values at the top hole. Keep that up until your bonus is maximized. Even then, stay with it since one good lit spinner shot plus random points up top is worth far more than the captive ball or any of the stand-up targets.</p></div></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Skill Shot:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">get the ball in the top saucer. Ideally, you want it lit for double bonus when it goes in, so you may want the ball to hit the top button once or twice first to change the saucer value. As you’ll see by the lights below the lower (center) saucer, they can also award lighting the spinners and bumpers, lighting the saucers for 5K or lighting one outlane for an extra ball lane if enabled.</p></section></div>
