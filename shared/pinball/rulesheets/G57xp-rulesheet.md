When the ball is active around the lower bumper, do not flip unless you can clearly move the ball higher\! You risk having it drain underneath the flipper otherwise.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This is the add-a-ball version of Magic City. They didn’t even bother to change the letters from “CITY” to “TOWN” on the playfield.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">While I don’t think much of this game, I have seen it used in competition a few times, so here’s a quick summary. Skill Shot is to hit the top “advance and on bumpers” rollover, then drop down into the lane for a M-A-G-I-C letter you don’t yet have. Then it’s UTAD; you want to try to nudge the ball off the top of the three upper bumpers so that it goes up one of the MAGIC letters and then down another one. There are two reasons for this: first, those magic lanes are worth 100 points each, the highest value on the game. Second, once you finish MAGIC, you want to hit the center stand-up target to get the star. Getting the star lights the upper blue bumper for 100, which then becomes your top UTAD goal. You can try shooting at the center target right away to advance your magic letters towards the star, but rebounds and misses can be dangerous; exercise caution on that approach.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">C-I-T-Y is useless other than for replays and extra balls, which will be turned off.</p></div></section></div>
