<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Paragon SS - Bally | 1979</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/4730d2c7-6bbe-4273-ae8f-2fc4f4149eba-medium.jpg" alt="Paragon backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Shortname:</strong> PG</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Shoot the left side inline drop targets until you’ve scored the EB (or the points for it). Now shoot the spinner until you have at least 20K in bonus. At that point, continue either one. If the spinner is extremely active, reverse the order and do the spinner first.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Right</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Mostly bumper action, though the rebounds from the inline drop targets and the exit from the “waterfall” shot on the far right can center drain. One other common drain is top right bumper to mini-rubber just right of the Paragon saucer to a center drain.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Paragon is one of the most popular solid state games ever made. It’s somewhat bonus-heavy, but there are a lot of points to be had other ways, too. Bonus maxes at 5X and 49K for 245,000; if you get the bonus all the way up to 49, listen for the special high-pitched tone as the game finishes scoring your bonus.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">EBs and Specials can be important. If set for points, EBs are 25K, specials 50K. EBs come only from the saucer behind the left side inlines; the first shot to it gets your 5X multiplier, the second gives you EB. There are several ways to score a special: finish the PARAGON letters [worth 25K plus a special]; finish the upper right side drop target bank 4 times on one ball; finish those same drops 5 times during the course of the whole game and then score a waterfall shot behind them.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Drop catches, bounce passes, etc. all can be used to try to get the ball under control. The double right flipper means the ball can only be cradled on the right side of the upper flipper. If the ball is on or coming to the right flipper, you have two options: shoot the in-line drop targets or the spinner just right of them. The best strategy depends on several things: how juicy is the spinner? Does the ball continue through the spinner and onward safely into the right side waterfall lane afterwards? How likely are shots to the inline targets to drain? And what is your current bonus situation, including how close you are to reaching the 20K “super bonus” [or 30K or 40K]? For juicy spinners and safe follow-throughs, shoot the spinner until the bonus reaches 20,000, at which point that value is held for the rest of the game. If you’re close to 20K, shoot the spinner even if it’s not juiced just to get to 20. Otherwise, shoot the drop targets to increase your bonus X and, eventually, score an extra ball or alternative points in lieu of one. Then get the base bonus to 20K or more later.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">If you have the ball on the left flipper and are uncomfortable with attempting to transfer it to the right flipper, shoot at the drop targets in front of the waterfall curve. These award points, and the value each time you complete a set goes up - - 10K, 15K, 20K, 25K, Special \= 50K. Completing them also raises the value of the ball going through the waterfall. If you’ve already raised the value of the target bank to 25K or special, don’t bother with a transfer, shoot the drops\! Shots at these targets should continue up into the bumpers where you might be able to nudge them into one of the top saucers for additional points.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Key Feeds - a lot of these</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">1. **rebounds from the left inline drops - down the middle or where you can get control with a flipper, usually a live catch on the left flipper?</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">2. **the exit from the right side waterfall lane - does it land on a flipper? Which one? Does it graze the slingshot above the right flippers? Wherever it goes, can you get it cradled somehow, or must you shoot on the fly? I’ve seen many variations: on-the-fly-only with the right flipper, dead bounce from left flipper to right, tip from right flipper to left, and ones where you must wing it each time depending upon how it hits the slingshot.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">3. **the kickouts from both top saucers, with the intent of finding a way to nudge a bit to keep the ball up there and maybe hit that center stand-up target for a few bonus advances, or have the ball pop back up into either saucer again.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The “Beast Lair” [lower left]: you’ll lose a fair amount of balls here; many can be saved but learning the nudging here is among the most advanced there is on any game. It’s hard to describe how to do it, it just takes a lot of practice.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Don’t be shy about using the upper left miniflipper\! While you can drain out the right side if you miss-time a flip with it, it’s very useful for keeping the ball up top and away from the Beast Lair. Aim your shots with it between the left and center bumpers.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Sometimes after you clear the four inline drops, your shot towards the 5X bonus / Extra Ball saucer behind them will be rejected. Don’t let that get to you; just try again. If it happens a lot, try adjusting the speed of your shots a bit, e.g. slower.</p></div></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Skill Shot:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">it depends on game settings. If the PARAGON letters are awarded sequentially, plunge for the Paragon saucer. If not, plunge for the left saucer [“Golden Cliffs”]. The left saucer value starts at 2K and goes up 2K per shot until reaching 20K, where it stays. The saucer value holds across balls for the entire game.</p></section></div>
