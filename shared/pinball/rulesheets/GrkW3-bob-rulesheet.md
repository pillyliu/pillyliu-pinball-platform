<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Voltan Escapes Cosmic Doom SS - Bally | 1978</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/d520c725-9d72-4f4c-9c43-c14468fc9762-medium.jpg" alt="Voltan Escapes Cosmic Doom backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">shatz the \#7 and \#3 return lanes if possible, then UTAD through the spinners; shoot the \#2, \#5 and \#8 targets if they’re the only ones you need.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Only the spinners are safe; bumper exits and rebounds from the stand-up targets can all drain or go into the slingshots. Note that the \#5 target is offset slightly right of dead center.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">A poor choice for tournament play, but I’ve seen it used, so I include it.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This game is bonus-heavy, unfortunately, given its layout. Bonus is built up by completing “planets” 1 through 9: the four top lanes, lower three stand-up targets and the two return lanes. End of ball bonus is 2K per planet. If you finish the planets, it holds 18K the first time as your “Jumbo” bonus and 36K the second time as your “Super” bonus, letting you get up to three sets of planets. Bonus multiplier is either 1X or 3X, rather unusual. Get 3X by completing A-B-C-D; A and B are the upper stand-up targets, C is either \#4 top lane or \#7 in the left return lane, D is either the \#6 top lane or \#3 in the right return lane.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The return lanes, \#7 and \#3, are the catch. You need to either be lucky or able to shatz them in order to get more than 42K in bonus. (42K is 14K for 7 planets \#’s 1,2,4,5,6,8,9 times 3X for ABCD.) But if you finish the planets three times and have 3X, your bonus is 162K. That’s a huge difference, and largely luck-driven due to the return lanes. That’s why I’m not a fan of this as a competition game.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Getting the 1-3-7-9 planets light the two outlanes for 50K, making the machine’s luck-boxness even worse. If you get the return lanes on ball one, you can get up to 150K from outlane drains. If you never get them, you get 3K for three outlane drains.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Finishing the four top lanes 1-4-6-9 raises their value to 5K each thereafter. Getting A and B lights the spinner.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Strategy, such as it is, is UTAD through the spinners, lit or not, and only shoot at the \#2, \#5 or \#8 targets when they’re all you need to complete a set of planets. Shatz \#3 and \#7 if you can, but if you’re not good at shatzing, stick with UTAD.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Bottom line if you have to play it is pray you can safely shatz the two return lanes, otherwise you’ll likely have to be luckier than your opponents to win.</p></div></section></div>
