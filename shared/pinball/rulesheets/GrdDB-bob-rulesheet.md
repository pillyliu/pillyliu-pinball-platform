<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Galaxy SS - Stern Electronics | 1980</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/ed4e7675-b2e5-4a6b-8314-9e1a0c09a238-medium.jpg" alt="Galaxy backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">spinner all day from the left flipper, drop target bank all day from the right flipper.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Right</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Rebound risk from the necessary drop targets on the left; unlucky feeds out of the bumpers after spinner shots. Watch how shots into the Galaxy collect chute exit towards left side return lanes: do they fall cleanly into a return lane or hit a return lane divider? If they tend to hit a divider, plan to nudge since balls can bounce off of that divider into the left outlane or down the middle.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Pretty simple: from the right flipper, shoot the left drop targets all day to score planets until the bonus multiplier is at 5X. One set gives 2X, two gives 3X, four gives 4X, six gives 5X. You get planet value awards along the way, too, 10K the first time and increasing by 5K each time up to 40K for Pluto.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Once bonus X is maxed, or at any time earlier when you have the ball cradled and the center spinner is lit, shoot the spinner. At 1000 per spin, it’s worth it, since it also gets the ball up top to do some point damage while there.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Your base bonus is built up from various lanes and the star rollovers; don’t worry about actively trying to build it, just keep shooting drops and up top and it will come.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The Galaxy Lane at the upper left is generally not worth shooting unless you have most of the Galaxy letters; its value is 2K plus 3K per letter. I do find, though, that it’s often useful to shoot it as a ball control strategy: if you’re unable to get to a cradle and or want to take a brief pause, shooting the galaxy lane gives you a chance to refocus yourself while the ball is in the chute. It usually comes out in a controllable manner, and you can then figure out what you want to do next in a more relaxed manner.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The Galaxy letter lanes are mildly useful, but not worth actually taking shots at.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The top saucer is also mildly useful, so try to Skill Shot into it on the plunge. I have an amusing anecdote regarding this saucer. I once played a Galaxy where after I made the skill shot into the saucer, the kickout from the saucer had just enough energy to hit the bumper to the right of it and then loop to the left back into the saucer. The kickout-loop then repeated. The game kept this up for more than five minutes before the kicker weakened enough to break the loop; the score was nearly a million at that point, and I had yet to flip\! After taking some video of the quirk, I turned the game off and alerted the owner so he could fix it before something burned out.</p></div></section></div>
