<p><b>Overview</b></p>
<p>It is easy to have fun while
playing this game. It is a good Family Game
Room addition. Comet is the hit of every
party I have. It has a fun and fairly
easy Rule Set and can be enjoyed by just about everyone, although drinking
moderately seems to help one's gratification with playing the game and its
associated frustrations. </p>
<p><b>NOTE:</b> These rules are compiled from hours of playing of
&quot;Comet&quot; and are as accurate as possible to the author's best
knowledge.</p>

to this version</b></p>

<p><b>Comet
General Description</b></p>
<p>The Comet was
manufactured by Williams Electronics (System 9), in June 1985 (1985-06)
with a model designation of 540.</p>
<UL>
<LI> Main CPU: M6808 @ 1 MHz
<LI> Sound CPU: M6808 @ 1 MHz
<LI> Sound chips: DAC, HC55516
</UL>
<UL>
<LI> Designed by: Barry Oursler (Comet is Barry's 17th design effort at Williams)
<LI> Art by:  Python Anghelo (Comet is Python's 1st art effort at Williams)
<LI> Software by:  Bill Pfutzenreuter (Comet is Bill's 2nd S/W effort at Williams)
</UL>
<p>Comet is a 4 Player,
Solid-State (SS) Electronic pinball machine of which 8,100 units were produced. Its theme is Happiness/Circus/Carnival. It has 2 flippers, 3 Pop Bumpers, 1-drop
target, 3 Ramps, 2 Eject Holes, a 4 Duck target bank and a 4 Rabbit target bank.</p>
<p>According to the book
Arcade Treasures, 'Comet' was named after a popular ride at Chicago's
now-closed Riverview Amusement Park. It was
the first solid-state machine to feature a one million point shot. </p>
<P>Python Anghelo has said that he thinks that Comet is one of his best efforts.</P>
<p>The voice announcing &quot;One Million!&quot;
is said to belong to Steve Kordek. Kordek became something of a figurehead for pinball having
designed almost 100 games at Genco and then at Williams where he
headed-up the pinball design section. Since the closure of the Williams pinball
division, he has been working on archiving the large number of historic
documents and designs.</p>
<p><b>STARTING
THE GAME</b></p>
<p>One to four players can
play. New players can enter the game by pressing START until the end of the
ball of the last player. After that, pressing START aborts the current game for
all players and begins a new one. </p>
<p><b>OBJECT
OF THE GAME, SCORE 1,000,000</b></p>
<p>One object is to light
the 1-9-8-6 rollover targets on the last ball (the 3<sup>rd</sup> or 5<sup>th</sup>
ball, depending on your set up) and shoot the ball up the Cycle Jump, while it's
lit, and land in the Upper Ring scoring 1,000,000 points.</p>
<p><b>THE
BALL DIDN'T REACH THE TOP DURING LAUNCH</b></p>
<p>A launched ball that
scores no points before draining will be re-served. This can only happen when the ball doesn't
reach the top when launched and drains back onto the playfield.</p>
<p><b>PLAYFIELD LAYOUT</b></p>
<p>Starting from the lower
playfield and working up here is a description of the Playfield and Game:</p>
<p><b>Flippers:</b> There
are 2 of them, located just above the Outhole. There is a small post with a
rubber O-Ring in between the flippers.
There is a "Ride Again" light at the Outhole.</p>
<p><b>Left Hand Return lane:</b> It
is normal size, for games of that time. The left return lane lights the Cycle
Jump Ramp. There is no right hand
Return Lane.</p>
<p><b>Outlanes:</b> Both of
the Outlanes have &quot;SPECIAL&quot; lights on them. The Left Outlane advances the Duck bonus by 3
and the Right Outlane advances the Rabbit bonus by 3. The left Outlane can also return the ball to
the playfield if the ball hits the Outlane bumper in the right place, English
helps here. Both Outlanes activate the
Train Whistle when the ball drains... you'll learn to hate that sound.</p>
<p><b>Duck targets:</b> Just above the left outlane are 4 Duck targets
and hitting all four lights the Corkscrew. </p>
<p><b>Rabbit targets: </b>Just above the right outlane are 4 Rabbit targets
and hitting all four lights the Funhouse.</p>
<p><b>Comet ramp:</b> Right in
the middle of the playfield is a ramp. This is the Comet ramp, and it returns
the ball to the lower right playfield. You can re-light it, activating larger
scoring potential, by hitting all of both the Duck &amp; Rabbit targets to the
left and right of the playfield. If you do not hit the ball hard enough to make
it all the way around, it will fall back down onto the center of the playfield
and sometimes even SDTM (Straight Down the Middle). </p>
<p><b>Funhouse:</b> Just to
the right of the Comet ramp, there is an Eject hole, called the Funhouse.</p>
<p><b>Cycle Jump Ramp:</b> In the
upper right corner of the playfield, above the Funhouse, is the Cycle ramp. </p>
<p><b>Pop Bumpers:</b> 3 of
them, just below the top rollover lanes and behind the Comet ramp. </p>
<p><b>Corkscrew Ramp:</b> In the
upper left corner of the playfield is the Corkscrew ramp.</p>
<p><b>TOP LANES 1-9-8-6: </b>At the top center are
the 1-9-8-6 rollover lanes.</p>
<p><b>Backglass:</b> Comet
has nice artwork. There is a roller coaster (The Comet) with John Belushi in
the back seat and Jane Curtin in the second row on the left; well it sure looks
like them!</p>
<p><b>Sound:</b> This game has average
sound effects for it's time. The game has some speech too. The attract
mode is very well synchronized, in terms of sound, and lights.</p>
<p><b>Displays:</b> Comet
was back in the time of alphanumeric displays. The highest score that the high
score table will hold is 9,999,999. </p>
<p><b>Miscellaneous:</b> Comet
has many options that can be adjusted by the operator, including the length of
certain timers. With that in mind, the settings on the machine you choose to play
may vary and for that reason, I did not put many of the timer values in here; most
of the features time-out in 15 to 20 seconds.
The game's electronic adjustments are detailed in the Owners Manual on
pages 6 &amp; 7.</p>
<p><b>PLAYFIELD
ADJUSTMENTS</b></p>
<p>The playfield has a few physical adjustments that
change the difficulty of game play: playfield pitch, playfield level &amp; post
position. While these are discussed on
pages 8 &amp; 9 of the Owners Manual, I'll highlight them here.</p>
<p>Playfield Pitch &amp; Level are set by the front and
rear leg levelers on the bottom of the machines legs. The nominal pitch (front to back) setting
should be about 6 degrees. The playfield should be level (side to side) when
measured at both the top and bottom areas of the playfield.</p>
<p>There are 5 posts at the Return lane and the
Outlanes that can be adjusted. These
adjustments make the game play "conservative" (easier to lose the ball) to
"liberal" (more difficult to lose the ball).
In general, opening up the Return lane makes the game more liberal
(harder to lose the ball) and opening up the Outlanes makes the game more
conservative (easier to lose the ball).
These adjustments are detailed on page 8 of the Owners Manual.</p>
<p><b>GAME
PLAY</b></p>
<p>One should start out
with the skill shot, shooting the ball into the area above the rollover lane
and allowing it to pass thru one of the lanes. From there, the ball goes into
the Pop Bumpers, and can come out either side of the Comet ramp. If you shoot the ball too hard, the ball will
just exit the rollover lanes area, via the Corkscrew, and head towards the
flippers. </p>
<p><b>TOP LANES 1-9-8-6: </b>Making (lighting) the
1-9-8-6 at the rollover lanes...
scores 50,000 and lights the playfield multiplier
for a 20-second interval (x2, x3 x5).
Rolling over the lanes to light them scores 1000.</p>
<p>The following Playfield
Multiplier (Red playfield light)
scoring can occur, if you light all of the Rollover lanes on that
ball (5-ball setting):</p>
<p>1<sup>st</sup>
Ball: 2x; 2<sup>nd</sup> Ball: 3x; 3<sup>rd</sup> Ball: 2x; 4<sup>th</sup>
Ball: 2x; 5<sup>th</sup> Ball: 5x</p>
<p>The following Playfield
Multiplier (Red playfield light)
scoring can occur, if you light all of the Rollover lanes on that
ball (3-ball setting):</p>
<p>1<sup>st</sup>
Ball: 2x; 2<sup>nd</sup> Ball: 3x; 3<sup>rd</sup> Ball: 5x</p>
<p>The right flipper button
changes which of the 1-9-8-6 are lit (Lane Change) and its use is key to
lighting all of 1-9-8-6.</p>
<p><b>DUNK THE DUMMY:</b> Hitting
the "DUMMY" drop target exposes the bull's-eye for a timed interval. Hitting bull's-eye, before the drop target
resets, advances bonus multiplier (Green playfield light):
1<sup>st</sup> time: 2x; 2<sup>nd</sup> time: 3x, 3<sup>rd</sup> time:
4x; 4<sup>th</sup> time: 5x... thereafter, hits score 20,000 for each additional
time. Hitting the "DUMMY" activates a
game voice.</p>
<p><b>Duck Target Bank:</b> Making
the 4 Duck Bank... lights CORKSCREW. </p>
<p><b>CORKSCREW:</b> Ride the
Corkscrew to collect the DUCK Bonus, an EXTRA BALL or SPECIAL, when lit.</p>
<p><b>Rabbit Target Bank:</b> Making
the 4 Rabbit Bank... lights FUNHOUSE.</p>
<p><b>FUNHOUSE:</b> Drop
into the Funhouse (Eject hole) to collect the RABBIT Bonus, when lit.</p>
<p><b>COMET RIDE:</b>
Completing BOTH the Duck &amp; Rabbit Banks... activates (lights) the COMET
ramp for higher scores, EXTRA BALLS and SPECIALS. "Riding" the COMET scores 10,000 when not
lit. When lit, it scores 30,000 and then
advances scoring to 50,000. The 2<sup>nd</sup>
COMET ride scores 50,000, then advances scoring to 100,000. The 3<sup>rd</sup> COMET ride scores 100,000
and some playfield EXTRA BALL. The 4<sup>th</sup>
COMET ride also scores 100,000 and some playfield SPECIAL. Each scoring advance must be achieved within
its time limit, or the scoring advance resets either back to 30,000 or if the 1<sup>st</sup>
100,000 has been scored, the lights go out.
Also, completing all 4 "Rides" resets it, and turns off the lights. BOTH the Duck &amp; Rabbit Banks must be
re-completed to re-light the Comet Ride.</p>
<p><b>CYCLE JUMP &amp; Playfield Multiplier:</b> Landing in the Lower Ring scores 10,000 (or 20,000
when lit). Landing in the Center Ring
scores 20,000 (or 50,000 when lit).
Landing in the Upper Ring scores 30,000 (or 200,000 when lit). Passage thru the LEFT Flipper return lights
all three rings. On the last ball, if
the 5x Playfield Multiplier is lit, landing in the Upper Ring scores 1,000,000. On the last ball with the Ramp lit and the
1-9-8-6 rollover lanes activated, until the 5x bonus times out the machine
shouts "A Million". Once the ball goes
up the Ramp into the eject hole, or goes into its eject hole from a Pop Bumper
(it's just to the right of the lower right Pop Bumper), the Jump is reset and
the ball must go thru the left return lane to re-light the Cycle Jump Ramp.</p>
<p><b>CYCLE JUMP &amp; Extra Ball:</b> Completing BOTH the Duck &amp; Rabbit Banks a
2<sup>nd</sup> time... lights more playfield EXTRA Ball's &amp; SPECIAL's and also
activates the Cycle Jump for EXTRA BALL action:
(A) Lights Lower Cycle Jump Ring for 5 seconds; (B) Then, lights Center
Ring for 10 seconds; (C) Finally, The Upper Ring lights for 15 seconds. If the EXTRA BALL is not made within the specified
time(s), the sequence starts over until an EXTRA BALL is made. Then BOTH the Duck &amp; Rabbit Banks must be
re-completed to re-light the EXTRA BALL.</p>
<p><b>CORKSCREW &amp; Special:
</b>Completing
BOTH the Duck &amp; Rabbit Banks a 3<sup>rd</sup> time- lights more playfield
EXTRA Ball's &amp; SPECIAL's. "Ride" the
Corkscrew to score the SPECIAL or the EXTRA BALL, when lit.</p>
<p><b>General Playfield Points:</b> The
following is in addition to the scoring shown above. The Left
 Return Lane scores 5,000. The left and right Outlanes score 10,000. The left Outlane scores the Duck Bonus and
the right Outlane scores the Rabbit Bonus.
Hitting the Ducks and Rabbits scores 2,000 the first time and 1,000
after that. The Funhouse scores 10,000,
the COMET ramp (unlit) 10,000. The
Corkscrew scores 20,000. The 3 Pop Bumpers
score 1000 per activation.</p>

<p><b>Other Sounds</b></p>
<p>The game plays this
addictive music that gets stuck in your head!</p>
<p>When a ball has scored a large amount of points, the machine
awards you with flashing backglass lights and the sounds of fireworks;
and if you score the 1,000,000 point shot, the machine also flashes
all the machine's lights and makes more fireworks and roller coaster
sounds.</p>
<p>The train whistle (left &amp; right Outlanes) and the Cycle Jump Ramp
drum roll are equally annoyingly cool sounds! Each player
also has different music for their 1<sup>st</sup> ball.</p>
<p>Comet also makes noise
in Attract Mode- and it can be annoying in a quiet house, when the machine yells
&quot;Hey Turkey&quot;.</p>
