<p>
Enough preamble, on with playfield layout, clockwise from the flippers:
<p>
<dl>
<dt>     Flippers<dd>       Standard Sega flippers, with green and red
lightsaber stickers (the first prototype's
were reportedly gold plated!).</dd>
<p>
<dt>     Slingshots<dd>     The usual.  Hitting a slingshot will move the
Light Saber Saver light to the opposite outlane
(this is adjustable).  Also, slingshots rotate
lit FORCE letters during single ball play.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     Inlanes<dd>        Lights Landspeeder at opposite orbit.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     Outlane<dd>        Light Saber Saver when lit, which activates a
virtual kickback.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     Special Lane<dd>   A tiny lane just below the swiveling X-Wing.
Normally advances bonus X, is sometimes lit for
special.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     X-Wing<dd>         A rotating turret aimed towards 4 drop targets on
the opposite end of the playfield.  Can be loaded
via the ramp or the Mode Start VUK.</dd>
<p>
<dt> Light Saber Saver Targets<dd>        3 targets on the left side of the game.  Hit any
on the bank 3 times for Light Saber Saver, the
virtual kickback.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     Magnet<dd>         A small magnet in the left-side ramp return, which
diverts balls into the X-Wing when active.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     Left Orbit<dd>     Collect Landspeeder when lit.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     'F' Target<dd>     One of 5 F-O-R-C-E targets.  Used to light a
Hurry-Up or other mode at the Han Hole.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     Ramp<dd>           Shoot to spell FALCON and start Falcon Multiball.
Also used to load the X-Wing.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     Cannon Turret<dd>  Cool toy above the ramp with guns that move when
the ball touches them.  It also is a diverter which
send ramp shots either to a left or right return
lane.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     'O' Target<dd>     See 'F' Target.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     Bumpers<dd>        4 pop/jet bumpers with the Imperial Signia on them.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     'R' Target<dd>     See 'F' Target.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     'C' Target<dd>     See 'F' Target.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     Mode Start<dd>     Used to light and start Heroic modes, and also
collects Extra Ball.  Can be used to load X-Wing.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     'E' Target<dd>     See 'F' Target.</dd>
<p>
<dt> Luke Jackpot Lane<dd>    A small lane above the drop targets, only useful
during one mode.  A tight shot, it can only be hit
directly from the X-Wing.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     Drop targets<dd>   4 targets used to light multiball, and also used to
block shots from the X-Wing(!)</dd>
<p>
<dt>     Tie Fighter<dd>    Another cool toy that won't harm anything if it
breaks.  It rocks when you hit drop targets, and
sometimes when you shoot the ramp.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     Right Orbit<dd>    Remarkably similar in purpose to the Left Orbit.    </dd>
<p>
<dt>     Carbonite Han<dd>  Toy #4 with 8 blinking LED's that tell you how
many more Han Hole lights you need to Thaw Han.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     Han Hole<dd>       A gigantic hole on the right end of the playfield
which starts Hurry-Ups, Probe Multiball, Bounty
Hunter, and Thaw Han (trivia question).  Don't be
misled by its size, more shots than you might
expect will skip over, cup out, or just plain miss,
especially with brand new flippers.</dd>
<p>
<dt>     Backglass<dd>      Okay, so it's not part of the playfield, but it
bears mentioning.  Operators have a choice of
buying a normal (cheaper) backglass, or a cool 3-D
version.  In addition to the convex shape
introduced with Space Jam, the 3-D backglass has
a nifty effect where an X-Wing fighter appears
to come out of the backglass, and the Tie-Fighter
appears to shoot when you move your head side to
side.  I'm told this is done with a lenticular
lens, whatever that is.  It's well worth the cost,
for all you collectors/operators out there reading
this.</dd>
</dl>
<p><span class="bodyTitle">Skill Shot</span><P>
Darth Vader scans his head from side to side, and when you press
the Fire button / both flippers, the escape pod (and your ball)
is launched.
<p>
The "shot" part was finished by production time, but not the
"skill" portion.  I half-recall something about gameplay being
affected in some unannounced way depending on where Darth was
looking when you plunged, but I don't remember if this was a
random theory or substantiated fact.
<p><span class="bodyTitle">Star Wars Multiball</span><P>
Let's cut right to the chase; Star Wars Multiball is a major part
of the game.
<p>
<b>How to Light</b><br>
A 4 x 4 matrix of Tie Fighter lights sits beside the 4 drop
targets.  At the start of the game, a solid row of lights will
cycle among the 4 drops.  Hitting the target next to the lit row
will immediately light multiball, ala Pin/Jack*bot.  If not, you
have to shoot each target 4 times (16 Tie Fighters) to light
multiball.  After your first multiball, you always have to do it
the long way.
<p>
If you have yet to start multiball, Tie Fighters will be
spotted for you when you drain.  I don't recall if multiball is
a gimme on Ball 3 even if you didn't work for it, like with many
Sega games of late.
<p>
<b>How to Start</b><br>
Shoot the ramp (or the Mode Start VUK if not lit) to load the
X-Wing.  At your first multiball, the middle 2 drop targets will be
down and the outside 2 will be up.  You have to shoot the ball
through the gap and into the heretofore unmentioned hole under
the Tie Fighter toy in order to start multiball.  If you miss, you
have to reload the X-Wing and try again.
<p>
Sometimes it gets confused if you make a partial ramp shot, and
it thinks that the ball is in the X-Wing when it's not (at least on
our machine).  Also, our X-Wing malfunctioned briefly during a
game, an "Operator Alert" message appeared, and the X-Wing was
considered disabled.  In this situation, ramp shots feed the left
flipper, and you have to shoot the gap from there (much harder).
<p>
<b>How to Play</b><br>
Once multiball (3-ball?) starts, the ramp is lit for Jackpot
(500K, plus 2K/switch).  After collecting the ramp, the F-O-R-C-E
targets are flashing.  They are worth the Jackpot value, which
continues to increase by 2K/switch.  Drops increase jackpot by
10K each.  Shoot all 5 F-O-R-C-E targets to light Super Jackpot.
<p>
When Super Jackpot is lit, shoot the ramp to load the X-Wing.
Then shoot the gap like you did to start Multiball.  You have to
do this with all the other balls bouncing around, ala Terminator 2.
Collecting Super Jackpot is one of 6 Darth Vader tasks.
<p>
If you fail to shoot at least the Ramp jackpot before draining
down to 1 ball, the ramp and Mode Start VUK are lit for 15 seconds
to load the X-Wing for another shot at starting Multiball.  A
restarted multiball will be 2-ball only.
<p><span class="bodyTitle">Heroic Modes</span><P>
There are 6 Heroic modes which correspond to 7 major characters:
Luke, Leia, Han, C3P0/R2D2, Chewie, and Obi Wan.  Collect all 6
for, um, something related to Extra Ball I'm guessing :)
<p>
Start a mode by shooting the Mode Start hole.  It's lit at the
start of each ball, and unlights after a mode.  Shoot it to
relight it, and shoot it again to start another mode.  The next
mode up is chosen in order, left to right, top to bottom, and
continues between players and even different games.
<p>
All modes are preempted by Multiball, which you can start in any
mode.  The mode continues when you're done.  Modes end at the
end of the ball, when you complete them, or if they time out.
Modes are worth 100K plus whatever you earn during the mode.
Completing any Mode is one of 6 Darth Vader tasks.
<p>
<b>Luke</b><br>
Shoot the ramp or Mode Start to load the X-Wing, and then
shoot the tiny Luke Jackpot Lane above the drop targets to trip
an Imperial Walker.  You have 20? seconds to make the shot,
which is worth 500K.  The mode ends on one successful shot.
<p>
<b>Leia</b><br>
Shoot the ramp to strangle Jabba.  3 shots are needed to
finish, which are worth 200K, 300K, and 400K.  The ramp shots can
be looped, and you have 20? seconds to make them.
<p>
<b>Han</b><br>
Hurry-up is lit at the Han Hole to escape from the Space Slug.
The value starts at 420K, counting down until 100K.  You can't
reasonably expect to get more than 380K or so.  The mode ends on
one successful shot, or if it times out (15? seconds or so).
<p>
<b>C3P0/R2D2</b><br>
Our heros are being crushed in the garbage compactor.  Shoot
the mode start hole for 50K, 150K, and 250K to end the mode.
<p>
<b>Chewie</b><br>
C3P0 is in pieces, and you have to make 4 shots to gather up
the parts (ala Graveyard from Frankenstein).  Shoot the left orbit,
ramp, mode start, and right orbit in any order.  The first shot is
100K, then 200K, 300K, and 400K.  These correspond to an arm, left
leg, right leg, and then the head.  Also, I don't think there's an
animation showing the mode total when you're done, unlike the other
5 Heroic modes.
<p>
<b>Obi Wan</b><br>
Use the force to get past weak-minded Stormtroopers.  Shoot
the right orbit for 250K, the left orbit for 300K, and then the
ramp for 350K to finish the mode.  Move along, move along.
<p><span class="bodyTitle">Falcon Multiball</span><P>
Loop the ramp to spell FALCON and start Falcon Multiball.  This
doesn't work during modes.  After your first Falcon Multiball the
letters start to time out if you don't get another quickly.  The
pace speeds up the more often you get Falcon Multiball.  There's
a cool display effect of a banking Millennium Falcom when
collecting a letter.
<p>
Falcon Multiball starts with 4 balls, and 40 switches needed to
light the ramp for Falcon Jackpot (ala Twister's Chase Multiball).
Collecting the first Falcon Jackpot adds a ball (unless you haven't
drained one).  You need 60 switches to light the next jackpot, then
80, etc.  The first jackpot is 200K plus 2K/switch after lighting
it, then 600K plus 2K/switch.  The third is reportedly worth 800K.
Collecting a Falcon Jackpot is one of 6 Darth Vader tasks.
<p><span class="bodyTitle">Hurry-Ups</span><P>
Shoot the 5 F-O-R-C-E targets in any order to light a Hurry-Up
at the Han Hole.  Shoot the Han Hole to start it.  You can't do
either during a mode.  They aren't all Hurry-Ups, per se.
<p>
Check the Instant Info (or shoot a FORCE target) to see which
hurry-up you're trying to light.  I haven't seen a pattern as to
which one is up next; it might depend on which target you hit
first, or the "skill" shot, or randomness.
<p>
<dl>
<dt>Extra-Ball Hurry-Up<dd>
<p>
The Mode Start hole is lit for 15 seconds, or else you'll be
blown up by a thermal detonator.  Ouch.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Special Hurry-Up<dd>
<p>
Shoot the very difficult Special Lane just under the X-Wing.
You have 15 seconds to shoot Greedo and win a free game (operator
adjustable to award EB or points).</dd>
<p>
<dt>Cantina Hurry-Ups<dd>
<p>
Shoot the ramp for hurry-up values, counting down from ?K to
100K.  You can loop the ramp for multiple awards.</dd>
<p>
<dt>Bounty Hunter<dd>
<p>
Boba Fett points to a seemingly random award, which I've never
seen be anything but Video Mode.  There are rumors of a hidden mode
possible here.
<p>
<dl>
<dt>Video Mode<dd>
<p>
Run over Stormtrooper with your Landspeeder, and avoid
everything else.  The perspective is similar to Guns'N'Roses'
video mode, and the pattern is completely identical!
Reportedly, you can get an Extra Ball with a perfect score;
you must not only survive, but also run over all the men.</dd></dl></dd>
<p>
<dt>Probe Droid Multiball<dd>
<p>
A 2-ball multiball, where one drop target is up.  Shoot it for
200K, and another will appear.  The value goes up by 50K each time.
<p>
Completing a Hurry-Up is a Darth Vader task.  In Cantina, you need
to make at least one shot.  In Probe Droid Multiball, you need to
make at least one drop target.  I'm not sure if you need a perfect
score in Bounty Hunter, or merely to survive completely through it.</dd>
</dl>
<p><span class="bodyTitle">Landspeeder</span><P>
Shoot orbits when lit for 50K, with a 300K award on your fourth
shot.  On your fifth shot after that, you collect extra ball.  On
you sixth shot after <u>that</u>, you get another 300K bonus.
<p>
Orbits are lit by the inlanes, or by making an orbit.  They may
be other ways too.  Getting the first 300K bonus is a Darth Vader
task.
<p><span class="bodyTitle">Thaw Han Solo</span><P>
You need 3 Han Hole shots (not during a Heroic mode) to Thaw Han
the first time.  You need 1 additional shot to get your next Thaw
each time you do it.  Shots which don't Thaw Solo are worth 50K,
and those that do are worth 300K.
<p>
When you Thaw Solo, you are asked a trivia question about Star
Wars.  See Appendix A for a list of questions, and the Spoilers for
answers.
<p>
Thawing Han Solo is a Darth Vader task.
<p><span class="bodyTitle">Light Saber Saver</span><P>
Shoot the Light Saber Saver bank a number of times to light an
outlane for the Light Saber Saver virtual kickback.  Only one
outlane can be lit at a time.  The lit outlane can be changed by
the slingshots; the opposite outlane from the slingshot hit will
become lit.  The number of hits required to light the Saver goes
up the more times you get it, and is percentaged.  You typically
need about 3 hits the first time.
<p><span class="bodyTitle">Extra Balls</span><P>
9 lit orbits, 6X bonus, and/or complete all 6 Heroic Modes.  You
may only need to start the 6th mode; I've heard conflicting
stories on this.  Bozo ball lit on Ball 3 of less than exemplary
performances.
<p><span class="bodyTitle">So what are all these Darth Vader tasks good for?</span><P>
Check Spoilers if you're insufficiently patient to do it on your
own.
<p><span class="bodyTitle">Bonus</span><P>
Bonus is multiplied by the Special Lane, at any point in the game.
Getting bonus X up to 6X is worth an Extra Ball.  Not that this is
easy.
<p>
Still working on how bonus works (composed of Ramps, Loops, Puzzle,
and Heroic components), but a good multiplied bonus can be worth
several million, which is quite a lot of points.  Replay on our
machine is about 10M, and goes up 3M each time.
<p><span class="bodyTitle">Wizard/Tournament Mode</span><P>
Wizard mode stops spotting of Tie Fighters upon draining before
your first multiball.  One or both of these likely disables Bozo
ball.  You start the game in one of these modes by holding a
flipper button while pressing Start.

<p><span class="bodyTitle">The Redemption of Darth Vader</span><P>
(courtesy Michael J. Lewis):
<p>
It's lit at the Heroic VUK after you get the last award.  It's a
two-ball multiball, 50 seconds long, ball-saver on the whole time.
Load the cannon, shoot into the Tie Fighter.  When the cannon is
loaded, though, no drop targets go down automatically.  You have to
knock down some of the drop targets with the other ball just before
the cannon starts rotating and fire through the hole you make (so
it's not really possible to sight the shot by putting your head
over the gun).  Shots are worth between 3M and 4M; I don't know if
the mode ends if you make enough of them, but (a) I assume that it
does end eventually because the intro seems to imply it, and (b)
it takes more than four successful shots to score it.
<p><span class="bodyTitle">Strategy</span><P>
Getting Super Jackpot is by far the most difficult task needed to
start the lucrative Darth Vader mode, and it's not too shabby for
point value on its own.
<p>
As you will soon/have already discovered, the 'C' and 'E' force
targets are the biggest obstacles to getting SJ, notably the 'C'
target.  Both are all the way in the back of the game near the VUK,
and the 'C' target is angled peculiarly and partially blocked by a
piece of rubber or something.  Direct shots are hard but not
impossible; aim a bit to the left to avoid the rubber.  You
sometimes get a free hit from the bumpers, if you can manage to
keep a ball there.
<p>
Once you've lit SJ, catch a ball on the left flipper and shoot the
ramp with the other ball (you're probably down to 2 by this point).
When the target drops, there's nothing that says that you have to
use the ball in the X-Wing to shoot the gap; you could decide to
shoot it with the caught ball if you want.
