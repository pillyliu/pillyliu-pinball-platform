<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Jacks to Open SS - Gottlieb | 1984</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/491d851a-c2cb-4326-967c-6ef4f70e047a-medium.jpg" alt="Jacks to Open backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Shoot drop targets to finish poker hands.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Rebounds from the drop targets, especially the center Queen; hit that on the edge. You can also get a bad feed from the top or from either side lane. Observe the speed when the ball rolls off of the center triangle above to rebound from the side wall and of the ball through the side lanes. Most speeds will be safe, but some may lead to center drains or balls into the slingshots; those are the ones you want to nudge to change.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The is a solid state remake of Jacks Open with a slightly different ruleset and score values ten times higher. The strategy is changed.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">As on Jacks Open, you start by going for two Jacks, then three Queens, then a Full House of three queens and two kings, then the royal flush of all five hearts. The order of the drop targets differs though: on Jacks Open, it’s A-Q-K-J-Q-K-J-Q-10; here, it’s A-K-K-Q-Q-Q-J-J-10. The five royal flush drop targets are in the same places, but the other four cards have moved.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Here, the top lanes light matching-colored side lanes, return lanes and outlanes, rather than increasing the value of the drop targets. Drops targets are always worth 5K. The lit upper star bumper is worth 10K; the center and unlit star bumper 100 points unlit, 1K lit. The star bumpers are each lit by the outer top lane above them and stay lit for the rest of the game. The skill shot is therefore to get one of those two outer top lanes to light a star bumper.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Unlike Jacks Open, this game has end of ball bonus: 10K per card earned. A royal flush earns double bonus.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">On Jacks Open, your strategy is to milk the target banks after you raise the target values rather than completing each “hand” as quickly as possible. Here on Jack To Open, you want to finish hands in the minimum number of shots to get to Royal Flush a.s.a.p. It’s still good to have as many upper lanes as you can to light the lower lanes, but now, putting the ball back up top to do so is no longer a priority.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Earned top lanes and cards are held from ball to ball.</p></div></section></div>
