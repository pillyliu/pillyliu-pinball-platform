<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Valencia SS - Williams | 1975</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/3b6eb6f7-4227-4749-aa68-82ef24eae8b1-medium.jpg" alt="Valencia backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">UTAD via the outer arcs over the rollover buttons. Shoot the A and B stand-ups to double your bonus once at 19K, then resume UTAD.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Balls exiting the top are dangerous if they hit the side walls and deflect out of reach of the upper flippers, often leading to center drains. If you choose to shoot at the button stand-up targets, watch out for the rebounds. The four letter targets A-B-C-D all have risk, especially rebounding into the opposite-side slingshot or outlane.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Valencia is the four-player version of Toledo. The safest way to score points is just go up top all day, scoring the rollovers and occasionally the top saucer.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Bonus goes up to 19K, built by the lower rollover buttons, the upper rollover buttons when lit (alternates on/off on 10 point switches) and the top saucer when lit. The saucer also alternates between “spot numbers” (see below) and advance bonus on 10-point switches. Bonus is doubled by scoring the A and B stand-up targets on the sides.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Once your bonus is at the 19K maximum, the top saucer value alternates between spot numbers and 5000 points.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The focal point of the game is the center \#1-\#8 stand-up targets. The first quirk is the targets themselves – they’re not normal stand-ups, they’re flipper buttons\! This is the only game I know of with that. The second quirk is their scoring. Targets are 100 unlit, 500 lit; \#1 starts lit, the others not. Hit \#1 and the light moves right to \#2; now both \#1 and \#2 score 500. Hit \#2 and the light moves to \#3, etc. on to \#8. You can also advance the light by scoring the little rollover button in the middle below \#s 4 and 5 (the button itself doesn’t score any points), and the top saucer when lit spots 2 numbers. Once you finish all 8 numbers, the targets change value to 1000 plus add bonus. You can get a lot of points this way . . . IF you survive the rebounds from the buttons. Odds are you won’t.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The playfield layout is similar to Air Aces. As on that game, avoid the temptation to shoot the center targets, especially from the upper flippers. That gets the ball going sideways too much and risks draining. It’s a bit of a shame that the centerpiece of the game is not worth the risk to shoot at.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Strategy is just up top to build bonus, then A and B to double it, then up top again for the 5K saucer. Everything resets each ball.</p></div></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Skill Shot:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">get the top saucer.</p></section></div>
