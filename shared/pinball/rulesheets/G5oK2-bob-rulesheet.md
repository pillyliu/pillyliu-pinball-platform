<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Aces &amp; Kings EM - Williams | 1970</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/b6907249-891f-4892-a5bf-cb2e5c2d1dfa-medium.jpg" alt="Aces &amp; Kings backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">shoot the lit spinner to increase saucer value, collect the saucer when near maximum, repeat.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Every shot other than a successful one into a saucer has risk here since the rebounds go out of control.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The game’s title is kind of a misnomer given how to best play it. Finishing either the Aces or the Kings lights the respective saucer on that side for extra ball, but if extra balls are off, they’re pretty useless. If EBs are turned on, go for Aces and Kings, but if not, don’t bother to shoot them\!</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Your plunge will go through either the “light green joker” or “light yellow joker” lane. That makes the saucer value for whichever color you get 10 times its base value for the rest of that ball. Shooting the spinner increases the saucer value, but only for the color lit at the spinner at the time. The switches labeled “change” will flip which color the spinner advances. Ideally, you want to keep the spinner on the color you plunged and shoot it to advance it. Rebounds off of spinner shots, however, will often hit a change switch and flip it to the other color. If the spinner is on the wrong color, shoot the ball up the side of the machine to change it; it may take more than one try, depending on how many times you hit a change switch before you get the ball back under control on a flipper. Once your 10X side color bonus is at or near maximum value, shoot the saucer to collect. Rinse and repeat.</p></div></section></div>
