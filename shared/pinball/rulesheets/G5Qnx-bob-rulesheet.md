<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Sound Stage EM - Chicago Coin | 1976</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/a3d6d085-f82f-4e57-9d0d-39d84b0aa504-medium.jpg" alt="Sound Stage backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 2</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Hit the C-D-E-F stand-up targets, then UTAD until your bonus is at maximum. After that, hit the spin stand-ups all day.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">All feeds and stand-up target rebounds have risk here. Be extra vigilant when the ball is coming off of the triangle behind the two spin targets as this often leads to a lot of side-to-side action.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This was Chicago Coin’s last solo game before they sold to Stern while making Stampede / Rawhide. It’s not the greatest, but the design is a bit unusual.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This game has two shots that are both important and difficult. The right orbit is narrower than most, so the ball often hits the left edge of the gap next to the special button and fails to go through. The other is the top saucer, which you have no direct shot for; UTAD plus nudging is your only hope.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The letter stand-up targets increase values: get C &amp; D, the middle ones opposite the center triangle, to light the spinner; the lower pair, E &amp; F, to light the hole for Double Bonus; and the upper left two, A &amp; B light the extra ball lane, if enabled. Get C-D-E-F and ignore A-B unless extra balls are on.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Bonus goes up to 19K, advanced by the return lanes, top lanes and three rollover buttons but not by the stand-up targets. The double bonus hole can’t be shot directly; just get your C-D, go UTAD and hope-and-nudge. Bonus here is slow to build, so focus first on lighting the spinner before going after double bonus.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The spinner is not the usual ball-goes-through kind, it’s two stand-up targets that make a pinwheel below the playfield spin. The rebounds from the spin stand-ups are chancy, but it’s good value when lit for 1K.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The top two lanes alternate which is lit on hits to the three small white rollover buttons. The lit lane is worth 3K.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The preferred strategy is to hit the C, D, E and F targets to light the double bonus hole and the spinner. After that, shoot the right orbit up top to get advances until your bonus is at 19K and hopefully have the ball drop into the double bonus saucer on one of those trips. Once your bonus is filled up, shoot the spin stand-ups from then on. If you have the ball on the right flipper and can’t transfer it easily to the left, shoot C or E if you need either, otherwise shoot the spin stand-ups.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">If you find the right orbit shot is too narrow for you, your best alternative is to shoot towards the A target from the right flipper and hope that bumper action kicks the ball up into the double bonus saucer.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Key feeds: rebounds from the spin stand-ups and from missed right orbit shots.</p></div></section></div>
