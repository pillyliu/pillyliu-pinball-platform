<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Super Spin EM - Gottlieb | 1977</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/67dd3191-72e6-4a9c-ac42-94ce3e5f715f-medium.jpg" alt="Super Spin backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 2</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Center roto-target and upper left bullseye stand-up target all day.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Right</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Bumper action (fully exposed, highest risk) and rebounds off any targets. The wide-open playfield lends itself to rebounds that may go anywhere - - drain, go to a slingshot or come to a flipper. Rebounds from the roto target and the far left chute are the most predictable and safest.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">An unusual game for its time in that the end of ball bonus is present but is not a large part of your total score. Bonus here only goes up to 15K and there’s no multiplier. Given other individual shots on the playfield score up to 5K, there’s no big reason to worry about bonus here.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Points here come mainly from the upper left bullseye stand-up target and the center roto-target. You can score each of the three exposed roto targets two ways: hit it or hit its award target elsewhere; the left stand-up scores the left roto target, the top right saucer for the center roto, the lower right stand-up for the right roto. The center one awards ten times value, so it&#x27;s the only one really worth getting. Since the two side roto targets aren’t at 10X, there’s little point in shooting at either of them or at the stand-up targets that award them.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The top lanes and the vari-target all spin the roto to display different values.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Key feeds: the return from the bullseye target and rebounds off the roto targets.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">While the vari-target awards up to 5 bonus advances, it’s a drain risk and I wouldn’t bother with it. Let your bonus build up from the bullseye, the center roto target, the return lanes and random bounces or missed shots. Just shoot the center roto and the bullseye all day. If the ball ends up in the bumpers, try to nudge it into the collect center roto saucer. Use dead bounces and shatzes to get the ball to the right flipper. See if you can backhand the center roto from the left flipper if it’s difficult to transfer the ball to the right.</p></div></section></div>
