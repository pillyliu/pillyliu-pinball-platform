<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Royal Guard EM - Gottlieb | 1968</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/2a2ed74f-2160-435b-bf10-709d4ecbd190-medium.jpg" alt="Royal Guard backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 1</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Hit most of the center moving-rail targets, then UTAD.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Center drains are mostly from balls exiting the top that fall onto the slingshots and go out of control or rebounds off of the moving stand-up targets. Side drains are mostly from slingshot action and rebounds off of the stand-up targets just above the tops of the slingshots. You can also side drain if you hit the outside edge of the moving targets on each end.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This is a fine example of a “deferred UTAD” game. There are 5 lettered lanes at the top, A-B-C-D-E. Each lights an outlane for 500 points [their base value is 50], corresponding to the outlane positions from left to right, e.g. the “A” lights the far left outlane [of the two on that side], C lights the center drain. Ideally, you want to get the C first, so you’re covered while using the flippers, then get a different letter each ball after that. When draining out the side, remember to nudge to get a lit 500 lane if only one of the two outlanes on that side is lit.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The primary feature here is the moving center rail of four colored disk targets, blue, red, yellow and green. Each disk lights a feature of the same color in the upper playfield when hit. When the rail is on the left side, the hit target lights a stand-up target above; when the rail is to the right, it lights a bumper. Once lit, any of these stay lit for the rest of the game.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Strategy: hit as many of the rail disk targets as you can to light upper playfield features. Once you’ve got most of the features lit, especially the stand-up targets, go UTAD. Nudge the ball when in the bumpers to try to direct it into any lit stand-ups. And if the opportunity arises to nudge to get the ball to go up a not-yet-lit top lane [A-E], by all means do it.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The ball will rarely drain directly from the upper playfield; it’s a tight angle to the sides, and a relatively small portion of the drop-downs from above go clean down the center without a chance to bounce them off a flipper or post. Most drains happen because you are unable to get control of the ball and it deflects around the lower playfield until going down the center or sides.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This is another back-loaded game as far as scoring, i.e. most of your score will come on balls 4 and 5 since you’ll have the most features lit then. Don’t let a bad ball 1 or 2 get to you; if you can get enough things lit later on, you can make a good comeback.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">There’s an important strategy element to keep in mind. If you’re the last player and you have two balls left and trail by 900 with the center lane lit, just try to center drain twice for the win. The same idea applies in pingolf situations when you’re close to the goal. Unless you have all 5 out lanes lit, any continuation of play risks draining for 50 points rather than 500.</p></div></section></div>
