<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Knockout EM - Bally | 1975</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/c9f80f84-902f-49e6-a8fc-ff04112669ab-medium.jpg" alt="Knockout backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 2</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">UTAD, through either spinner.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Balls falling through either spinner or the gaps towards the center from them that go onto the slingshots. Balls that are hit by a bumper into the rubber just above the spinners that go through the spinner to center drain. Occasionally, the center saucer will lip out for a drain down the middle. The bottom stand-up targets have rebound risk, but you shouldn’t shoot at them anyway.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">One of the center wiggly awards is double bonus, but it’s on a switch hit cycle, you can’t change it on your own. If it’s not lit, just go up top again, eventually it will be.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Ignore the two bottom stand-up targets.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">As for the center saucer, it scores 30 the first time shot, 300 the next time, and 3000 per shot thereafter for the rest of that ball. But it’s a risky shot due to the posts on either side, so I usually don’t bother shooting directly at the saucer either.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Key feeds: when the ball comes down through either spinner, does it trigger the slingshot or roll down to a flipper? And how’s the kickout from the center saucer? The saucer kickout should hit the left flipper, but you need to determine if you should dead bounce, live catch, hold up the flipper, or maybe need to nudge, too, if it tends to come out too close to the slingshot or flipper pivot. It’s possible for the ball to lip out of that saucer as well, which can lead to an instant drain. If lip-outs happen too often, try to nudge just before the ball gets to the saucer to ensure that it goes in.</p></div></section></div>
