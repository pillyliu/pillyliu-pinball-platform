<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Airport EM - Gottlieb | 1969</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/e6aff411-05cb-4263-8c24-928b06a33cc3-medium.jpg" alt="Airport backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 2</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">either UTAD or lit vari-target, except left side lane when lit.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Mild bias Right</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Risk abounds, both vary-target rebounds and any shot up top to the bumpers. Highest risks are exiting the bumpers, either down the middle or into the slingshots and then out of control.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This was the first game to feature Gottlieb’s “vari-targets”; the four-player version of it is College Queens (Airport is two-player). Shots at the vari-targets drain often enough to make them risky, though, so use caution. If you’re accurate and the flippers are strong enough to push the targets almost all the way back, shoot them when the 10 times value light is on; when not lit, or with weak flippers, avoid them.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The game is later-ball-heavy due to when things light up. On each ball, more features – lanes and targets – will be lit for ten times starting value. This means a long ball 1 or 2 will be beaten by a ball 3, 4 or 5 of the same or even shorter length. Ball 5 in particular, with maximal lit values, is super important to have a long ball time; you can come from well behind at the end.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The game has a side exit to the plunger lane right of the center bumper. Any time you see the ball in that area, try to nudge it out that lane.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">There’s no real strategy here, just flip the ball up into the bumpers and hope for lots of action and maybe a right-side exit or shoot vari-targets when lit. The other thing to do is shoot the ball up the left side lane, where it will exit into the bumpers at an angle. When the side lanes are lit for 300 points, that’s your go-to shot; the indicator light is the one below the bottom bumper.</p></div></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Skill Shot:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">go through any lit top lane.</p></section></div>
