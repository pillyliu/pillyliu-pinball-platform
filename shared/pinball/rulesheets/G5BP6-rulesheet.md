<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Haunted House SS - Gottlieb | 1982</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/b51a1735-a66f-461a-8f0a-f909947dbe02-medium.jpg" alt="Haunted House backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Shortname:</strong> HH</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Shoot the ball to get to the upper and lower playfields (details below), then hit drop targets all day.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Bumper kicks, rebounds from all targets, and balls falling down the ramp from the upper playfield can all drain or go out of control. The kickout from the lower playfield is usually safe. Nudging to save a ball is much less effective than on most games due to the weight and open layout of the machine.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">First things first, let’s discuss the three levels and going between them. When you plunge, balls will land in one of three holes at the top left. The left-most hole drops the ball to the lower playfield, where it is kicked out from “above” (towards you) the lower left flipper towards the bumper. The center hole / saucer kicks the ball out downwards on the main playfield. The right hole / saucer kicks the ball up and to the right onto the left side of the upper playfield towards the left flipper. Balls can go between the main and the upper playfield via the ramp on the right. Balls can go from the upper to the lower playfield via the hole on the left side of the upper; these land near the lower left flipper. Balls exiting the lower playfield are always popped to the main playfield through the scoop just below and right of the top left plunger holes; they usually are thrown towards the upper of the two flippers on the main playfield left. Balls can enter the lower playfield from the main one via the top left plunger hole, by going through the hole behind the center push-back target, and by shooting the “trap door” shot on the far right. From the center hole, the ball lands just above the lower left flipper. From the trap door, the ball is kicked out over the lower right flipper going left across the playfield.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The flippers require getting used to. The upper and lower playfield flippers are not reversed, but it sometimes seems like it. The upper “right” flipper faces the way a left flipper normally does and vice versa. The lower playfield flippers aren’t reversed in terms of where they are visually either, right being right, but are reversed if you think in terms of which flipper it is if you were “facing” the lower playfield.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">To activate both the upper and lower flippers, you need to use the set of flipper buttons slightly further back on the cabinet than the regular buttons. When the ball changes playfields to or from the main playfield in either direction, you’ll have to quickly switch which set of buttons you use\!</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">On the main playfield, there’s a small drain area between the two flippers on the right; beware\!</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Completing a set of drop targets in either the lower or upper playfield activates double scoring on the opposite playfield. Completing them a second time activates double scoring on the main playfield. (How’s that for convoluted?) If you do enough work in the top or bottom, the other two playfields will both be worth double. Completing the lower playfield drops seems to be the easier of the two.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Each visit to the upper or lower playfield raises your “monster bonus” multiplier, up to 5X. Monster bonus is earned on just these two playfields by hitting the drop targets and the lower stand-up targets. If you plunge to the upper playfield and the ball exits it out the left to the lower playfield rather than out the right via the ramp to the main, your bonus multiplier will already be at 3X.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Points are primarily earned by hitting the drop and stand-up targets on all three playfields. Strategy is to keep going upstairs and downstairs where you’re temporarily safe and smacking the heck out of the targets while there.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Lastly, the game tends to play slow unless the operator has consciously steepened it, and it’s notorious for having maintenance issues. Fun, but it can be a risk to use in competition due to its sometimes iffy reliability.</p></div></section></div>
