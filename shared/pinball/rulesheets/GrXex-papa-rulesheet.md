<p><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>This is a
glossary of the shots and features that will be referred to during the
discussion of the game. </span></p>

<p><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>BASICS,
SWITCHES, DEVICES ETC:</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>At
Start Up </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>If there are no pinball(s) left in the
Kick-Out-Holes or the Deep 4 Alley, from a previous game, the Deep 4 Caper Gate
will be open and lit up when the first ball is kicked out into the plunger lane.
</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>If there are pinball(s) left in the
Kick-Out-Holes or the Deep 4 Caper Alley, from a previous game, the Deep 4
Caper Gate will be closed and not lit up when the first ball is kicked out into
the plunger lane. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>The Code Zapper Gate is closed at the start
of a game.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Flippers
</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>There are two Flippers with double rubbers
on each of them. They measure 2-1/2�. The �Flipper Zipper� was introduced in a
game called �Bazaar�. The flipper-gap is closed by a skill shot where you tap
the Red Mushroom Bumper, located on the lower-middle right side of the
playfield. �The Flippers open when you tap one of three (3) Yellow Mushroom Bumpers,
located in the center to left side of the playfield.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Manual
Plunger (Starting Skill Shot)</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>This is a normal plunger. Its lane is standard
length and leads to a gate, beyond which there is a Rollover Button at the top
center, a Rollover Switch between two post and two Kick-Out Holes which may
capture the ball. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Rollover
Button </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>The Rollover Button at the top center is a
10 point shot that also opens the �Code Zapper Caper� Gate and lights a
porthole in the Sea Ray Ship.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>�</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Top
Rollover Switch</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>The Top Rollover Switch is between two posts,
just below the Rollover Button and it is a 100 point shot. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Kick-Out-Holes
</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>There are two Kick-Out-Holes located at the
top to the left and right of the Top Rollover Switch. They do not score any
points. They capture and hold the ball, if there is not a ball in the Deep 4
Exit Alley. If the first ball is held than a second ball is produced to be
played from the Manual Plunger, this also happens if both balls are captured. The
captive ball(s) are released for Multi-ball by a skill shot where you tap the White
Mushroom Bumper, located on the upper-right side middle of the playfield. A
captive ball, in the Kick-Out-Holes, will carry over from player to player and
game to game. If a ball is in the Deep 4 Alley then any ball that lands in a
Kick-Out-hole is immediately �kicked out� and put back into play.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Code
Zapper Caper Gate </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>This gate is located on the lower-left side
middle of the playfield. It opens when the Blue Mushroom Bumper is tapped or
when you cross the Rollover Button.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Code
Zapper Rollover Switch</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>When crossed by the ball this switch
activates the Code Zapper Kicker Solenoid.<b> </b></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Code
Zapper Kicker Solenoid </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>The Code Zapper Kicker kicks the ball up
the left side of the playfield across the Left Rebound Gate Rollover Switch and
back into play.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Left
Rebound Gate Rollover Switch</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>When crossed by the ball the Left Rebound
Gate Rollover Switch is worth whatever the Code Symbol is on the Code Zapper
wheel.� Triangle = 100 points.� Circle = 300 points and a Square = 500 points. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif";
color:red'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Code
Zapper Symbols </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>There are 3 symbols. The Triangle, which is
worth 100 points. A Circle, which is worth 300 points and a Square, which is
worth 500 points. Symbols on the Code Zapper Caper wheel advance if one of the
Yellow Mushroom Bumpers is tapped, if the Blue Mushroom Bumper is tapped, if
one of the Out Lane Rollover Switches is crossed or if the Rollover Button, at
the top center is crossed.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Deep
4 Caper Gate &amp; the Free Ball Gate</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>The Deep 4 Caper Gate is located on the
upper-right side middle of the playfield. It is open when there is no captive
ball. It closes when there is a captive ball or when a ball enters the Deep 4
Alley.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>The Free Ball Gate is located on the right
side middle of the playfield in line with the Plunger Lane. It is closed to the
Deep 4 Alley and opened to the Plunger Lane when a pinball is in the Deep 4
Alley. It is opened to the Deep 4 Alley and closed to the Plunger Lane when the
Deep 4 Caper Gate is opened. The function of this gate is to prevent a ball
from entering the Deep 4 Alley if another ball is already present there.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif";
color:red'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Right
Rebound Gate Switch</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>The Right Rebound Gate Switch, located at
the top end of the Plunger Lane on the right side of the playfield, sets Player
Advance, Ball Return and Next Play relays. It keeps the machine sequence in
order since balls can be held on the playfield by the Kick-Out-Holes and the
Deep 4. It also turns off the Same Player Shoots Again Light. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif";
color:red'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Deep
4 Alley Switches</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>The Deep 4 Alley Switches are actually one
bar that is pulled in by the Ball Advance Solenoid that is activated when the
White Mushroom Bumper is tapped. The bar has small levers that pivot when it is
pulled in and the levers kick the ball to the next step. A captive ball, in the
Deep 4 Alley, will carry over from player to player and game to game.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif";
color:red'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Super
Bonus Caper Unit</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Counts up one, each time you complete all
six porthole lights on the Sea Ray Ship. Points or a Free Game are awarded at
5, 6, 7 and (or) 8. (See �Sea Ray Super Bonus Caper� for details.) </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Left
Slingshot Rebound</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>The Left Slingshot rebound is 10 points. It
has a solenoid that does kick the ball out to the playfield. (See Skill Shot
below.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Right
Slingshot Rebound </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>The Right Slingshot rebound is 10 points.
It does not have a solenoid kick the ball out to the playfield. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Other
Rubber Rebounds �</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>There are 5 other rebounds. They are all 1
point each. Two are on the right side and three are on the left side of the
playfield.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>�</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Thumper
Bumpers</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>There are three Thumper Bumpers. They are
always 10 points each. They should not have �10 Points when Lit� written on the
top.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>�</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Left
and Right Out Lane Rollover Switches</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>The Out Lane Switches are 50 points each.
They also advance the Code Zapper Symbol. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Red
Mushroom Bumper </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>There is one Red Mushroom Bumper, located
on the lower-right side middle of the playfield. It closes the gap on the Flippers.
It does not score any points when tapped.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>White
Mushroom Bumper </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>There is one White Mushroom Bumper, located
on the upper-right side middle of the playfield. It is a 10 point shot. It releases
any captive balls when tapped to start a two or three ball Multi-ball. It also
moves a ball down to the next Deep 4 Alley position.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>���
</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>��
Yellow Mushroom Bumper </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>There are three Yellow Mushroom Bumpers,
located in the center to left side of the playfield. They each score 100 points
when tapped. Each one also advances the Code Zapper and lights one of the portholes
in the Sea Ray Ship. They also open the �Flipper Zipper� if the flippers were
closed.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Blue
Mushroom Bumper </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>There is one Blue Mushroom Bumper, located
on the upper-left side middle of the playfield. It scores 10 points when
tapped. It also opens the Zapper Gate and lights the Zapper Gate light.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>THE
4 CAPERS: </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Code
Zapper Caper </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>The Code Zapper Gate is opened when you tap
the Blue Mushroom Bumper or cross over the Rollover Button. When entering the
Code Zapper Caper Gate the ball will cross the Code Zapper Kicker Rollover
Switch which activates the Code Zapper Kicker Solenoid. During this time the
Code Zapper Gate closes. The Code Zapper Kicker kicks the ball up the left side
of the playfield across the Left Rebound Gate Rollover Switch which gives the
player points equal to whatever the Code Symbol is on the Code Zapper wheel at
that moment. The ball is then up at the top of the playfield and back into play.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Deep
4 Caper </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>The Deep 4 Caper Gate is open when there is
no captive ball. When you enter the Deep 4 Alley Gate the ball stops at the
first of four steps and the Deep 4 Caper Gate closes. The ball is then moved to
the next step when you tap the White Mushroom Bumper. After moving the ball
down the Deep 4 Alley four steps the ball exits out of the Deep 4 Alley to the Plunger Lane and back into play.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>Sea
Ray Caper </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;

cross the Rollover Button at the top center of the playfield, tap the Blue

Super Bonus Caper wheel for that player advances by one. (If you cover the

<p class=MsoNormal><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>MULTIBALL:</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Book Antiqua","serif"'>The game has two different versions of
multi-ball play. If you have a ball locked in the Deep 4 Alley, the two Kick-Out-Holes
will simply kick the ball back into play without scoring any points. If the <br>
Deep 4 Alley is empty then the two Kick-Out-Holes will capture balls for multi-ball
play and the gate to the Deep 4 Alley will close. In other words both the Deep
4 Alley and the two Kick-Out-Holes will trap balls for multi-ball play but if
one is active the other is not. As for releasing the trapped balls you would
have to hit the White Mushroom Bumper near the Deep 4 Alley. For balls trapped
in the Kick-Out-Holes the mushroom bumper will simply release them into play.
For a ball trapped in the Deep 4 Alley, each time you tap the White Mushroom Bumper
the ball will move down one step. After four hits the ball will exit the Deep 4
Alley and be returned to the Plunger Lane. </span></p>
