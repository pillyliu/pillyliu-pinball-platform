<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Straight Flush EM - Williams | 1970</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/d9b1537a-4a92-4a1e-b9ac-ab896e8bf5ac-medium.jpg" alt="Straight Flush backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 1</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">UTAD via the left orbit.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Right</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Riskiest type - exposed bumpers playfield. Bumper action and target rebounds can drain or go out of control. Avoid hitting bumpers face-on. The only safe shots are the left orbit and the saucer.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">On Straight Flush, the goal is to collect all five cards. Each has a lane at the top, but they can also be scored via the center saucer. You get 100,000 [or an extra ball, depending on settings] for the set of five. You also get 100K for scoring all three jokers via the center saucer, and 100K for the left orbit when lit for Special.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Each top lane or card lights the bumper below it.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">On the right side are Up Post and Down Post stand-up targets, which raise and lower the center ball-save post. Like many other games with center posts, you can let the ball settle between either flipper and the post when it’s up, then use soft flips to set up shots.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The center saucer scores the card indicated by the pointed arrow below it. The arrow moves one position clockwise whenever you hit either of the two Advance stand-up targets near the top or go over either advance rollover button. The arrow does not advance when you score the saucer. You also score the arrowed card when the ball goes through the “spot card \+ 5000” lane on the left.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The Ace lights the left outlane for 5K, the Ten lights the right outlane; no big deal.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Since the left orbit shot scores 10,000 even when not lit, that’s your go-to shot all day. Lighting it for Special is pseudo-random; it goes on and off based on 100’s digit of your score, apparently lit for two digits out of the ten. You may find that shots up top via the left orbit tend to bounce into one or two of the card lanes more often than the others. If so, skew your plunges to get the other cards.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Don’t worry about the center saucer; the ball will wander into it periodically after bouncing around in the bumpers. Treat getting cards or jokers from the saucer as a fringe benefit but not a goal. The kickout from it should go cleanly to the left flipper.</p></div></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Skill Shot:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">get any lit lane at the top; the harder ones are to the left, so try to get those first.</p></section></div>
