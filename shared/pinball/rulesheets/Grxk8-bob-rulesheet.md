<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Joust EM - Bally | 1969</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/66e844e9-722c-4a8c-8137-096087a502c6-medium.jpg" alt="Joust backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 2</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">UTAD along the left wall into the top mushroom bumper from the right flipper, and through the gate at the top right from the left flipper.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Left</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Bumper action and balls falling down from left of the bumpers. Rebounds from regular and mushroom bumpers are risky, so try to just graze them enough to register rather than a face-on hit. More drains come from balls going into the slingshots than directly draining.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Joust is an odd-ball game with layout and rules different from most others of its era. It has no drop targets, no spinner, no end-of-ball bonus and a two-stage outlane with no return lane on the left. The primary scoring is via a set of nine white rollover disks in a chamber at the upper right of the playfield. Completing specific sets of numbers earns you points: 1 through 6 for 3000, 7-8-9 for 5000 and all nine for 10000. The ball enters the chamber from the upper left, shootable from either flipper. Once in the chamber, it deflects off pins, the rollovers and dividers, scoring three of the numbers on the way down. Obviously, you want to nudge the game so the ball goes over numbers you haven’t got yet.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Five of the numbers can be earned outside the chamber, as marked on the playfield. Get the 1 from the center mushroom bumper, which also scores 1000. Get the 2 and the 6 from the side mushroom bumpers, which also open the flippers if closed. The top left mushroom bumper gives you the 4, closes the bumpers and scores 1000, making it a good thing to shoot for if you can’t backhand the chamber from the right flipper. The top saucer gets you the 5, which is naturally your desired Skill Shot.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Any shot into the chamber opens the flippers.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">There’s a ball save gate on the right outlane of the machine. You open it by hitting the white rollover disk above and to the right of the right flipper. If you can nudge a ball exiting the chamber to go over it, definitely do so. You can shoot at it with the left flipper [late flip, somewhat dangerous - - a miss-hit may just drain out that same right outlane], or hit it from the right flipper with a n early backhand shot when the flippers are zipped and the ball cradled.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The game overall is pretty much UTAD. The exceptions I’d make are if you just need the 1, 2 or 6 digit to reach to one of the “lighting” scores, go ahead and hit the mushroom bumper to get it, just try to graze it on the inner side so that the ball continues up the playfield after the shot.</p></div></section></div>
