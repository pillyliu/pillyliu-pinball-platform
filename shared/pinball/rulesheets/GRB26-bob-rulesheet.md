<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Solar Fire SS - Williams | 1981</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/03b3f368-2ee0-4d27-baa7-342d85190892-medium.jpg" alt="Solar Fire backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Upper 5-bank all day. Take multiball if you can get it safely, but think 5-bank first.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Balls falling down either ramp from the upper playfield. Any shot on the lower playfield has high rebound risk. Magna saves will try to grab the ball but don’t always do so and don’t always drop it into a return lane when they do.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Solar Fire has possibly the most complex rule set of its time; Elektra would be the other contender.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The playfield is similar to the more common Grand Lizard, featuring a drop target 5-bank on the upper playfield with 5-level timing-based scoring [value locked in at 20K, 40K, 60K, 80K or 100K with first target dropped]. The targets reset if not completed quickly.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Completing the top 3-bank when flashing spots a FIRE letter; getting the flashing FIRE letter gives 20 seconds of double scoring. The upper 3-bank lights the loop behind it for 25K, then 50K, then drain shield. You can sometimes score this loop twice by shooting the ball into the loop on the bottom end, where it can go in and up, trip the switch hidden under the plastic, then fall back down that way to trip the switch again rather than continuing out the top.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Multiball here is far riskier than on Grand Lizard. You need to complete the lower left target bank or lower center target bank to light a lock. Which order the locks light in appears random.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Two-ball multiball play starts when you lock your second ball in either the lower center or top right saucer. Locking all three balls starts multiball; note this requires locking your second ball in the far left saucer. Subtle\!</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The top 5-bank scoring is 2X during 2-ball multiball and 3X during 3-ball multiball. If you’re in 3-ball multiball and lose one ball, you drop down to 2X scoring. It’s pretty easy to lose one ball quickly while trying to juggle things on multiple levels, so odds are your 3X won’t last long. After draining one ball during three-ball multiball, you can relight a lock by completing one of the 3-banks.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Collecting S-O-L-A-R letters in the chute at the left between the lower saucers each give you 1 additional bonus multiplier up to 6X max. The lit letter in SOLAR changes continuously, so you may need more than 5 shots to get all the letters. Base bonus goes up to 63K on each side for a maximum of 756K at 6X. Left side is bonus earned during single-ball play; right side is bonus earned during multiball play. Unique\!</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The game has both magna saves and drain shield. The magna saves are activated by buttons on either side above the flipper button and trigger the magnet above the return lane on that side of the machine. Finishing a 3-bank awards a magna save activation, up to three times per side.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The drain shield is activated by completing the upper left target 3-bank three times and activates one outlane as protected (sides alternate). When active, a ball draining through the lit side is returned to play.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Ball control. The top saucer should kick the ball out to the upper left flipper. You’ll find that balls that fall just below the upper right flipper but still manage to hit the metal above the center ramp will bounce just enough to be tipped by the upper left flipper and kept on the upper playfield. Keep your left flipper down and ready when the ball goes there. The center ramp is by far the easier route to the top vs. the right ramp. Check before you play to see if the center post between the flippers has been removed or had the rubber taken off.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Ignore the solar gun captive ball shot.</p></div></section></div>
