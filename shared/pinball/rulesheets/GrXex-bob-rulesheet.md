<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Capersville EM - Bally | 1966</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/6f6b92c2-a397-49a4-a3eb-7766d4a3160c-medium.jpg" alt="Capersville backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">shoot the red bumper to close the flippers, then UTAD while avoiding the green bumpers as best you can.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Bias Left</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">With the wide-open playfield and wider-angle slingshots, you’ll drain a lot when balls fall onto the slings and exit the outlanes. The middle drains a lot when the flippers aren’t zipped, largely from balls falling at unfortunate angles and with little chance to nudge them away.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The colored mushroom bumpers each play a different role here. The red one zips the flippers together, preventing center drains. The blue one opens the gate at the left for access to the chute there. The white one advances a ball in the 4-stage “Deep 4 Caper” lane on the right side if one is there, or releases any balls locked in the saucers. Lastly, the three green bumpers reopen the zipped flippers and advance the left chute “zapper” value.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This is one of the first multiball games. Get a ball in either top saucer to lock it provided there are no balls in the right chute. Release the ball(s) by shooting the white bumper. Balls can be re-locked and re-released during multiball.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">You can’t shoot the ball into the left chute to collect its value; it goes in mainly from slingshot kicks. Both outlanes advance the left chute value, shown on the backglass via the triangle (100 points), X (300) and square (500) icons.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The right chute will have the gate to enter it open when there are no balls in it or locked. Once a ball enters the chute, the gate closes. Balls are advanced in 4 steps down the chute by hitting the white bumper; once the ball gets to the bottom, it’s returned to the shooter lane for a fresh plunge.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Both locks and Deep 4 chute progress carry over from both ball to ball and game to game, so stealing locks and progress is in play here.</p></div></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Skill Shot:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Get the ball in either saucer, if empty, otherwise get it into the center lane.</p></section></div>
