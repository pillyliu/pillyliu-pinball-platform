<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Eight Ball Deluxe SS - Bally | 1981</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/fa289985-0b62-4116-a46d-38283589b107-medium.jpg" alt="Eight Ball Deluxe backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Shortname:</strong> EBD</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 4</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> alphanumeric</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">From the left flipper, shoot the drop targets until complete, then shoot the 8-ball target above them. From the right flipper, left orbit all day unless 8 balls are complete and you’re working on rack \#2, then shoot the bonus X inline targets until at 5X, then resume left orbit.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Bias Right</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">The side drop targets, inline targets and 8-ball target all have rebound risk; the 8-ball shot risk is partly dependent on the machine’s steepness and how hard the ball hits it. Bumper drains are common, two annoying sequences in particular: top right lane, center bumper, right bumper, center drain; and right bumper, sling above the right side of the right bumper, center drain.</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Sequel to Eight Ball and considered the more fun of the two. It has the same super-bonus for completing your eight balls, but a different layout.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Skill Shot and after any shot to the top: nudge for the lit lane.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Let’s start with the right flipper. Why left orbit rather than the bonus X inlines at first? Simple math. A multiplier is worth 7000 times however many targets you’ve collected. Left orbit shots are worth 70K once you’ve made 5 of them. Once you’re on rack \#2, you have a minimum of 56K and will likely have more than 70K in base bonus by the end of the ball, so a multiplier shot is now worth more than 70K. Strictly speaking, left orbit shots when maxed out are really worth more than 70K, since you’ll either get a top lane as well, worth either 1K or 25K, or the ball will occasionally fall back over the orbit rollover for a second score of 70k.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Key feed is actually out of the bumpers, which drains more often than you’d like. The rebounds from the drop targets can, of course, but no two shots at them will be the same, so you’ll have to observe and react to each. Also watch out for the rebound from the 8 target, which can drain down the middle.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">When the ball doesn’t go all the way up the left orbit or is otherwise rolling down above the upper flipper, you’ll need to gauge if it’s better to flip at the 1-7 drops or let the ball roll down the front of the left slingshot, hopefully without triggering it, and then either trap it on the left or dead bounce it to the right flipper. I’ve found that shooting the drops with the lower flipper is usually safer than using the upper one, so I lean towards letting it roll. If all I need is the 8-ball, though, I’m inclined to use the upper flipper to shoot it, since that flipper has a better angle at the 8-ball and requires less force to get the ball there.</p></div></section></div>
