<div class="max-w-3xl mx-auto p-8 print:p-4"><h1 class="text-2xl font-bold text-dark-green mb-4">Klondike EM - Williams | 1971</h1><div class="flex gap-6 mb-6"><div class="flex-shrink-0"><img src="https://img.opdb.org/77a116ac-dd45-49b4-bb2d-f70b286e4ca0-medium.jpg" alt="Klondike backglass" class="w-80 h-auto"/></div><div class="flex-1"><p class="mb-1"><strong>Players:</strong> 1</p><p class="mb-1"><strong>Display:</strong> reels</p><div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Quickie Version:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Line up matching symbols on the reels, then shoot the center collect saucer.</p></div></div></div><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Go-to Flipper:</h2><p>Balanced</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Playfield Risk:</h2><p class="whitespace-pre-line">Mostly feeds out of the bumpers. The side lane feeds and center saucer kickout are usually safe. Rebounds from the two reel advance stand-up targets can drain, of course, depending on how you hit them, i.e. on the edge (outlane risk) vs. straight-on (center risk).</p></section><section class="mb-4"><h2 class="text-lg font-bold text-dark-green mb-1">Full Rules:</h2><div class="space-y-3"><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">This game and its sibling, the 4-player Gold Rush, are very similar to Yukon. The difference is that these have a center saucer, a bumper blocking easy access to the top reels from the flippers, no return lanes, and a center post. Yukon has return lanes, no center saucer, no center bumper and no center post. Klondike has posts behind the flippers that prevent the ball from draining between the flippers and the slingshots as can happen on Doodle Bug. Gold Rush lacks those posts.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The scoring is somewhat obscure since it’s based on how well your slot machine reels at the top match up. With mixed reel symbols, e.g. three different or two of one and one of another, collecting the reel value scores 500. When there are three of the same symbol showing, the reel value goes up. A “wagon” serves as a wild card. Three hats or two hats plus a wagon is worth 3000. Three pans or two pans plus a wagon is worth 4000. Three lanterns or two lanterns plus a wagon is worth 5000 plus an extra ball if enabled. Three wagons is worth 5000 (and a special).</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">There are seven places to score the reel value: both top lanes, both top saucers, both outlanes and the center saucer. The top two saucers also each light a pair of bumpers when collected.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Hitting any reel advances it, with the reel moving top-forwards towards the player. Reels are also advanced by the top rollover buttons and mid-playfield stand-up targets (left and right) and by the two side lanes (center reel). The side lanes also raise your center post; a rollover button at the bottom lowers the post.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The current reel value is preserved when a new game is started. If you begin a game with it lit for more than 500 points, try to soft plunge for the score reel lane at the top right without hitting the advance reel rollover above it.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">The center post is raised by going through either of the two side lanes that advance the center reel. A small rollover button a few inches above the post lowers it. As with any center post, if it’s up, don’t flip; use it to get control of the ball.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Strategy is somewhat UTAD unless the reels are lined up for increased value. If they’re at high value, try to keep collecting the reel value at the center saucer without changing them. If you see a good lineup available if you change a reel, try to advance the appropriate reel.</p><p class="whitespace-pre-line capitalize-first-letter">Both Gold Rush and Klondike suffer from “block-itis” by which I mean many of the things you might want to hit cannot be shot directly from the flippers. You can’t hit either upper saucer to light the bumpers other than through a bank shot or help from the bumpers. You can’t hit the advance reel targets at the top without bumper help even though these are safer than hitting the targets lower down. And one last indignity, that collect reel value saucer in the center, the only one you can shoot directly? The saucer is shallow enough that shots at it often skip right through it.</p></div></section></div>
